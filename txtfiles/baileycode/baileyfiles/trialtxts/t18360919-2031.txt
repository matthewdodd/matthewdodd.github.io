



2031. 



DAVID WALKER
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
19th of August, 1 handkerchief, value 3s. 6d., the good of 



William Bloxam, from his person.






WILLIAM BLOXAM
. I live in George-street, Hanover-square. On the 19th of August, about half-past eight o'clock in the evening, I was at Holborn-bridge—I perceived somebody draw my handkerchief from my coat pocket—I turned round and seized the prisoner, who said, "I have not got it, sir"—I had not spoken to him—I found my handkerchief in his right hand trowsers pocket—he then said he did not take it.





THOMAS BROWNJOHN
. I am an officer. I received the handkerchief from Bloxam.

Prisoners's Defence. I picked it up at the corner of Swan-yard.

Guilty

(The indictment also charged the prisoner with having been previously convicted of felony.)





JOHN SLATE
. I am an officer of St. Andrew, Holborn. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction—(read)—I am certain he is the person.




GUILTY
.—


Transported for Fourteen Years.
