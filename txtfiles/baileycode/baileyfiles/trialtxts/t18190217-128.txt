





441. 
               
                  
                     STEPHEN 
                     HICKLING
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 10th of February
, one looking-glass, value 15 s.; two pillows, value 4 s.; one bolster, value 4 s.; two blankets, value 4 s.; one quilt, value 2 s., and one picture, value 5 s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Stephen 
                           Sanderson
                        




                     
                  , in a lodging-room
            .


                  JANE 
                  SANDERSON
               



            . I am the wife of 
               
                  Stephen 
                  Sanderson
               



            , who lives in Butler-street, Spitalfields


; I let the prisoner the front-parlour, furnished, at 5 s. per week; he came on the 8th of February, on the 10th I found he was gone. I forced the door open, and missed the articles stated in the indictment.


                  JOHN 
                  BUGG
               



            . I am servant to Mr. Salmon, who is a pawnbroker, and lives in Brick-lane. On the 10th ofFebruary, about eight o'clock in the morning, the prisoner pledged a looking-glass with me.


                  THOMAS 
                  CRANFIELD
               



            . I am a pawnbroker; the prisoner pledged a quilt with me.
(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 24.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
First Middlesex Jury, before Mr. Recorder.
