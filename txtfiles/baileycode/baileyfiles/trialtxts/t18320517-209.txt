





1335. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     WARE
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 24th of March
, 1 saw, value 1s. 6d., the goods of 
                     
                        Thomas 
                        Capps
                     




                  ; and that he had been before convicted of felony
            .


                  THOMAS 
                  CAPPS
               



            . I am a pawnbroker
, and live in Old-street-road


. On the 24th of March I missed a small brass-backed saw from a stall in front of my house - I had seen it safe half an hour before; it has not been found since - we had a young man to mind the property, but he turned his back for a moment.


                  JOSEPH 
                  ROBINS
               



            . I live opposite the prosecutor. I was at my door a little after nine o'clock in the morning; I saw a person, who has been convicted, take the saw from the board - he gave it to the prisoner, who gave it to another person.
WILLIAM ROBINS. I saw Porfleet, who has been convicted, take the saw and give it to the prisoner, and they all moved off together.


                  ROBERT 
                  LOVELOCK
               



            . I am an officer. I took the prisoner in Wilderness-row - he denied all knowledge of it.
CHARLES WALLER. I am a Police-constable. I have a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got at Mr. Clark's office - I was present, and know he is the person - (read.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 17. - 


               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
