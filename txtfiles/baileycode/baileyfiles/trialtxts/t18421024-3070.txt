




3070. 




WILLIAM HAYES
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
8th of October, 1 pair of boots, value 3s.; the goods of 



Henry Tatnell, from the person of 



John Tatnell
:, and 



MARY ANN MUNDAY, 

for receiving the same, well knowing the same to have been stolen; against the Statute, &c; to which


HAYES* pleaded 


GUILTY
. Aged 10.—


Transported for Ten Years.
Convict Ship.





JOHN TATNELL
. I am the son of Henry Tatnell. On the 8th of October Hayes came and told me something—he took off my boots and ran away with them.





JOHN POULFORD
. I am a pawnbroker, and live in Great Suffolk-street, Southwark—I produce the boots, which were pawned at our house by Munday, on the 8th of October, in the name of Ann Thompson, John-street.





WILLIAM SMITH
 (police-constable M 170.) I received charge of Munday—I asked how she came in possession of this duplicate, which I had received from two young men, in a pawnbroker's shop—she said she picked up the ticket outside the street door, and she had given it the young men, she had not pawned the boots.

Munday. I pawned one pair of boots; Hayes said they were his own.

MUNDAY—


GUILTY
. Aged 21.—



Confined Six Months.
