                     John 
                     Simpson 
                  



                
             and 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Mallet 
                  



                
             were indicted for 


               stealing a hat, value 3 s.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     George 
                     Tone 
                  




                
            , October 29
. ++


                  George 
                  Tone 
               



            . I am a butcher
, and keep a shop in the Fleet-market


: I was not at the shop when the hat was taken away.


                  Thomas 
                  Mickle 
               



            . I am servant to the prosecutor: the two prisoners came into the shop as I was shutting it up, on the 28th of October, about twelve at night; Mallet took up a loin of mutton, and said, Weigh this: I put it in the scale: in the mean time Simpson took a hat from off-the hook, and went round me, and as he was going out at the door, I stopped him with it in his hand: Mallet got out at the door, and took it out of Simpson's hand, and away he ran, and I after him: a watchman stopped him, and another pursued Simpson, and took him: the hat was found by a watchman, flung behind the shop.
Prosecutor. I was sent for, and when Mallet was taken, he behaved very impudently, and threatened to destroy me if ever he got his liberty.
The prisoners, in their defence, said they went in to buy some meat, but denied the fact laid to their charge.
Both 

               Guilty
            .
