



1883. 



JOHN HALL
 was indicted

 for that he being a person employed in the General Post-office, did embezzle and steal a letter, containing a pair of gloves, the goods of 

Thomas William, Earl of Lichfield, her Majesty's Post Master General.—4 other COUNTS, varying the manner of laying the charge.—6th COUNT, for stealing a letter out of the Post Office.—To the 6th Count, of which the prisoner pleaded





GUILTY
. Aged.—


Transported for Seven Years. (By request of Mr. Attorney General a noli prosequi was entered on the other counts.)

Before Mr. Justice Williams.
