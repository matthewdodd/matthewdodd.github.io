






161. 
               
                  
                     CHARLES 
                     VAUGHAN
                  



               
             and 
               
                  
                     CHARLES 
                     SIBERY
                  



               
             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 26th of December
 last, one linen handkerchief, value 6 d.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     Samuel 
                     Venner
                  




               
            .


                  SAMUEL
                   VENNER
               



             sworn.
I am a journeyman cabinet-maker
; at the corner of Fenchurch-street


, the two men that are here told me my pocket was picked of my handkerchief; I saw my handkerchief, and knew it to be mine: the prisoners were immediately secured.


                  JAMES
                   NEWMAN
               



             sworn.
I am patrol; on the 26th of December, I saw Sibery draw the handkerchief half out of Verner's pocket; he then called the other, and he pulled it quite out at the corner of Fenchurch-street.


                  THOMAS
                   CRAWLEY
               



             sworn.
I saw the gentleman lose his handkerchief in the manner the last witness described.
(The handkerchief produced and deposed to.)


                  CHARLES
                   VAUGHAN
               



            , 

               GUILTY
            .





               Sentence respited
            .


                  CHARLES
                   SIBERY
               



            , 

               GUILTY
            .




               Transported for seven years
            .
Tried by the London Jury before the Lord CHIEF BARON.
