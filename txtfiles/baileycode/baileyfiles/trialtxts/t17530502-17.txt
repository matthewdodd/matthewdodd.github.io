





198. (M.) 
               
                  
                     Mary 
                     Horne 
                  



               
            , was indicted for 


               stealing three linnen shirts, value 3 s. one muslin neck-cloath, value 2 d. one flannel waistcoat
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Tomline 
                  




                
            , March 3
. ++
The prisoner was employ'd in the house of the prosecutor at Twickenham


, as a chair-woman
; the goods being missing, a search warrant was taken out; the prisoner's room was searched, and a pair of ruffles were found, the property of the prosecutor; and the goods mentioned in the indictment, at three pawnbrokers; the shift belonging to the ruffles, was found at 
               
                  Richard 
                  Armstrong 
               



            's, two shirts, two shifts, and one muslin neckcloth, was found at Edward Wade's, one shirt, and the flannel waistcoat to Toothacre's, by the direction of the prisoner, who confessed the taking and pawning them. The prisoner had nothing to say for herself.



               Guilty
            .
