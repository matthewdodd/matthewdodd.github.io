







                     James 
                     Ferguson 
                  



               
            , was indicted for 


               stealing a Cherry Derry Gown, val. 20 s. a Lustring Gown val. 20 s. and a Petticoat val. 10 s. the Goods of 
                     
                        
                           Tho.
                            Poulter
                        




                     
                  , in the House of 
                     
                        Elizabeth 
                        Jones 
                     



.
It appeared that the Goods were put in a Bundle, and left in the Bar at the Nag's-Head Alehouse in Aldersgate-Street


, in order to be sent by the Highgate Coach. The Prisoner took them from thence, but not so privately as to escape unobserved. He was immediately pursued, and was taken in Noble-Street. 


               Guilty 39 s.
            



               Transportation
            .
