





1384. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     MORRIS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 27th of June
, two firkins, value 2s., and 10 gallons of lamp-black, value 14s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  William 
                  Francis
               




            .
HENRY COLLINGTON. I am shopman to Mr. 
               
                  William 
                  Francis
               



            , who is an oilman
 and lives, in Long-acre, and has a warehouse in Mercer-street


, the prisoner was his porter
. The keys of the warehouse hung in the counting-house. I missed them about eight o'clock in the morning of the 27th of June, went to the warehouse, and saw the prisoner come down with two firkins of lamp-black under his arm. I watched him into Short's-gardens - He took them into a house belonging to one Hambleton. I met two officers, who went with me, and took him and the lampblack.
RICHARD ROW WICKS. I am an officer. I took the prisoner and lamp-black at an old iron-shop in Short's-gardens; he said he took it from his master's warehouse.
(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. My mistress told me to take it to Soho-square. I left it in Short's-gardens while I got my breakfast.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 19.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
Fourth Middlesex Jury, before Mr. Recorder.
