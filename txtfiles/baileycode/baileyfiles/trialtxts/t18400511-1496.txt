



1496. 




JAMES HOGAN
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
10th of May, 1 handkerchief, value 4s., the goods of John M'Hele, from his person.







JOHN M'LLELE
. I am footman to a lady in Wimpole-street, I was going along Lisson-grove on the 10th of May—I stopped to bear a man delivering lectures on temperance—I felt something tug at my pocket—I turned and collared the prisoner with my handkerchief in his hand—this is it—(examining one)—it had been in my pocket.





SAMUEL BARNARD
. I was in Lisson-grove, listening to the lecture—I felt my pocket tugged—I looked round and saw the prisoner standing behind me—in two or three minutes I felt my elbow touched, and I saw the prisoner with a handkerchief in his hand, winding it up—the prosecutor turned and claimed it.

Prisoner. It was thrown down at my feet by a man, who took it.





THOMAS HENRY THOMPSON
 (police-sergeant D 4.) I was passing, and saw the handkerchief in the prisoner's hand—he dropped it.




GUILTY
.* Aged 17.—


Transported for Ten Years.
