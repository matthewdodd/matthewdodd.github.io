



1788. 




ALEXANDER M'DERMOTT
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
18th of July, 1 handkerchief, value 3s., the goods of a certain man whose name is unknown, from his person.






CHABLES THORPE
. I am a patrol of St. Bride's. On the 18th of July, at twenty minutes past nine o'clock, I was in Fleet-street. I saw the prisoner take this handkerchief from a gentleman's pocket—I went to take him—he ran up Salisbury-court-Welsh overtook him, and I saw him throw this handkerchief out of his jacket-pocket-when I got back the gentleman was gone.

Prisoner. I was going along the middle of the road, and he came and hit me with a stick—I never saw the handkerchief at all.





JOSEPH WELSH
. I am a watchman of St. Bride's. I saw the prisoner take this handkerchief from a gentleman's pocket, and put it into his own—he crossed over, and ran away—I pursued him with Thorpe-just at I. got to the prisoner he threw this down, and I took him—I do not know who the gentleman was.




GUILTY
. † Aged 16.—


Transported for Ten Years.
