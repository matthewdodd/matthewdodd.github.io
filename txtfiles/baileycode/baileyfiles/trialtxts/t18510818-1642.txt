









1642. 




JOHN TANKINS,



 WILLIAM TANKINS
, 




SAMUEL MORRIS.





ALFRED LAWRENCE
, 




WILLIAM CARROLL
, and 




JAMES PEARCE



. stealing 120 yards of cotton print, value 3l.; the goods of 



Michael Smith.—2nd COUNT, receiving the same.


JOHN TANKINS pleaded 


GUILTY** to 2nd Count.
 Aged 22.—


Transported for Seven Years.

WILLIAM TANKINS pleaded 


GUILTY* to 2nd Count.
 Aged 16.—



Confined Six Months.

SAMUEL MORRIS pleaded 


GUILTY* to 1st Count.
 Aged 17.—



Confined Six Months.

WILLIAM CARROLL pleaded 


GUILTY
to 2nd Count
 Aged 16.—



Confined One Month,





and Whipped.

JAMES PEARCE pleaded 



GUILTY
to 1st Count.
 Aged 16.—



Confined One Month, and 



Whipped.



No evidence was offered against
LAWRENCE.




NOT GUILTY
.
