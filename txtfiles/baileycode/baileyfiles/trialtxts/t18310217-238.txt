





664. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     PATTISON
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 3rd of February
, 1 watch, value 31s.; 2 seals, value 30s., and 2 keys, value 1s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Ann 
                           Stubbs
                        




                     
                  , his mistress
            .


                  ANN 
                  STUBBS
               



            . I live in Peerless-row, City-road


. The prisoner was my errand-boy
 for two days - he went out with me about ten o'clock in the morning; I sent him back for something, and when I returned, the watch which I had left hanging up, was gone, with the seals and keys, from the back parlour.


                  EDITH 
                  WARWICK
               



            . I am servant to Mrs. Stubbs. I let the prisoner in when he returned, but did not see him take the watch - he went into the back parlour to warm his hands.


                  JOSEPH 
                  WILLIAM CLARK
               



            . I am servant to Mr. Fleming, a pawnbroker, of Farringdon-street. On the 3rd of February this watch and seals were pawned, but I do not know who by.


                  JOHN 
                  BROWN
               



            . I took the prisoner on Friday evening, two days after the robbery, and found the duplicate on him.
Prisoner's Defence. I never had the watch.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 17. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
There was another indictment against the prisoner.
