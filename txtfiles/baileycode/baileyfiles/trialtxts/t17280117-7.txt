







                     John 
                     Hews 
                  



               
            , of St. Mary's Whitechapel


, was indicted for 


               privately stealing a Firkin of Soap, value 25 s.
             on the 4th of this Instant, the Property of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Allen 
                  




                
            .


                  Robert 
                  Johnson 
               



             depos'd, That he saw the Prisoner go into the Prosecutor's Shop and take the Firkin of Soap, which he deliver'd to a Comrade at the Door; and this Deponent and others suspecting them to be Thieves, stopped the Prisoner and carried him before a Magistrate, where Mr. Allen proved the Firkin to be his by the Mark: The Prisoner making but a weak and frivolous Defence, and the Fact appearing plain, the Jury found him 


               guilty to the value of 4 s. and 10 d.
