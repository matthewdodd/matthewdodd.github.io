






1409. 
               
                  TIMOTHY 
                  KAYLOR
               




             and 
               
                  BENJAMIN 
                  BROMMAGE
               




             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 24th of July
, 1 handkerchief, value 6d., the goods of 
                     
                        Saint John 
                        Mason
                     




                  , from his person
            .
2nd COUNT. For stealing from a person unknown.


                  GEORGE 
                  ANTHONY
               



            . I live in Park-street, Camden-town. On the 24th of July, a few minutes past ten o'clock at night, I was at the top of Tottenham-court-road


 - I saw the two prisoners and another attempting to pick pockets - I then saw Kaylor go and pick Mr. Mason's pocket of his handkerchief - I had watched them for half an hour - I called the policeman, and went and told the gentleman he had been robbed - I then crossed and took the prisoners - this handkerchief was found on Kaylor.


                  JOHN 
                  WILLIAMS
               



             (police-constable E 99). I had been watching the prisoners for some time - Anthony spoke to me - I took Kaylor, and found this handkerchief in his right hand trowsers pocket.
(
               
                  Henry 
                  Tapp
               



            , No. 2, Cromer-place; and 
               
                  Celia 
                  Barton
               



            , of No. 9, Spa-place, gave Brommage a good character.)
KAYLOR - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 14.
BROMMAGE - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 17.





               Transported for Seven Years
            .
