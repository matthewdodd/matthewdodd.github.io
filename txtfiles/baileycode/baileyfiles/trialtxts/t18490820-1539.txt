



1539. 




HENRY MILLS



, stealing 1 handkerchief, value 2s. 6d.; the goods of 



James Saick, from his person: having been twice before convicted.






JOHN WREN
. I am a shoemaker, of Church-passage, Basinghall-street. On 1st Aug. I was in my shop and saw the prisoner take a handkerchief from the prosecutor's pocket, and put it under his coat—I told him, he called" Stop thief! "and ran after him.





JAMES SAICK
. I missed my handkerchief, and went after the prisoner—a

gentleman stopped him—my handkerchief was picked up about forty yards off—he had passed there.

Prisoner's Defence. I heard a cry of "Stop thief!" a man passed me, and I ran.





JAMES HITCHCOCK
. I produce a certificate—(read—


Thomas Lavender, convticted Nov., 1846, confined one year)—the prisoner is the man.

Prisoner's Defence. It was not me, but my brother.





SAMUEL TAFF
 (policeman). I produce a certificate—(read—


Charles Weston, convicted Aug., 1848, confined six months)—the prisoner is the man.




GUILTY
. Aged 21.—


Transported for Seven Years.
