



1945. 




GEORGE SMITH
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
27th of May, 1 pair of trowsers, value 10s., the goods of 



William Bayfield; and that he bad been before convicted of felony.






BENJAMIN LOCKWOOD
. I am servant to William Bayfield, a pawnbroker, in the New Cut, Lambeth. I had a pair of trowsers—I saw them on the 27th of May, and missed them about four o'clock.

Prisoner. I saw them lying down, and picked them up. Witness. They were pinned with three pins.





JOHN CALLARD
. I live in the New Cut. I was near the shop on this day, and saw the prisoner drop these trowsers four or five doors from the prosecutor's.





JAMES BROOK
. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction which I got from the Clerk for the Peace for Surrey—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
.* Aged 12.—


Transported for Seven Years—Convict Ship.
