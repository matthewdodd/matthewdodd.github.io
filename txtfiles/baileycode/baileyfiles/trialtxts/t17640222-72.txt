





213. (M.) 
               
                  
                     Isaac 
                     Jones 
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing one callimancoe petticoat, value 30 s. two linen gowns, value 20 s. one sattin hat, value 4 s. nine pair of linen sleeves, value 7 s. four shifts, value 10 s. four aprons value 10 s. nine linen caps, value 6 s. six handkerchiefs, two pillow-biers, one sheet, one cloth cloak, and four napkins, the property of 
                     
                        Ann 
                        Green 
                     




                   , spinster
, in the dwelling-house of 
                     
                        Richard 
                        Robinson 
                     



, February 11
.
The prosecutrix went to live servant
 with a nobleman, and left the things mentioned in a box, at the house of 
               
                  Richard 
                  Robinson 
               



            , at the Three Tulips, in Orchard-street, Westminster


. The prisoner was a soldier
, and quartered in the said house, and lay in the same room where the box stood. She went for her box on the 11th of February, found it broke open, and the things missing. The prisoner was charged with taking them; he owned he had taken them, and sold them in Rag-fair.




               Guilty of stealing, but not in the dwelling-house
            .
