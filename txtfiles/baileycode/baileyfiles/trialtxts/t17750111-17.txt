





117. (2d M.) 
               
                  
                     JOSEPH
                      GARRETT
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing a gun, mounted with steel, value five pounds, the property of 
                     
                        Thomas
                         Hancock
                     



                  , in the dwelling house of the said Thomas
            , December 26
. +


                     Thomas
                      Hancock
                  




               
            . I keep a public house
 at Chelsea


. I lost a gun on the 26th of December; it was taken from behind my bar door: sometime after 
               
                  William
                   Lee
               



             informed me he had seen my gun at Mr. Hanney's at Westminster. I went with a search warrant to Mr. Hanney's and there I saw my gun.


                  Richard
                   Steel
               



            , and 
               
                  William
                   Lee
               



            , deposed, that they saw the gun at 
               
                  Peter
                   Hanney
               



            's, and knowing it to be the property of the prosecutor, they gave him information of it.


                  William
                   Gardiner
               



            . The prisoner came to Mr. Hanney's and called for some liquor; he had not money to pay his reckoning, he told Mr. Hanney that he had a gun lay for a little money, at the ship in Little Sanctuary. Mr. Hanney gave 
               
                  Garrett
               


             the money and went with him to the ship; he paid five shillings and eleven-pence halfpenny, they gave him the gun, and he delivered it to Mr. Hanney, he promised to pay the money for his reckoning the next day and redeem the gun.


                  Peter
                   Hanney
               



             confirmed the evidence of the last witness. (The gun was produced in court and deposed to by the prosecutor.)
The prisoner in his defence denied the charge.




               Guilty of stealing to the value of thirty-nine shillings
            



               T
            .
