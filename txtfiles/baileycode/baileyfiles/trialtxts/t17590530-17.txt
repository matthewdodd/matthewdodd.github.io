                  Charles 
                  Dighton 
               



            . I live in Clerkenwell


: I was asleep in bed, and my breches, and a guinea and half in them, under my head: when I awoke, my money was gone, but the purse left behind: the prisoner lodging in the same house, I charg'd her with taking it; she at first deny'd it; but at last own'd it, and produc'd it.
