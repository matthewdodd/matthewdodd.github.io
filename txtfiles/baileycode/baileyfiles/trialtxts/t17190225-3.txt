







                     James 
                     Spurgin 
                  



               
            , was indicted for Felony and Burglary in 


               breaking open the House of 
                     
                        
                           Mary 
                           Fletcher 
                        




                      
                   , and taking thence a Camblet Coat 2 pair of Plush Breeches 23 Coats and Waistcoats,&c
            . The Prosecutor deposed that between 4 and 5 in the Morning a Man came and knockt at her Door, and ask'd if she had lost any Goods, whereupon she went with him to the Watch-house, (where the prisoner was and had the Coat and a pair of Breeches on him) and own'd her Goods. There being no proof of the Burglary the Jury acquitted him of that, and brought him in 


               Guilty of Felony only
            , 


               Transportation
            .
