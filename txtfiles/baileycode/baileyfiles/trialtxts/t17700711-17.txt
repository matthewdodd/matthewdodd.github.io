





444. (M.) 
               
                  
                     William
                      Iveson
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing eight silver tea-spoons, value 14 s. and a shagreen spoon-case, value 6 d.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     John
                      Aberdeen
                  




               
            , May 26
. *


                  John
                   Aberdeen
               



            , the prosecutor, deposed that he lost the spoons out of a corner-cupboard in his parlour.


                  John
                   Frier
               



            , the pawnbroker, deposed that the prisoner pawned the spoons to him for 14 s. in the name of 
               
                  William
                   Williams
               



            ; and that he gave him a duplicate of the ticket.


                  James
                   Thompson
               



            , the constable, deposed that the prisoner was brought to the watch-house for abusing a publican in the neighbourhood; some suspicious circumstances arising, he searched him, and found the duplicate of the spoons upon him.
The prisoner said in his defence, that he found the pocket-book in which the duplicate was.



               Guilty
            , 


               T
            .
