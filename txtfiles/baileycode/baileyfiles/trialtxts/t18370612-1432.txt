



1432. 




JOHN SAUNDERS
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
23rd of May, 1 handkerchief, value 1s. 6d. the goods of 



Charles Alexander M' Intosh, from his person; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






CHARLES ALEXANDER M'INTOSH
. I live in Great New-street, Gough-square, On the evening of the 23rd of May, I was in Fleet-street—I felt a tug, turned, and saw my handkerchief in the prisoner's hand—I could not catch hold of him, but I did not lose flight of him till he was secured.





ALEXANDER M'INTOSH
. I was with the prosecutps, and saw the handkerchief in the prisoner's hand—I saw it taken from him—he ran away—Ipursued and saw him taken.





GEORGE BUCKLEY
. I am a patrol of St. Bride's—the prisoner was running owards me and I caught him.
(Property produced and sworn to.)





JAMES ANDREWS
 (police-constable M 45.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction from Mr. Clark's office—office(read)—he is the man that was tried.




GUILTY
. Aged 24.—


Transported for Seven Years.
