





29 
               
                  
                     Joseph 
                     Glassard 
                  



               
            , was indicted for 


               stealing on the 5th of November
, 1 plow-chain, value 1 s. 2 iron collar-links, value 18 d. 1 iron plow-pin, value 2 s. and 6 d.
             the goods of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Atkins 
                  




                
            .


                  Q.
                   John Atkins
               



            , Do you know the Prisoner?
Atkins. No, Sir, but my father's plow was robb'd, but I cannot tell when it was.
Q. to 
               
                  John 
                  Pearse 
               



            . Do you know the Prisoner?
Pearse. I believe so, one of my master's plow-irons I can swear to.
Q. When did he lose them?
Pearse. The fifth of November at night; there were two jumping collar'd-links, and a twisting nail gone.
Q. What reason have you to charge the Prisoner with these things?
Pearse. A gentleman took them a top of him.


                  William 
                  Dimmock 
               



            . We caught the Prisoner in the field with these things upon his back, in a field in Tottenham parish; we stopt him first upon a suspicion.
Q. And do you know them to be Mr. Atkins's ?

Dimmock. No, I don't know they are, but Mr. Atkins's people came afterwards and owned them.


                  Thomas 
                  Bernfather 
               



            . I am a butcher, and the Prisoner has come oftentimes to my house, and by what I have observed of the man, he does not appear to be right in his senses.
The Prisoner in his defence said, that a person gave him a shilling to bring it to town.




               Guilty 10 d.
