





685. (M.) 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM
                      JOHNSON
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               ripping and stealing seventy-three lb. of lead, the said lead being affixed to a certain empty house, the property of 
                     
                        
                           John
                            Read
                        




, September 5
. *


                  John
                   Read
               



            , who keeps the Kings Arms in High-Holborn
, deposed that he lost some lead from his house, which was repairing.
- Murray deposed that he stopped the prisoner in High-Holborn, about three o'clock in the morning of the 5th of September with seventy three lb. of lead on his back; that he gave an indifferent account how he came by it, upon which he secured him.


                  Richard
                   Holmes
               



             confirmed this evidence.


                  James
                   Mansfield
               



            , who was employed in repairing the Prosecutor's house, deposed that the lead fitted the place on the prosecutor's house from whence the lead had been stolen.
The Prisoner, in his defence, called one witness, who gave him a good character.



               Guilty
            .
