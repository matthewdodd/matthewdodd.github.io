



1244. 




THOMAS WAKEFIELD
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
4th of April, 1 pair of boots, value 2s. 6d., the goods of 



John Matthews and another; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






WILLIAM GOFTON
. I am shopman to John Matthews and another, who are pawnbrokers. On the 4th of March I saw the prisoner take a pair of boots from inside the shop—he ran down a court—I pursued, calling "Stop thief," and the policeman took him—I did not find the boots on him—these are them—(examining them.)





JOHN RUSSELL
 (police-constable G 165.) I saw the prisoner drop the boots at the corner of White Bear-yard—I took him.





JOHN BULLOCK
 (police-constable G 116.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clark's office—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 18.—


Transported for Seven Years.
