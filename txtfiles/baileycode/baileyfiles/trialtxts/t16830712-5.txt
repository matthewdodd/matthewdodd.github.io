








                  William 
                  Knowles
               



             was Tryed, for that he the 20th of June




               broke open the house of 
                     
                        Phillip 
                        Stubb
                     




                   in Cornhill


, and took thence in Plate and Money, to the value of 50 pounds
            , which Robbery he before the Justice upon his being Apprehended confessed to have thus effected, viz. That going up and finding the Key in the Chamber-door, he opened it, and broke open the Boxes with an Iron Skewer, and took out the Plate, Money, &c. some of which Plate upon proffer to Sale was stopped, and he Apprehended. This and other Evidence given against him he was found 

               Guilty
             of the Felony.
