





149 (L) 
               
                  
                     Dorothy 
                     Peak 
                  



               
            , widow
, was indicted for 


               stealing three linnen shirts, value fifteen shillings, and four handkerchiefs
            , the property of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Lewis 
                  




                
            , Feb. 12
. ++


                  John 
                  Lewis 
               



            . The prisoner at the bar was my servant
, she got up in the morning to go to washing, and the things were given her to wash; they were missing, I went and found them at the pawnbroker's by the prisoner's direction.
Q. What is his name?
Lewis. His name is Spencer. She confessed she took and pawned them.


                  Mary 
                  Groveby 
               



            . I saw the shirts and handkerchiefs delivered to the prisoner to wash. She was the prosecutor's servant.


                  Jane 
                  Spencer 
               



            . I am daughter to Mr. Spencer.
Q. What is he?
J. Spencer. He is a pawnbroker. The prisoner at the bar brought two shirts and three handkerchiefs, and pledged with me. (Produced in Court, and deposed to.)


                  Mary 
                  Hobart 
               



            . The prisoner pledged a shirt with me. ( Produced, and deposed to.)
Prisoner's Defence.
I know nothing of the things.



               Guilty
            .
