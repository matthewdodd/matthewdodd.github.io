





200. (M.) 
               
                  
                     Solomon 
                     Peters 
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing one bill of exchange; signed 
                     
                        Anthony 
                        Selvin 
                     



                  ; value 3 l.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     Ann 
                     Selby 
                  




                
            ; March 17, 1758
. ++


                  Ann 
                  Selby 
               



            . I had a bill of three pounds, drawn upon Mr 
               
                  Robert 
                  Cliff 
               



            , Banker, in Lombard-street; which I receiv'd from Mr 
               
                  Anthony 
                  Selvin 
               



             of Durham; he having appointed to lend me 50 l. he order'd me to return this in a post letter, and he would send me 50 l. I put it in a letter before the bill was due, and deliver'd it to the prisoner, to put it in the Post; after that I had word came, it was not receiv'd; I ask'd the prisoner about it; he own'd he had broke open the letter, and taken it out; and had receiv'd the money for it.
James Quin. (be produc'd the draught) This I receiv'd of the prisoner at the bar, above a year ago; he ow'd me half a guinea, and I gave him the rest of the cash; I paid it to Mrs Macoby, and she gave me 3 l. for it.


                  Ann 
                  Macoby 
               



            . The draught that Mr Quin deliver'd to me, I kept it 'till due; then I sent my man ( name, 
               
                  John 
                  Smith 
               



            ) with it for the cash.


                  John 
                  Smith 
               



            . I carry'd it to Mr Cliff's, and receiv'd the money for it; here is my name on the back of it.


                  Thomas 
                  Bourne 
               



            . I am clerk to Mr Cliff, and Co. I remember particularly well, Mrs Selby bringing this draught to our house, before it was due; and when Mr Smith brought it afterwards; I paid the money and took it.
The draught read.
Sir, Pay to Mrs 
               
                  Ann 
                  Selby 
               



            , or order; the sum of 3 l. thirty days after date; and place it to account of your humble servant,


                  Anthony 
                  Selvin 
               



            .
To Mr 
               
                  Robert 
                  Cliff 
               



            ; Lombard-street.
Durham, February 12, 58.
The prisoner in his defence, said, he was near upon marrying to the prosecutrix then; but it is since broke off; and that she gave him liberty to take the draught and make use of it.



               Guilty
            .*

                  Charles 
                  Asgill
