               stealing a pair of Silver Clasps, value 5 s. and twenty six Ounces of Silver, value 5 l. the Goods of 
                     
                        
                           John 
                           Slaughter 
                        



                      
                  , and a pair of Silver Clasps, value 4 s. the Goods of 
                     
                        
                           Jeremy 
                           Lee 
                        



                      
                  , in the House of John Slaughter
            , March 18
.
The Witnesses deposed that the Prisoner was Apprentice
 to Mr. Slaughter, a Silver Smith

, and at several times had sold thirty Ounces of Silver Cuttings, &c. but being examined after some time confest the whole.
In his Defence, he said, he sold the Silver by his Master's Order, but his Master denying he ever gave him such Order, the Jury found him 


               guilty to the value of 39 s.
