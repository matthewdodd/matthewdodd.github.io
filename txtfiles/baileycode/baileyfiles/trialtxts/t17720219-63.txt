                     DAVID
                      HIND
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing twenty pounds of tobacco, value 4 s.
             the property of persons unknown. Feb. 22
. ++
"
               
                  George
                   Whitehall
               



            , a merchant's watchman at the Keys, deposed, that on the 22d of February, about four o'clock in the morning, the prisoner came a-board a lighter of tobacco that he had the charge of; and that he found 20 lb. of tobacco lying by a hogshead which had been broke open; that he found an empty bag in the boat; and that he secured the prisoner, who was endeavouring to hide himself in the lighter; he said that the prisoner b egged for forgiveness."
"The prisoner in his defence said, that he had no lodging nor money, and so went to lie in the lighter."



               Guilty
            .
