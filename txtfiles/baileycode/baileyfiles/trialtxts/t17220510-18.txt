







                     John 
                     Jackson 
                  



               
            , of Aldgate


, was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing a Coat value 40 s. a Wastcoat, 20 s. and other Things
            , the Goods of 
               
                  
                     Joseph 
                     Bird 
                  




                
            . It appear'd that Joseph Bird belong'd to Mr. Ruffels Harwich Hoy, which lay at Duval's Wharf


, opposite to Bar street; and being order'd ashore, he left the prisoner and two Boys aboard. The prisoner sent the Boys ashore, and went off with the Goods. He at first pawn'd em to.
Mitchell in George Yard, White Chappel, and afterwards sold'em for 35 s. to Bulman, a Slop Seller. His Confession was read in Court. The Jury found him 


               guilty
             of Felony.
