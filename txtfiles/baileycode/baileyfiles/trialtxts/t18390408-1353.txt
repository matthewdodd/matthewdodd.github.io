



1353. 




LOUISA CONNELL
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
6th of April, 1 umbrella, value 2s. 6d., the goods of 



John Edward Wilde; and that she had been before convicted of felony.






JOHN EDWARD WILDE
. I keep an umbrella shop in Bowling-buildings, Stingo-lane. On the 6th of April, the prisoner sold me an old socket lamp—after she left, we suspected her, and went after her—I saw her run up the lane—I then went home, and missed an umbrella—I pursued, and apprehended her fn James-street—I am sure she is the person.





GEORGE SMITH
. I am a pawnbroker, and lire in Chapel-street. I produce an umbrella, which was pawned by the prisoner on the 6th of April.





CHARLES CHAMBERLAINE
 (police-constable D 98.) I took the prisoner.





THOMAS LEACH
. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clark's office—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. * Aged 22.—


Transported for Seven Years.
