





Fourth Middlesex Jury. - Before Mr. Common Sergeant.
981. 
               
                  
                     FREDERICK 
                     BROOKS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 7th of April
, 1 blanket, value 9d.; 1 quilt, value 1s.; 2 pillow-cases, value 1s.; and 1 sheet, value 2s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Samuel 
                     Denby
                  




               
            .
SARAH DENBY. I am the wife of Samuel Denby. The prisoner had a furnished room in our house - I missed the articles stated, and had him taken on the 15th of April; I had let these articles to the prisoner.


                  JOSEPH 
                  BEAL
               



            . I am an officer. I was sent for and took the prisoner - I found four duplicates of the articles, which were pawned; they were in a drawer, and he gave them to me.


                  HENRY 
                  ROBERT REDWOOD
               



            . I am in the service of a pawnbroker - I gave these two duplicates to a person who pawned this blanket and pillow; these other two were written by a young man in our service.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. They would have been redeemed on the Saturday following - I had a job, and wanted 5s. worth of varnish.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 27. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
