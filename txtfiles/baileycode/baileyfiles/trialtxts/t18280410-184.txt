





976. 
               
                  ANN 
                  McINTYRE
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 8th of February
, 1 pair of sheets, value 8s.; 1 blanket, value 3s.; 1 bolster, value 3s.; 1 pillow, value 2s.; 1 looking-glass, value 2s., and 1 candlestick, value 1s. 6d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  Samuel 
                  Howard
               




            .
SAMUEL HOWARD. I live in Leg-alley, Long-acre


. On the 3d of February, the prisoner took my second floor front room, at 5s. a week; she left me in ten days without notice, and I missed this property - I found her on the 25th; she said she had pawned my things and intended to redeem them, when she got the money - she told us where they were pawned; we went, and found they had been redeemed - I have not recovered them.


                  WILLIAM 
                  OXLEY
               



            . I am a constable. On the 25th of February, I took the prisoner, and found on her three duplicates, not relating to this property; she told me where she had pawned the things, but they had been taken out.


                  PHILIP 
                  EATON
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, and live in High Holborn. The prisoner pawned a sheet on the 5th of February, it was redeemed on the 15th.


                  CHARLES 
                  WILLIAMS
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. The prisoner pawned a pillow on the 5th of February, and redeemed it by an affidavit, on the 20th.
The prisoner put in a written defence, pleading distress.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 40.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
