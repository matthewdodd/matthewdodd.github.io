





Before Mr. Sergeant Arabin.
2162. 
               
                  
                     ANN 
                     SWEENY
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 30th of August
, 1 tippet, value 4s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Mary Ann 
                     Waters
                  




               
            .


                  MARY 
                  ANN WATERS
               



            . I am single
; I live at No.35, Frith-street


 - the prisoner was servant
 to a lodger in the house; I lost my tippet on the 2nd of August from a back parlour -I saw it again on the prisoner on the 30th of August.


                  ELIZABETH 
                  HOEPFNER
               



            . My husband keeps the house in Frith-street. Waters is my servant
 - the prisoner is servant to Mrs. Rymer, a lodger; this tippet and a gown of my own were taken on the 2nd of August, when the prisoner's mistress took her to Margate; I described my gown to her mistress, and she found it on her person.


                  FANNY 
                  RYMER
               



            . This tippet was in possession of the prisoner on the day we returned from Margate.


                  JAMES 
                  WARD
               



             (Police-constable C 172). I took the prisoner at the prosecutor's house on the 30th of August; this tippet was under her gown and petticoat, tied round her waist. Property produced and sworn to.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 26. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
