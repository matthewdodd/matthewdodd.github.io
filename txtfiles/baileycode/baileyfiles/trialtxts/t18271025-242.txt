





2219. 
               
                  WILLIAM 
                  REYNOLDS
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 21st of October
, two bells, value 16s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  Benjamin 
                  Gould
               




            .
BENJAMIN GOULD. I am a scavenger
. I use these bells when I go out with a cart. I saw them safe in a basket, in my stable, in Worship-street


, on Saturday night, the 20th of October, and lost them next morning.


                  ROBERT 
                  CHRISTIE
               



            . I work for Gould; last Saturday week, between two and three o'clock, I put the bells into a basket, in the stable - the door was not locked, and on Monday they were gone; a man could get in at the wicket, which was not fastened.


                  JOHN 
                  HANDS
               



            . I live with Mr. Gould. I saw the bells put away, and missed them on Saturday morning. I do not know the prisoner.


                  JOSEPH 
                  TAYLOR
               



            . I am an officer. I apprehended the prisoner at seven o'clock on Sunday morning, the 21st of October, in Petticoat-lane, on the City side, endeavouring to sell these bells,(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 36.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
