






182, 183. (L.) 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM
                      OWEN
                  



               
             and 
               
                  
                     JAMES
                      BUGDEN
                  



               
             were indicted; the first for 


               stealing 24 lb. of bohea tea, value 4 l. 4 s.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     James
                      Longden
                  



               
            , Dec. 2d

; the other for 


               receiving the said goods, well knowing them to have been stolen
            . ++


                  James
                   Longden
               



            . I am keeper of the George inn
; I had the custody of the tea and was answerable for it; the prisoner was a servant of mine at the time it was lost.


                  Samuel
                   Spencer
               



            . I directed my servant, 
               
                  John
                   Rhodes
               



            , to carry 24 lb. of bohea tea to the George inn.


                  John
                   Rhodes
               



            . I did carry such a kind of package, and delivered it to Giles, the bookkeeper of the inn.


                  Richard
                   Weston
               



            . The prisoner being in custody owned having taken several parcels at different times, but particularly this 24 lb. he sa id he took it out of the George inn yard.


                  Thomas
                   Compton
               



            . I heard him confess that he took it out of the George yard.
Prisoner's Defence.
I never made any confession.
OWEN, 

               Guilty
            . 


               T
            .
BUGDEN, 

               acquitted
