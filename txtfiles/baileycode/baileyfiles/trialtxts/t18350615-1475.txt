



1475. 




WILLIAM BUCKINGHAM
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
18th of May, 1 cushion, value 10s. the goods of 



Joseph Coatsworth.






DAVID COOPER
 (police-constable D 81.) On the night of the 18th of May, I was on duty in Bulstrode-street—I saw the prisoner coming up the street with this cushion under his arm, between ten and eleven o'clock, in a hurried manner—I asked what he had got—he made no answer—I then took him to the station-house—he then said he found it.





HENRY SMITH
. I am coachman to Mr. Bazalgette, of Gloucester-place—we hired a carriage that day, which was waiting at the door of our

stable, in Gloucester-mews—the driving-cushion was on the box, and it was stolen.





JOHN SLY
. I am ostler to Mr. Joseph Coatsworth, who lives in Clay. street—we lent the carriage, with this cushion on the seat, to the master of the last witness.

Prisoner's Defence. I found it.




GUILTY
. Aged 28.—


Transported for Seven Years.
