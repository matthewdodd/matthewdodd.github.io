



2374. 




GEORGE SMITH
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
22nd of October, 1 handkerchief, value 5s. the goods of 



Richard Scrivener, from his person.






RICHARD SCRIVENER
. I am porter at Reeds and Co., of Aldennanbury. On the night of the 22nd of October I was near the Exchange, and felt my pocket picked—I turned round and looked, and the prisoner was running away—I called, "Stop thief," and pursued him—he was stopped, and the watchman produced my handkerchief—this is it.





THOMAS BENJAMIN MACKEY
. I was passing near the Exchange, and immediately followed the prisoner—I saw him throw the handkerchief

away when I was within a yard and a half of him—I did not lose sight of him at all—the officer took him.





PHILLIP TURNER
. I am a policeman. I was on duty and heard the alarm, and saw the prisoner running—I stopped him, and took him to the station-house—I found the handkerchief by the Insurance office, when he had run, and where the witness pointed out.




GUILTY
. Aged 18—


Transported for Ten Years.
