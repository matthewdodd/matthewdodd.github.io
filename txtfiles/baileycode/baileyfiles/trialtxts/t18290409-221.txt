





Fifth Middlesex Jury - before Mr. Common Sergeant.
900. 
               
                  
                     ELEANOR 
                     COTTER
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 24th of February
, 1 gown, value 4s., and 1 apron, value 6d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Sarah 
                     Porton
                  




               
            .


                  MARY 
                  JACKSON
               



            . The prisoner came to me, on the 24th of February, about twelve o'clock, and asked for Louisa's things; I said I had not any of them - she then asked for Sally's blue gown and apron; I gave them to her, supposing 
               
                  Sarah 
                  Porton
               



             had sent her.


                  SARAH 
                  PORTON
               



            . I had never seen the prisoner that day; she used to work in the same house as me - I did not send her for the things.


                  BOYD 
                  SILVESTER
               



            . I am an officer. I took the prisoner on the 2d of March, she had this apron on - I asked where the gown was; she told me she went for some things, and got these in mistake; that 
               
                  Samuel 
                  Smith
               



            , who was outside, said "Keep them;" they took them to the Minories, and pawned the gown.


                  EDWARD 
                  PERKINS
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I took this gown in pawn from the prisoner.(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 17.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
