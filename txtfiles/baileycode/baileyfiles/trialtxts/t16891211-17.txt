                   on the 14th of Novemb.
 last: Seward Confest before the Justice, that he being Servant
 in the next House, got into Halls, and let in Harwood, and that Harwood took away 12 pair of Silver Clasps value 30 s. 20 Gold Rings value 5 l. one Silver Bell value 5 s. 40 Ounces of Silver value 10 l. with some Silver Buttons value 20 s.
             It was Credibly Evidenced, that the Prosecutors Cellar was broke, and that Seward let the other Boy into the House, and put him into the Dust-hole for 6 d. and about 4 or 5 a Clock in the Morning Committed the Robbery: but Harwood had good Evidence to prove his Honesty, so he was 

               Acquitted
            , and Seward found 

               Guilty
             of the Felony and Burglary.
