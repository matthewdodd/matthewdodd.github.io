





258. 
               
                  
                     CHARLES 
                     PENRY
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               that he, on the 27th of December
, did unlawfully, and by false pretences, obtain from 
                     
                        Samuel 
                        Hazeland
                     



                  , 7lbs. of pepper, value 1l. 1s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  Miles 
                  Stringer
               



             and 
               
                  Thomas 
                  Richardson
               



            .


                  SAMUEL 
                  HAZELAND
               



            . I am warehouseman to Messrs. Miles Stringer and Thomas Richardson, of Monument-yard, tea-dealers

. On the 27th of December, the prisoner called and produced this order-(reads).
"27th of December, 1817.
"7lbs. of white pepper, "R. STERRY and Co." by which I understood that he came from Sterry and Co. for 7lbs. of pepper for them. I gave it to him, and he took it away. I asked him his name - He said it was Clarke. I am sure he is the man.
WILLIAM FAULKNER. I am warehouseman to 
               R. 
            Sterry and Co. The prisoner formerly lived servant with them, and was discharged in 1816-the order is his hand-writing, and is not their order.
Prisoner. I have nothing to say.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 40.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
London Jury, before Mr. Common Sergeant.
