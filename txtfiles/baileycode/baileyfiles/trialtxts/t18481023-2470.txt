



2470. 




JOHN MILLER



, stealing 1/4 lb. weight of tea, and 1 sheet of paper, value 2s. 5d.; the goods of 



James John Charles Child, having been befre convicted. 







STEPHEN WENTWORTH
. I am in the service of Mr. Child, grocer, of Norwood. On 13th Oct. I was with his cart, at Norwood—I went into a customer's house, and the prisoner was brought there to me by a gentleman, who gave him in charge, and gave me a 1/4 lb. of tea—the prisoner said I was to give him a thrashing, and let him go—it is James John Charles Child's property.





GEORGE ROUS
 (policeman). The prisoner was given into ray charge.





JOHN TANNER
. I was at my window, and saw the prisoner come away from the cart—he was about twenty yards from it—I went, took hold of him, and the tea dropped from his coat—I picked it up, and gave it to Wentworth.





WILLIAM WESTON
 (policeman). I produce a certificate of the prisoner's conviction—(read—Convicted Jan., 1848, and confined three months and whipped)—I was present—he is the person.




GUILTY
 †. Aged 16.—


Transported for Seven Years
.
