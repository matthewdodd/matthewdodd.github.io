






SEVENTH DAY. THURSDAY, SEPTEMBER 21.
Third Middlesex Jury, before Mr. Common Sergeant.
1657. 
               
                  MARTIN 
                  PALFRIN
               




             and 
               
                  JOHN 
                  CARRON
               




             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 25th of July
, 1 handkerchief, value 5s., the goods of 
                     
                        Joseph 
                        Moore
                     




                  , from his person
            .
JOSEPH MOORE. On the 25th of July I was in Southampton-row


 - a person gave me information, and I missed my handkerchief, which I had used immediately before. I turned round, and found it in the possession of Palfrin; Carron was behind, but I did not see him till he was in custody.


                  THOMAS 
                  BELLINGHAM
               



            . I am a tailor, and live in Southampton-row. I saw Moore pass my door, and Carron was following close at his heels - I saw Palfrin on his left; Carron took the handkerchief from Moore's pocket, and gave it to Palfrin - I am certain of their persons; I went and took them.
Prisoner CARRON. He was looking through the glass door. Witness. No, my door was open.


                  ROBERT 
                  DUKE
               



            . I am the officer, and took the prisoners.(Property produced and sworn to.)
PALFRIN - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 16.
CARRON - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 17.





               Transported for Seven Years
            .
