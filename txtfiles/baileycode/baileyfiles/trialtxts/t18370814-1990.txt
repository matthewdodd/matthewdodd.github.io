






1990. 




WILLIAM HENRY HAIRS,



 ABRAHAM HAIRS
, and 




JAMES HOLGATE
 were again indicted for

 stealing, on the 
27th of July, 1 gown, value 1s.; 2 shirts, value 6s.; 2 caps, value 3d.; 1 neckerchief, value 3d.; 1 pillow-case, value 2d.; 1 pair of stockings, value 1s.; 1 apron, value 2d.; and 1 handkerchief, value 2d.; the goods of 



James Bigsby.






MARY ANN BIGSBY
. I am the wife of James Bigsby, of Barking—about half-past six o'clock in the morning, on the 27th of July, these things were left in the shed—I came back in an hour—they were then gone—these are the things.





GEORGE BIGGS
. In consequence of information, I went after the prisoners, and found all three a little after eight o'clock, with all these things wrapped round them wet.

William Henry Hairs' Defence. I and my brother said we would go and see the country, to get a living, and met this other boy—he asked whether he might come with us, and I said "Yes"—about half-past five o'clock in the morning—and this boy said, "Here is a tub I found."

WILLIAM HENRY HAIRS*—


GUILTY
. Aged 19.




Transported for Seven Years.


ABRAHAM HAIRS—


GUILTY
. Aged 17.

JAMES HOLGATE—


GUILTY
. Aged 13.






Confined for Six Months.
