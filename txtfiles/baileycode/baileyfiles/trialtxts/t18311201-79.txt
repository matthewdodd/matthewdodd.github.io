





First London Jury, before Mr. Recorder.
79. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     HARRISON
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 25th of November
, 1 handkerchief, value 3s., the goods of 
                     
                        Thomas 
                        Shackleton
                     




                  , from his person
            .


                  THOMAS 
                  SHACKLETON
               



            . I live in Goswell-road. On the 25th of November, between eleven and twelve o'clock in the day, I was at the corner of Long-lane, Smithfield


, coming from St. John-street - there was a cry of Thieves! and people were running - I put my hand into my pocket and my handkerchief was gone; I am sure it was safe half an hour before, when I left home - it was produced to me after the prisoner was secured; I had not seen him near me.


                  JOHN 
                  ROSE
               



            . I am an officer. I saw the prisoner near Mr. Shackleton, and saw him take the handkerchief out of Mr. Shackleton's pocket; he had two companious with him - he ran away; I went after him, and lost the other two - there was a cry of Stop thief! and he was stopped at the corner of the first turning, about thirty yards off; I took the handkerchief out of his hand - he denied having had it; Mr. Shackleton claimed it.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I picked it off the ground by a picture-shop, and told the officer so.


                  JOHN 
                  ROSE
               



            . He did not.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 19. - 


               Transported for Life
            .
