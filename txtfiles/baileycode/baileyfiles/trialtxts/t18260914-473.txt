






1785. 
               
                  THOMAS JOSEPH 
                  BACON
               




             and 
               
                  THOMAS 
                  GARROWAY
               




             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 11th of July
, 1 pair of breeches, value 9s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  Edward 
                  Adams
               



             and 
               
                  George 
                  Clamp
               



            .


                  WILLIAM 
                  ADAMS
               



            . I live with Edward Adams, my father, who is in partnership with George Clamp; they live in Aldersgate-street


. On the 11th of July these breeches hung about five inches within the door; I heard the cry of Stop thief! I ran to the door, missed some things, crossed over, and found the prisoners in custody, with the breeches, twenty doors off.


                  CORNELIUS 
                  BIRDSEYE
               



            . My father lives in Old-street. I saw the prisoner, Bacon, coming from Adams' shop - I and another person met him; I had seen the prisoners in company about the neighbourhood for a quarter of an hour, and watched them, but did not see the breeches taken.


                  ROBERT 
                  TYRRELL
               



            . I took the prisoners on the 11th of July, in Aldersgate-street, near Adams' shop; we had been watching them; I saw Garroway cross the road - Bacon came to him with something under his coat - I seized him, and took him with the breeches; Birdseys ran and took Garroway.(Property produced and sworn to).
BACON'S Defence. I bought them.
BACON - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 22.
GARROWAY - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 16.





               Transported for Seven Years
            .
