





1106. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     THOMPSON
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 29th of August
, one handkerchief, value 2 s., the goods of a certain 
                     man


                   unknown, from his person
            .


                  HENRY 
                  YATES
               



            . I was on duty as a constable in St. James's-street


, and saw the prisoner very active in the mob - he attempted a dozen different peoples' pockets. At last I saw him take a blue handkerchief from a gentleman's pocket, and as the Queen left the house, I followed him to Pall-mall, apprehended him, and took six handkerchiefs from him, one of which was that I saw him take I had told the gentleman of it, but he went away.


                  EDWARD 
                  RHIND
               



            . I was with Yates, and saw the prisoner take the blue handkerchief from a gentleman in the square, secured him, and found six handkerchiefs on him.


                  THOMAS 
                  GOOK
               



            . I was in the square, and confirm the witness's statement.
Prisoner's Defence. I bought the handkerchiefs of a man on Westminister-bridge.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 36.




               Transported for Life
            .
Third Middlesex Jury, before H. R. Reynolds, Esq.
