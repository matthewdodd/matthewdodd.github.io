





NEW COURT Seventh Day.
Second London Jury - Before Mr. Common Sergeant.
1835. 
               
                  
                     PHILIP 
                     OVERTON
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 5th of December
, 1 dressing-case, value 3l. 15s.; 1 pair of boot-books, value 3s. 6d.; 1 pair of nail-nippers, value 5s.; 1 pair of scissors, value 3s. 6d.; 1 shaving-glass, value 4s. 6d.; 1 shaving-brush, value 4s. 6d.; 2 glass boxes, with plated tops, value 4s.; 1 hair-brush, value 2s. 6d., and 1 clothes-brush, value 2s. 6d., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Joshua Strickland 
                           Rigge
                        




                     
                  , and another, and to whom he was servant
. Also for stealing, on the 16th of February
, 12 dessert knives, value 2l. 2s.; 12 dessert forks, value 1l. 1s., and 30 pocket-knives, value 9l., the goods of the same persons. Also for stealing, on the 2nd of February, 6 dressing-cases, value 6l.; 16 razors, value 30s.; 5 combs, value 13s.; 1 bottle of otto of roses, value 3s.; 17 pairs of scissors, value 2l. 6s.; 16 brushes, value 16s.; 4 razor-strops, value 4s.; 1 workbox, value 12s.; 130 knives, value 12l.; 130 forks, value 6l.; 5 snuff-boxes, value 25s.; 100 fish-books, value 5s.; 1 segar-case, value 1s.; 1 razor-case, value 2s.; 1 glass bottle, value 1s.; 4 pairs of tweezers, value 2s.; 4 shaving-boxes, value 4s.; 2 nail-files, value 1s.; 2 corkscrews, value 2s., and 1 button-hook, value 1s., the goods of the same persons. And also for stealing, on the 2nd of March, 61 lbs. weight of ivory, value 7l. 10s., their property.
            

The prisoner 


               pleaded GUILTY
            . Aged 23.




               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
