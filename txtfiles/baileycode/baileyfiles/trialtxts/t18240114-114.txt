





321. 
               
                  
                     DAVID 
                     HOPE
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 10th of December
, a stock, value 11 s., and six stock bits, value 4 s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     James 
                     Whiting
                  




               
            .


                  JAMES 
                  WHITING
               



            . I am a carpenter
. On the 10th of December, I left a stock and six bits at a building, in Wardour-street


 - they were stolen from there on the 21st. I know nothing of the prisoner.


                  JOSEPH 
                  TILL
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, and live in Compton-street, Soho. I have a stock and six bits, pawned on the 11th of December, in the name of Davis - I do not know who by.


                  BENJAMIN 
                  WEBB
               



            . I am a constable. On the 20th of December, I apprehended the prisoner and three others. on another charge, and found some duplicates on him. I went to his lodging, by his direction, and found more duplicates, among which, was one for these tools - he said they were his uncle's, and then that he bought the duplicateof a man in the street, and afterwards that he bought it at the Blue Posts, public-house, Berwick-street.


                  JOSEPH 
                  TILL
               



            . It is the duplicate I gave the person.
(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I bought it of a man at the public-house.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 18.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
