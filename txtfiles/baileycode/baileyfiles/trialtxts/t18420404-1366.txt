




1366. 




JOHN BARRELL
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
12th of March, 2 nose-bags, value 3s., the goods of 



John Edgington and another; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






WILLIAM BOSWELL
. I am carman to Mr. John Edgington and another, coal-merchants, at Blackfriars Wharf. On the 12th of March I was unloading ray wagon, in Wentworth-street, Whitechapel—I had four nose-bags hanging on the side of the wagon—I left it for a short time, and when I came back I missed two nose-bags—these now produced are them, and are my master's property.





WILLIAM BROOMCROSS
. I am a policeman. On the 12th of March, about six o'clock in the evening, I met the prisoner in Shoreditch, offering these nose-bags for sale to a cab man—I took him into custody.

Prisoner. I was tipsy at the time.





GEORGE COMARFORD
. I am a policeman. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, from Mr. Clark's office—(read)—I was present at the trial—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 40.—


Transported for Seven Years.
