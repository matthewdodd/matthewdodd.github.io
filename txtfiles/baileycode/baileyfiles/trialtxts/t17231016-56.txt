            , the 30th of September
 last. It appeared by the Evidence; That the Things had been stolen out of t Compting: House of Mr. Scot, and the Goods were found upon the Prisoner. Reeves pleaded, he borrowed the Coat of Kent. Their Confession was read in Court, wherein they both confess'd the Breaking the Compting House, and stealing the Goods mentioned in the Indictment; and also some others, but the Compting-House not being adjoining to the Dwelling House, it was only a single Felony, of which the Jury found them both
