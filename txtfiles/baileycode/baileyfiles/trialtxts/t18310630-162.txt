





1350. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     BAILEY
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 25th of May
, 30lbs. of butter, value 1l. 6s., and 1 basket, value 2s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Richard 
                     Parker
                  




               
            .


                  WILLIAM 
                  TIMS
               



            . I am a waggoner in the employ of Mr. 
               
                  Richard 
                  Parker
               



            ; he lives at Witney. I was driving a waggon on the 25th of May, from Red Hill to London - the prisoner came with me from Red-hill; I missed him on Ealing-common


 - he had been riding and walking about ten miles; he rode behind the waggon, not in it - I missed him and a basket of butter at the same time; the butter was worth 30s. - it was in the County of Middlesex; I have seen the flat of butter since - this is it; I had seen it about an hour before, and I saw it in the officer's hands the next day - I had given the officer information.


                  SAMUEL 
                  BOYD
               



            . I am a Police-officer. In consequence of information, on the 25th of May, I looked for the prisoner, and found him in Webb-lane, just going into Hammersmith, with this flat of butter - the butter was given up; this is the basket - this ticket was with it.(Property produced and sworn to)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 27. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
