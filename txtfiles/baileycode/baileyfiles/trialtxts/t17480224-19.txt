





139. 
               
                  
                     Ann 
                     Barrett 
                  



               
            , of St. Giles's Cripplegate , London


, was indicted for 


               stealing three shirts , value 18 s. an apron, value 9 s. a handkerchief, value 3 s. two caps, value 9 s. 6 d. and a pair of ruffles , value 2 s.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     Thompson 
                     Packer 
                  




                
             , January 9
.


                  Grace 
                  Packer 
               



            . I delivered this linen to 
               
                  Catherine 
                  Stanton 
               



             to wash; I went to enquire after them, and Stanton told me a kinswoman of hers had robbed her of them .


                  Catherine 
                  Stanton 
               



             . I received this linen of Mrs. Packer, and the Prisoner owned she took it all this shirt was pawned , which is all that is found: she told Mr. Packer where it was; she said some were pawned, and some sold.


                  Thompson 
                  Packer 
               



             . The Prisoner confessed she had pawned this shirt for 2 s. 6 d. at Mrs. Olliman's on the other side of the water, and I found it there.


                  John 
                  Lewis 
               



            . The Prisoner confessed she had stole these things, and had pawned an apron and a laced cap for 3 s. 6 d. and a lawn handkerchief and a pair of ruffles for a shilling.



               Guilty
            .
