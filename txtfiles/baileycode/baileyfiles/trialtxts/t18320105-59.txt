






352. 
               
                  
                     THOMAS 
                     PEARCE
                  



               
             and 
               
                  
                     HENRY 
                     SELLERS
                  



               
             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 15th of December
, 1 quart pot, value 18d., and 1 pint pot, value 1s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           William 
                           Best
                        



                     
                  ; and 1 pint pot, value 1s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Abraham 
                           Hadway
                        



.


                  HENRY 
                  BROWN
               



            . On the 15th of December, I saw Sellers take a pint pot off the rails of No. 57, Harley-street


 - I was at the next house; the other prisoner was close to him at the time - I saw a pot-boy, and told him of it.


                  ABRAHAM 
                  RADWAY
               



            . This pint pot is mine - it was found on Sellers.


                  HENRY 
                  EGERTON
               



            . I am a Police-constable. I was in Portland-place when the pot-boy told me of the prisoners; I followed them, and knocked off their hats - I found this quart pot in Pearce's hat, and the other pint pot belonging to Radway in his acket pocket - Sellers had the other pint.


                  WILLIAM 
                  BEST
               



            . I keep the George, in Queen Anne-street, St. Marylebone
 - this quart pot and pint pot are mine; I have lost a great many pots lately.
PEARCE - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 22.
SELLERS - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 18.





               Transported for Seven Years
            .
