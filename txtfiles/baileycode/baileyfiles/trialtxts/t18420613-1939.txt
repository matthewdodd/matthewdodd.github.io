



1939. 




JOSEPH SMITH
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
14th of June, 1 handkerchief, value 3s., the goods of 



George Spelt, from his person; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






GEORGE SPELT
. I live in Seymour-street, Euston-square. About twelve o'clock in the day of the 14th of June I was in Bridge-street—I saw the prisoner put his hand into my pocket, take my handkerchief out, and put it into his own—I took it from his pocket, and took him to the station.





JOHN EDWARD GLASCOCK
 (police-sergeant R 25.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clack's office—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 15.—



Transported for Seven Years.—Convict Ship.
