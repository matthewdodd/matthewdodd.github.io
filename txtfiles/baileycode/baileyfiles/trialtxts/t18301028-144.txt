







1984. 
               
                  
                     SUSAN 
                     REYNOLDS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 11th of October


. 1 ring, value 8s.; 1 pair of earrings, value 1l.; 3 yards of lace, value 6s.; 1 gown-skirt, value 3s.; 1 petticoat, value 1s.; 2 handkerchiefs, value 1s., and 2 pillow-cases, value 2s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Frances Oke 
                     Smith
                  



               
            .
FRANCES OKE SMITH. I was housemaid
 to Mr. Marks, of No. 33, Euston-square




, and the prisoner was cook
 - she lived there one month: I missed these articles the day after she was gone.
ROBERT ISAACSON. I am a Police-constable. I was called by the prosecutrix, and took the prisoner; she gave me the key of her box, and I found this property in it.
Cross-examined by MR. PHILLIPS. Q. Did not she seem surprised that they were found there? A. No - I found these articles on the Saturday evening, and on the Monday I went and found the ring and the ear-rings.


                  FRANCES 
                  OKE SMITH
               



            . I searched her box very attentively the first time, except one place, which she kept her finger upon, and said I should break her work-box.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 24.
