





646. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     JOHNSON
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 15th March
, forty-nine yards of flannel, value 2 l.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Adam 
                     Mather
                  




               
            .


                  ADAM 
                  MATHER
               



            . I am a hosier
, and live in Sun-street, Bishopsgate-street


. On the 15th of March this flannel stood on a stool at the door. I saw a hand lift it off, there was a cry of Stop thief, and it was thrown down about three yards from my door, I picked it up.
(Property produced and sworn to.)


                  ROBERT 
                  WAY
               



            . I was in Sun-street, I saw the prisoner in company with another man. I saw a lady looking intoMather's shop, and as soon as she entered the door the prisoner snatched the flannel. I was loaded or I could have stopped him. I called Stop him, he dropped it, and a person followed him towards Bishopsgate-street. I put my load into a shop, followed, and met him in the custody of Riley, and am certain that he is the person.


                  WILLIAM 
                  RILEY
               



            . I saw him running. A gentleman apprehended him, and gave him to me. Way came up and said he was the man.
Prisoner's Defence. I was coming up Sun-street, heard an alarm, and pursued a man, a gentleman came up and said I was the man.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 17.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
