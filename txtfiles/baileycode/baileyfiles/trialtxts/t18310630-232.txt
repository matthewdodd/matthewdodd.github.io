





1420. 
               
                  WILLIAM 
                  TIGHE
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 25th of June
, two pewter pots, value 18d., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           George 
                           Young
                        



                     
                  ; 2 pewter pots, value 18d., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           John 
                           Gurr
                        



                     
                  ; and 1 pewter pot, value 6d
            , the goods of 
               
                  John 
                  Bennett
               



            .


                  CHARLES 
                  HAYWARD
               



            . I am servant at a public-house, in George-street, Manchester-square. I was coming up an area in Manchester-square, and saw the prisoner with two pots - I went and asked what he was going to do with them; he said he was starving - I took him to Young's, and these other pots were found on him.


                  GEORGE 
                  YOUNG
               



            . I keep a public-house
, in Duke-street, Manchester-square. Two of these pots are mine; I know nothing of the prisoner.
GEORGE BANCHINI. I live with Mr. 
               
                  John 
                  Gurr
               



            , of the Albion tavern, Duke-street. Two of these pots are ours- I know nothing of the prisoner.


                  JOHN 
                  BENNETT
               



            . I keep the Red Lion, in Gilbert-street
, a quarter of a mile from Manchester-square. One of these pots is mine - I do not know the prisoner.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 19. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
