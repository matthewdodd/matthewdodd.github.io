               privately stealing a wooden Box for Tea, Value 8 s. the Goods of

                           Briller 
                        



                      
                  , in their Shop
            , Feb. 19
.
Messieurs Kelar and Briller keep a Cabinet-maker's Shop

 in Holborn


; the Prisoner had been often there to cheapen Goods, but commonly offer'd them less than prime Cost. On Saturday Morning last, he came again on the same Pretence, and soon after he was gone, a Mahogony Tea-box was missing: The Prisoner went to the Ditch side, and sold it to 
               
                  John 
                  Polly 
               



             for 4 s. in whose Shop Mr. Kelar found it. Mr. Polly described the Person he bought it of, and the Prisoner coming soon after to Mr. Kelar's Shop to cheapen more Goods, he was apprehended. The Jury found him 


               guilty to the Value of 4 s. 10 d.
