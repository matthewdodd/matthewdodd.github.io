





456. (L.) 
               
                  
                     Peter 
                     Pennythorne 
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing 56 lb. weight of paper, val. 10 s.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     Archibald 
                     Hamilton 
                  




                
            . ++
The prisoner lived servant
 with Mr. Hagard, next door to Mr. Hamilton's, in Chancery-lane; a quantity of numbers of Smollet's History of England being sold by the prisoner to Mr. Showel, a cheesemonger, in Fleet-market, he serving Mr. Hamilton with butter, &c. some of the said paper was sent there with goods, by which means it came out; the numbers were looked over in Mr. Hamilton's warehouse, and the quantity mentioned in the indictment missing.
Mr. Showel deposed, that the prisoner brought and sold it him as waste paper.




               Guilty 10 d.
            



               T.
            


See him tried before, No. 277 in Mr. Alderman Chitty's mayoralty, for stealing a looking-glass.
