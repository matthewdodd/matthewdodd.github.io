








                     Richard 
                     Eaton
                  



               
             and 
               
                  
                     James 
                     Arden
                  



               
             were both Indicted for 


               Robbing 
                     
                        
                           Henry 
                           Moseles
                        




                     
                   of Stepney, on the 20th of August
, of 13 Guineas and 3 pieces of Jacobus, 2 King Jame's Pieces of Gold, and other Gold, besides 5 l. in Mony, value about 24 l.
             which Eaton took out of the Prosecutor's House and gave to Arden; Which Gold afterwards was found buried under an Elder-Tree, in a Yard of the Prosecuters. All which the Boy
, Eaton, confest before Justice Withers but Arden would not confess any thing He said, He did not know whose Mony it was, and that he never buried it. Upon the whole, Arden was found to entice the Boy to Rob his Master; yet in the Conclusion they were both found 

               Guilty
