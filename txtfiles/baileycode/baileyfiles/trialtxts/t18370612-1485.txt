



1485. 




GEORGE BOLTON
 was indicted

 for embezzlement.






GEORGE JAMES BRAIN
. I an a cabinet-maker and live in Wbrtmore-road. The prisoner is my apprentice—I received 3l. with him from the parish—on the 15th of May, I sent him with a flute to pawn at Mr. Leach's in Hoxton, for 2s. 6d.—it was just to finish some work I had in hand—he did not return—I found him next morning in the Lower-road, Islington, looking at some boys playing at pitch-halfpenny—I asked him for two half-crown—he said if I went to his sister perhaps she would pay me; but I took him into custody.





CHARLES CLIFT
 I am apprentice to Mr. Leach, a pawnbroker. On the 15th of May, the prisoner pawned a flute for his master for 2s. 6d—I knew him before.





JOHN KERSHAW
 (police-aonstable G 123.) I took the prisoner, and found the duplicate on him.

Prisoner. My master ill-used me, and did not give me half enough victuals.




GUILTY
 Aged 15.—


Transported for Seven Years.
