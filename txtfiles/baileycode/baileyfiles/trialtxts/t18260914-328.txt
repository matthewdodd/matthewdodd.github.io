





1640. 
               
                  JOHN 
                  CATLIN
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 17th of July
, 1 handkerchief, value 4s., the goods of a certain 
                     man unknown


                  , from his person
            .


                  DANIEL 
                  RIERDON
               



            . I was in the Strand


 between nine and ten o'clock at night, on the 17th of July - I saw the prisoner and two lads opposite Exeter-Change, following two gentleman - one of them took a handkerchief from one of the gentleman's pockets, and handed it to the prisoner, who put it into the flap of his breeches; I took him, and got it from him.


                  WILLIAM 
                  McGREGOR
               



            . I was with Rierdon - I saw the prisoner and a companion rather less than him, take the handkerchief and gave it to him - he was about half a yard from him; he put it into the left side of his breeches - I followed the gentleman and said, "You have lost your handkerchief;" he turned round, and said, "My God - it is gone." I do not know who he was.
Prisoner's Defence. When he took me he said "Mac, let us take him to our old shop - we can do as we like there, and have him fully committed in the morning - let us take him to St. Ann's watch-house, they will take in any charge there;" the next morning McGregor said I took the handkerchief, and the other said, "Mind what you say," and then he said one of the others took it.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 15.




               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
