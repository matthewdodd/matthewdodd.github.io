





36. 
               
                  
                     Jane 
                     May 
                  



               
            , was indicted for 


               stealing a Silver Spoon, a Saucepan, 6 Napkins, 2 Knives, 3 Forks, 4 Wine Glasses, 1 Mustard Glass, and a Vinegar Crewet
            , the Goods of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Goosetry 
                  




                
            , November 27
.
The Prisoner was Servant
 to the Prosecutor, at the Old Devil Tavern, Temple-Bar


. He had missed several Things, and being informed that she frequently went out early in the Morning, he order'd his Boy to watch her. The Boy dogg'd her to the House of - Barns, an old Woman in White's-Alley, where she was taken and the Goods found upon her. She was carry'd before Justice Newton, to whom she made a full Confession which was taken in Writing; and was prov'd and read in Court. The Jury found her 

               Guilty
            .
