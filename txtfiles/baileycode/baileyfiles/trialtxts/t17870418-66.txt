





395. 
               
                  
                     JANE 
                     DUNDAS
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 26th day of February
 last, one linen tablecloth, value 4 s. two napkins, value 2 s. one towel, value 4 d.
             the property of the Right Honorable 
               
                  John 
                  Skinner
               




            , Knight
.
- DOWSON sworn.
I am butler to Sir 
               
                  John
                   Skynner
               



            ; the prisoner was laundry maid
 about a year and a half; the pawnbroker informed us before we missed the linen; the prisoner did not deny it.
(Mr. Burkitt, the pawn-broker, produced the things, which were deposed to.)
She said she lived with Judge Heath; I sent there and they knew nothing of her; then I was a going to send for a constable, and she fell a crying, and said she lived at 
               
                  Baron
                   Skinner
               



            's, and they were his property.

Prisoner. I beg for mercy; I have nobody to my character; I leave myself entirely to Sir John's mercy and the gentlemen of the Jury.
Dowson. She behaved vastly well always; bore a very good character for years before, a quiet sober girl.
Prisoner. I intended to redeem them again.
Court. You could not be distressed for money in that service I am sure.
Prisoner. It was for the lottery.



               GUILTY
            .




               Transported for seven years
            .
Tried by the first Middlesex Jury before Mr. RECORDER.
