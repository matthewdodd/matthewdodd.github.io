



2424. 




HENRY FIELD



, stealing one handkerchief, value 4s.; the goods of 



William Maynard; having been before convicted.






ELIZABETH MAYNARD
. I am wife of William Maynard, carpenter of Stratford—on 12th Oct., just after eleven o'clock,. I hung out a silk handkerchief, belonging to William Biggs, and some linen to dry, in the yard—Mary Reed told me something—I missed the handkerchief.





MARY REED
. I was at Mr. Maynard's, and saw the prisoner take a handkerchief from the line, and walk away with it—I knew him by sight about Bow—I told Mrs. Maynard.

Prisoner's Defence. I was not in Stratford that morning.





CHARLES ROWBURY
 (policeman). I produce a certificate of the prisoner's conviction—(read—Convicted Jan. 1848—confined six months)—I was present—he is the person.




GUILTY
 **†. Aged 19.—


Transported for Seven Years
.
