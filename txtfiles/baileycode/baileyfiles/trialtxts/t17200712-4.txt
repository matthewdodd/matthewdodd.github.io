







                     Margaret 
                     Townley 
                  



               
            , of St. Brides


, was indicted for 


               privately stealing 13 yards of Ribbon, value 6 s. 6 d. out of the Shop of 
                     
                        
                           Elizabeth 
                           Eades 
                        




, on the 18th of June
 last. 
               
                  Mary 
                  Leighton 
               



             deposed, that the Prisoner came into her Mother's Shop to buy Ribbon, and that she saw her take the Goods mentioned in the Indictment, out of the Window; that she let her go out of the Shop a good way, and then sent to fetch her back again, that when she was brought back she searcht her, and took the Ribbon out of her Pocket, which was produc'd in Court and swore to; that she found other Pieces of Ribbon about her, but could not swear to them; that then the Prisoner fell down on her Knees and begg'd pardon. Two other Evidences deposed that they saw the Ribbon taken out of her Pocket, and that she fell down on her Knees and begg'd Pardon. The Jury considering the matter, found her 


               Guilty to the value of 4 s. 10 d
            . 


               Transportation
