





849. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     SLY
                  



               
             was indicted for that 


               he on the 9th of September
, in the 49th year of his Majesty's reign, feloniously and without any authority in writing for the purpose from the governor of the bank of England, did engrave and cut and knowingly aiding and assisting in engraving and cutting in and upon a certain plate of copper, a certain note purporting to be a bank note for the payment of one pound
            .
To this indictment the prisoner 


               pleaded GUILTY
            .




               Transported for seven years
            .
Second Middlesex jury, before Lord Ellenborough.
