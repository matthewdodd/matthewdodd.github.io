





1968. 
               
                  FRANCIS 
                  LACLAIR
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 19th of September
, 2 shirts, value 4s.; 2 nightgowns, value 1s.; 3 aprons, value 1s. 6d.; 1 towel, value 6d.; 11 pairs of stockings, value 12s.; 2 sheets, value 4s.; 1 handkerchief, value 1s.; 1 table-cloth, value 2s.; 2 pairs of drawers, value 4s., and 1 night cap, value 6d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  Robert 
                  Brooks
               




            .
ROBERT BROOKS. I live in Francis-street, Chelsea


. On the 19th of September, about nine o'clock, I saw these things safe in my wash-house, which is part of the house - between five and six o'clock next morning. I missed them - my wife is a laundress - I know nothing of the prisoner.


                  ELIZABETH 
                  BROOKS
               



            . I am the prosecutor's wife. I left these things all safe in the wash-house; the person who took them had got over the wall, into the yard - the wash-house door will not shut.


                  WILLIAM 
                  MILLS
               



            . On the 19th of September, in the morning - I heard Mr. Lake call for assistance - I went and found the prisoners on his premises, behind some timber, with this bundle by his side. I collared him, and took him to the watch-house with it.


                  WILLIAM 
                  ALLEN
               



            . The prisoner was brought to the watch-house - I found two sheets round his body, a handkerchief in his hat, and these drawers in his pocket.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I saw two men get over this wall- these things laid in a bundle, and some were loose.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 20.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
