





1706. 
               
                  
                     HENRY 
                     DAVIES
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 2nd of September
, 1 cruet-stand, value 10., and 1 pair of sugar-tongs, value 5s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Joseph 
                     Poulton
                  




               
            .


                  HENRY 
                  AKER
               



            . I am agent to Mr. Joseph Poulton. who is a tea-dealer
, in the City. On the 1st of September. I was in the shop, at Hackney


, and passing the parlour-door I saw the prisoner - I went into the parlour. and he had this cruet-stand in his hand, which he had taken from the cupboard - I charged him with having stolen it; he said a lady sent him for it; he said it was his first offence, and wished me to let him go - I sent for an officer, and he found these sugar-tongs in his pocket, which are also the property of the prosecutor.


                  JOHN 
                  BEDFORD
               



            . I am a Police-officer. I was sent for,and found these tongs; the stand, and these two dozen of pencils were on the table.(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 14 - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
