





144. 
               
                  
                     JAMES 
                     WILLIAMS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 25th of November
, one looking-glass, value 20 s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Samuel 
                     Lince
                  




               
            .



                  SAMUEL 
                  LINCE
               



            . I keep the Coachmakers' Arms, public-house
, Long-acre. On the 25th of November, I was in the bar - the prisoner came in. I saw him go out with the looking-glass. I went out, and called Stop thief! he dropped it immediately, and I secured him.
(Property produced and sworn to.)


                  JOHN 
                  NEWMAN
               



            . I am an officer. I heard the cry of Stop thief! and saw the prisoner running; he fell down. I secured him, and he begged for mercy.
Prisoner's Defence. I was not in the house.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 23.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
Second Middlesex Jury, before Mr. Recorder.
