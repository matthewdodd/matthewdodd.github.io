



1615. 




CHARLES WILSON
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
24th of May, 1 shilling, 1 sixpence, 1 halfpenny, and 1 farthing, the monies of 



Elizabeth Crow, from her person.






ELIZABETH CROW
. I am single, and live in Long-walk, Bermondsey-square. On Tuesday evening, between five and six o'clock, I was at Greenwich fair with my sister—I felt a hand in my pocket—I turned, and the officer had got the prisoner—I had a shilling, a sixpence, a halfpenny, and a farthing—my money was gone, and his hand was taken as it came out of my pocket—the money fell on the ground.





GEORGE POWELL
. (police-constable E 10.) I saw the prisoner with another—I saw the prisoner put this hand into the prosecutrix's pocket—I seized it, and drew out one shilling, one sixpence, one half-penny, and one farthing—he dropped it—I took him to the station-house—he said he should not have done it but for the other boy.

Prisoner She said she would not appear against me, if my father gave her 5l






ELIZABETH CROW
. I saw his father on Sunday—I said I would not say more than I could help, but nothing, was said about 5l





GUILTY
.* Aged 13—


Transported for Seven Years.

Fifth Jury, before Mr. Sergeant Arabin.
