





209. (L.) 
               
                  
                     Eleanor 
                     Nelson 
                  



               
            , spinster
, was indicted for 


               stealing one linen mob, laced, value 4 s. the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Sarah 
                           Crosland 
                        



                      
                  ; one linen handkerchief, laced, value 2 s. one pair of stays, value 12 s. and one linen shirt
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Vivel 
                  



                
            , May 1
. ++


                  Sarah 
                  Crosland 
               



            . The prisoner lived servant
 with me about a month or five weeks; I missed a laced mob and other things of my daughter's, and charged the prisoner with taking them, and she owned it.
Q. Does your daughter live in the same house with you?
S. Crosland. She does; her name is Vivel.
Q. What goods of hers were missing?
S. Crosland. A pair of stays, a shirt, a handkerchief, (the goods mentioned produced in court, and deposed to.)
Q. Where were these goods found again?
S. Crosland. At a pawnbroker's by her direction. She owned also to the taking them before my Lord-Mayor.


                  William 
                  Vivel 
               



            . The prisoner ran away from us this day fortnight; we missed these things; we took her up, and by her direction we found them again.
Prisoner's defence.
I was mean in cloaths and had not necessaries, and I did it for that.



               Guilty
            .
