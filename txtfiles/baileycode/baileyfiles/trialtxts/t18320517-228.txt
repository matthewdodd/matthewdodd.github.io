





1355. 
               
                  JOHN 
                  CHAPMAN
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 8th of May
, 1 tea-caddy, value 7s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           George 
                           Wilson
                        




                     
                  ; and that he had been before convicted of felony
            .
DORCAS CHAMBERLAIN. Mr. Wilson keeps a broker's-shop
. About two o'clock on the 8th of May, I was looking out of my window; I saw the prisoner go and take the caddy and walk away with it - I called Stop thief! and he was taken with it; he had turned a corner, but I know he is the person.
WILLIAM BROWN. I am a green-grocer, and live near the prosecutor. I heard the alarm, and took the prisoner with this caddy under his arm, in Tavistock-place.
MARY WILSON. I am the wife of George Wilson. This is my caddy; I was at home, but did not see it taken.
JAMES FULLER. I am a Police-constable. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clark's office; I know he is the man.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 22. - 


               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
