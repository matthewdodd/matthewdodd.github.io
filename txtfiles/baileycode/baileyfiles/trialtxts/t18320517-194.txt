






1320. 
               
                  
                     FRANCIS 
                     O'BRYAN
                  




               
             and 
               
                  THOMAS 
                  McMAHONEY
               




             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 15th of April
, 1 handkerchief, value 2s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Thomas 
                           Oliver
                        




                     
                  , from his person; and that McMahoney had been before convicted of felony
            .
THOMAS OLIVER. On the 15th of April I was in Riding-house-yard


, between four and five o'clock - I had a handkerchief in my pocket; I felt it go from me; I turned - the prisoners and another boy were close behind me; the officer took them; McMahoney had my handkerchief.
THOMAS HOBBS (Police-constable C 85). I saw the two prisoners follow the prosecutor, and McMahoney took the handkerchief out of his pocket - the other prisoner, and another boy, who got away, were close to him - I had watched them all three in company.


                  CHARLES 
                  LANKINHORN
               



            . I produce a certificate of the conviction of McMahoney, on the 5th of April, for stealing a handkerchief - he was whipped and discharged; I know he is the person.
O'BRYAN - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 16.
MCMAHONEY - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 13.





               Transported for Seven Years
            .
