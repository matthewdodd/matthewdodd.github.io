Esq

; on the 1st. of March
 and taking thence two Coats, one Hat, one Cain and one pair of Fringe-Gloves, all to the value of 3 l. 5 s. being the proper Goods of 
                     
                        James 
                        Crook
                     



. The proof against him was, that the Goods were removed and he found in the house, to which he pleaded he came to enquire for one Mr. Tuisden; but it further appearing that he had Confessed one Clark had been there before him, and removed the Goods; and he not able to give any satisfactory account of himself, was found 


               guilty of the Felony but not of the Burglary
