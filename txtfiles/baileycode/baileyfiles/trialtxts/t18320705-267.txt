





1693. 
               
                  
                     GEORGE 
                     MONEYLAWS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 21st of June
, 1 handkerchief, value 1s.; 1 towel, value 8d.; 1 basket, value 2s., and 6 lbs. weight of beef, value 4s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           James 
                           Ramage
                        




                     
                  ; and that he had been before convicted of felony
            .


                  THOMAS 
                  FARRANT
               



             (Police-constable C 42). On the 21st of June, at nine o'clock at night, I saw the prisoner in Brewer-street, carrying something in his apron - I followed, and asked what he had got; he said a piece of linen, to make himself an apron - I took him to the station, and found the prosecutor, who claimed the property.


                  JAMES 
                  RAMAGE
               



            . I am a tailor
, and live in Little Compton-street


. These are my property, and were taken from my kitchen - I do not know the prisoner.


                  JAMES 
                  RICHARDSON
               



            . I am pot-boy at the Duke of Argyle. On the 21st of June I saw the prisoner in the tap-room, with the parcel.


                  THOMAS 
                  HOBBS
               



             (Police-constable C85). I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clark's office - I know he is the man.
Prisoner's Defence. I bought the things in Clothfair, and intended to make myself an apron.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 33. - 


               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
