            . I lost a pair of silver knee buckles, and an odd stone knee buckle; I saw them where they were pledged; the silver ones were in the possession of

                   Marsh
               



            , the stone one in the possession of 
               
                  John
                   Gant
               



             ( produced and deposed to by the prosecutor.)


                  William
                   Marsh Martha Smith
               



             pledged the silver buckles with me before the 18th of September. I know nothing of the other two prisoners.


                  John
                   Gant
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, in Brick-lane, Spital-fields, the corner of Bush-street; this stone buckle was brought to me by 
               
                  Martha
                   Smith
               



             on the 18th of September. The evidence says she was at the door the while I saw her, when she went out to speak to somebody, I don't know who.


                  Mary
                   Woodward
               



            . I went into this gentleman's house to pawn a child's hat; 
               
                  Mary
                   Thompson
               



             and Smith told me while I went in, they put a knife in the shew glass and took out these buckles.

Smith's Defence.
I have nobody to speak for me.
