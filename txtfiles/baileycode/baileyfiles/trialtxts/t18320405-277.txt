





1080. 
               
                  
                     ANN 
                     EDMONDS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 23rd of March
, 1 shirt, value 3s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Daniels
                  




               
            .


                  JOHN 
                  DAWSON
               



            . I am shopman to William Daniel, who is a pawnbroker
. This shirt hung in the shop for sale; I saw it about eight o'clock in the evening of the 28th of March - I missed it about eleven o'clock the next morning - I do not remember seeing the prisoner.


                  CHARLES 
                  HENRY WATERS
               



            . I am apprentice to Mr. Gofton, pawnbroker, of Gilbert-street, Oxford-street. The prisoner came to our shop to pawn a frock; I saw this shirt in her possession, and in consequence of suspicion,stopped her, and gave her into custody - she said she had it from a young man in Marylebone-lane, and was going to take it to his mother to wash.


                  WILLIAM 
                  TRODD
               



            . I am a Policeman. I received the prisoner in charge; she did not tell me she had the shirt from Marylebone-lane - she ran away as I took her to the watch-house, but I caught her.(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 24. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
