



1721. 




WILLIAM SMITH
 was indicted

for stealings on the 
29th of june, 1 cap, value 3s., the goods of 



George Robert Dawson.






GEOEGE ROBERT DAWSON
. On Friday, June the 29th, about ten o'clock, I was walking in St. Martin's Church-yard. I saw the prisoner come behind me, and snatch my cloth cap off—I and my brother ran after him, and hallooed "Stop thief," and Franklin stopped him—the cap has has been found—there were two more boys with him.





WILLIAUM CHARLES FRANKLIN
. I am a porter. I was in Castle-street, Leicester-square, and saw the prisoner running, followed by Dawson—I ran and stopped him.





THOMAS GOULD
. I am an officer. I took the prisoner into custody.

Prisoners Defence. I know nothing about the cap.




GUILTY
.* Aged 15.—


Transported for Ten Years.
