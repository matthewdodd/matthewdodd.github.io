



762. 




THOMAS FIGG



, stealing 1 coat, 1 handkerchief, and 1 plane, value 15s. 6d.; the goods of 



Benjamin Robert Symons; having been before convicted






BENJAMIN ROBERT SYMONS
. I am a carpenter. I was at work at a new building, at the corner of Warwick-place, last Saturday week—I left my work about a quarter before one o'clock for three or four minutes—as I name beak I saw the prisoner coming from the parlour where I had been at week, with my coat under his arm, which had been hanging on the wail by the side of the bench—I did not know him—I asked what he wanted with it—he said a man over the way had sent him for it, and he would show me the man if I would let him go—I took my coat, and this plane dropped from it—I bad left it on the bench—this handkerchief was in the coat.





JOHN WHITE
 (policeman, B 262). I took the prisoner, and produce these articles.





RICHARD COSINS
 (police-sergeant, B 30). I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction at this Court, by the name of James Condon—(read—Convicted Aug., 1648, confined six months)—the prisoner in the person. 


GUILTY
. Aged 17.—


Transported for Seven Years.
