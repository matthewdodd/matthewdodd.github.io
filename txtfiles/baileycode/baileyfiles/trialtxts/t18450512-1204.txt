



1204. 




RICHARD BAKER. alias
 George Underwood

, was indicted

 for burglariously breaking and entering the dwelling-house of 



Fanny Stewart, about the hour of eleven in the night of the 
4th of May, with intent to steal, and stealing therein, 22 gold rings, value 4l. 8s.; 7 silver watches, 8l.; 2 caddy spoons and 6 tea-spoons, 12s.; 3 sugar-tongs, 15s.; 1 child's coral, 10s.; 1 shirt-stud, 1s.; 1 key, 1s.; 1 watch-ring, 1s.; and 1 seal, 10s.; her property: 2nd COUNT, for burglariously breaking out of the said dwelling-house:—also for stealing, on the 
15th of April, at Putney, 40 sovereigns, 80 half-sovereigns, and 40 half-crowns, the monies of the said 



Fanny Stewart, in her dwelling-house, to both which he pleaded





GUILTY
. Aged 17.—


Transported for Ten Years.

(John Wilson, of No. 90, High-street, Southwark, deposed to his good character.)

Before Mr. Recorder.
