





289. 
               
                  
                     THOMAS 
                     HAINE
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               embezzling
            .


                  THOMAS 
                  MATTHEW
               



            . I am clerk to Messrs. 
               
                  William and 
                        
                           
                              John 
                              Whitehouse
                           







            , carriers

. The prisoner was employed to drive one of their carts, he was entrusted to receive the carriage for goods, and he ought to bring it to me. On the 10th of August, I gave him a basket to take to Messrs. Welsh and Newall, Knight Rider-street, he was to bring me 1 l. 17 s. 9 d.
MR. 
               
                  JOHN 
                  WILSON NEWALL
               



            . I am in partnership with Mr. Welch, I paid 1 l. 17 s. 9 d. for carriage on the 6th of September, but cannot swear the prisoner is the person who received it, he gave me a receipt which I produce. The goods came on the 10th of August; I paid it to the person, who brought this note, (looking at it.)


                  THOMAS 
                  MATTHEWS
               



            . This is the note I gave the prisoner, on the 10th of August, it is written by me, they do not always receive the money upon delivery; hewas to keep the note till the money was paid; he told me on the first Saturday after the goods was delivered, that it was not paid. He was in our service till a fortnight ago, when he was apprehended; and said he had received this money, and lost five sovereigns out of his pocket coming from Beal's wharf.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 21.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
