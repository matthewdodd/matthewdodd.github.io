



1552. 




ROBERT HAYWARD
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
8th of June, 1 handkerchief, value 1s., the goods of 



James Townsend, from his person; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






JAMES TOWNSEND
. I am a tailor, and live at Lambeth. I went, on the 8th of June, to Greenwich fair—I had this handkerchief in my pocket—it

has my mark on it—I lost it at the fair—the officer told me of it, and brought back the prisoner with the handkerchief.





WILLIAM CLAREY
 (police-constable K 278.) I was on duty at Greenwich fair, and saw the prisoner and another standing behind the prosecutor—he took this handkerchief, and put it into his own trowsers—I seized him, and told the gentleman of it on the spot.





BENJAMIN HONE
 (police-constable N 39.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got at Mr. Clark's office (read) I know he is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 18.—


Transported for Seven Years.

Before Mr. Common Sergeant
