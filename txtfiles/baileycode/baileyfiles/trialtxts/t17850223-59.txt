






350. 
               
                  
                     GEORGE 
                     PATTERSON
                  



               
             and 
               
                  
                     JAMES 
                     DONOVAN
                  



               
             were indicted for 


               feloniously stealing, on the 14th day of January
 last, one deal drawer called a till, value 1 s. and seventy-two copper halfpence, value 3 s. and one shilling and sixpence in monies
            , the monies of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Oldham
                  




               
            .
The prisoners were taken in company together in the street, with the till in the hand of Patterson.
PRISONER PATTERSON's DEFENCE.
I picked up the till in the street, with the money in it.
BOTH 

               GUILTY
            .
Each to be 



               transported for seven years
            .
Tried by the first Middlesex Jury before Mr. RECORDER.
