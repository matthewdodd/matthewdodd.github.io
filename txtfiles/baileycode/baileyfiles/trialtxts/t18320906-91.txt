





1817. 
               
                  
                     JAMES 
                     JEFFERIES
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 15th of September
, 1 pair of stocks, value 18s.; 1 pair of dies, value 5s., and 3 taps, value 7s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Charles 
                     Gray
                  




               
            .


                  THOMAS 
                  CARR
               



            . I am a smith and engineer, in the service of Mr. 
               
                  Charles 
                  Gray
               



            , in New Park-street, Southwark


. On the 15th of September, 1831, the prisoner came to his shop to me, and said he came from Mr. Embleton, to borrow a pair of stocks and dies, as he had a job to do for Mr. Magnay; I knew Mr. Embleton to be a customer, and though the prisoner was a stranger, I let him have the articles, as I believed he came with a true story.
Prisoner. Q. Do you mean to say I asked you for these things? A. Yes, you asked for them - it was not my Mistress asked me.


                  LUKE 
                  EMBLETON
               



            . I am a smith. I know Mr. Gray, and have dealt with him; the prisoner worked for me about four years ago, but I did not authorize him to borrow these articles.


                  JOHN 
                  MILTON
               



            . I produce the stocks and dies, which were pawned with me on the 17th of September, 1831.


                  JOHN 
                  JARVIS
               



            . (Police-constable C 13). I took the prisoner on another charge.
Prisoner's Defence. I only borrowed them for work.



               GUILTY
            . - Aged 28. 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
