





1822. 
               
                  
                     HENRY 
                     BROWN
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 8th of July
, 4 frocks, value 4s.; 5 aprons, value 3s.; 2 pillow-cases, value 1s.; 3 shirts, value 2s.; 4 handkerchiefs, value 1s. 6d.; 1 cap, value 1s.; 1 collar, value 6d.; 1 table-cloth, value 1s., and 4 window-blinds, value 6d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     James 
                     Grant
                  




               
            .


                  ELIZABETH 
                  GRANT
               



            . I am the wife of James Grant; we live in King-street, Camden-town


. On the night of the 7th of July this linen was in the back garden, and at half-past one o'clock in the morning the Policeman alarmed me.


                  JAMES 
                  CARTER
               



            . I am a Policeman. About one o'clock in the morning I was on duty in King-street, and saw a bundle thrown over the wall; I saw the prisoner getting over the wall - I asked what he was about; he said he intended to have slept there - I took him in charge, and saw him drop this frock; I got over the wall - nobody was there.


                  WILLIAM 
                  LODWICK
               



            . I am a Policeman. I took up the bundle, which contained some of the things - this handkerchief was found round the prisoner's body, with other things, and another article in his hat.
Prisoner's Defence. I had had no food all day; the things laid under the wall, and I took them up.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 21. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
