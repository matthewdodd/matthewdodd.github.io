





949. 
               
                  
                     THOMAS 
                     MANSFIELD
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 9th of April
, 2 bit-halters, value 18s., and 1 crupper, value 2s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Charles 
                     Chamberlain
                  




               
            .


                  CHARLES 
                  CHAMBERLAIN
               



            . I live at Alridge


; I am a carman
, and drive hay and straw to town; I lost these two bit-halters and a crupper from my stable in my yard- I left them there on the night of the 9th of April, and missed them the next morning; the door had not been locked.


                  SAMUEL 
                  JOHNSON
               



            . I am a saddler, and live in Turnmill street. The prisoner came and offered two bit-halters for sale, for 4s.; I gave him no answer, and he went away - I informed the constable, who took him.


                  JOHN 
                  ROBINSON
               



            . I am the constable. Johnson told me, and I took the prisoner - he said he had brought up two horses to the Ram inn, in Smithfield, and was going to take them home to Hertfordshire; I said if he would go there I should be satisfied, but he would not; I was forced to call in another officer - we found this crupper on him.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I found the harness near Chigwell.



               GUILTY
            . Aged. 30.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
