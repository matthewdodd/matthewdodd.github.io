





1841. 
               
                  
                     GEORGE 
                     SICE
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 26th of July
, 1 watch, value 1l.; 1 pair of breaches, value 15s.; 1 pair of shoes, value 2s.; 1 shirt, value 5s.; and 1 pair of trousers, value 2s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           James 
                           Cox
                        



                     
                  ; and 2 coats, value 3l. 10s., and 1 gold seal, value 5s., the goods of 
                     
                        George 
                        Cox
                     



                  ; and 2 1/2 yards of woollen cloth, value 30s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           David 
                           Callaghan
                        



                     
                  , in the dwelling-house of 
                     
                        James 
                        Cox
                     



.


                  DAVID 
                  CALLAGHAN
               



            . I am a journeyman-tailor
, and work at Mr. Cox's, Little Russell-street, Bloomsbury


. The prisoner is a shoemaker
, and boarded in the house, but - did not lodge there - I missed this piece of cloth on the 26th of July; the prisoner had been there a good deal before - I left the cloth there on the Saturday night, and it was taken.


                  JOHN 
                  GANT
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, and live in London-street, Tottenham-court-road. I took in this coat on the 27th of July, from Kilerby.


                  BENJAMIN 
                  KILERBY
               



            . I am a tailor, and have known the prisoner about six months; I pawned a piece of cloth and a pair of breeches for him, but not this coat.


                  GEORGE 
                  MACKAY
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, and live in Grafton-street, Fitzroy-square. I have a remnant of cloth and a pair of breeches, which were pawned by Kilerby.


                  BENJAMIN 
                  KILERBY
               



            . These are what I pawned, by the desire of the prisoner - I got 25s. for them, and gave it to him.


                  DAVID 
                  CALLAGHAN
               



            . This cloth is my property.


                  JAMES 
                  COX
               



            . This watch and other articles are mine.


                  GEORGE 
                  COX
               



            . These articles are mine.
The prisoner put in a written Defence, stating that the property had been stolen by a boy named 
               
                  George 
                  Weston
               



            , in whose company he was.




               GUILTY (of stealing to the value of 25s.)
             Aged 18.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
