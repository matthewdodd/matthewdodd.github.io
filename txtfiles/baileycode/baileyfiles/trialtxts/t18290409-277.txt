                     SMITH alias 
THOMAS FOWLER SMITH

               stealing, on the 15th of March
, 36 lbs. weight of mustard, value 2l.; 36 lbs. weight of starch, value 1l. 12s. 9d.; 24 lbs. weight of packthread, value 17s.; 6lbs. weight of twine, value 5s. 6d.; 14 lbs weight of colour, value 14s.; 14 lbs. weight of pepper, value 1l. 3s.; 3 pints of blacking, value 3s.; 1000 nails, value 2l.; 2 tin boxes, value 2s.; 56 lbs. weight of soap, value 2l. 9s.; 1 box, value 2s; 1 chest, value 1s. 6d.; 2 casks, value 2s.; 80 lbs. weight of cloves, value 1l. 9s.; 14lbs. weight of carraway seeds, value 6s.; 4 1/2 lbs. weight of powder blue, value 6s. 9d.; 3 1/2 lbs. weight of Minerva green, value 7s.; 6 lbs; weight of Brunswick green, value 4s. 6d.; 5 1/2 lbs. weight of Oxford cotton, value 5s. 3d.; 8lbs. weight of rose pink, value 4s., and 3 bags, value 1s. 6d., the goods of 
                     
                        Edward 
                        Yates
                     



                   and another, his masters. - Also for stealing, on the 8th of March
, 7lbs. weight of nutmegs, value 2l. 2s.; 4 lbs. weight of galls, value 4s.; 4 lbs. weight of Italian juice, value 8s.; 5lbs. weight of gum arabic, value 7s.; 10 lbs. weight of capers, value 16s. 8d.; 7lbs. weight of arrow-root, value 5s. 3d.; 5 lbs. weight of almonds, value 9s.; 6 lbs. weight of tapioca, value 3s.; 23 lbs. weight of blue, value 1l. 9s.; 17 lbs. weight of ginger, value 19s. 6d.; 14 lbs. weight of sago, value 6s.; 14 lbs. weight of sal prunella, value 5s.; 8 lbs. weight of Dutch pink, value 4s.; 4 lbs. weight of crone yellow, value 8s.; 1 lb. weight of pepper, value 2s.; 4lbs. weight of red lead, value 8d.; 1 lb. weight of gum, value 3s.; 3 lbs. weight of isinglass, value 2l.; 6 bags,value 6s. 6d., and 1 chest value 1s. 6d., the goods of Edward Yates and another, his masters. - Also for stealing, on the 19th of March

                        Edward 
                        Yates

, 18 lbs. weight of soap, value 10s.; 3 gallons of oil, value 1l.; 30 lbs. weight of starch, value 1l. 1s., and one stone bottle, value 3s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Edward
