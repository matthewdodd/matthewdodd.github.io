



357. 




GEORGE DOUSE
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
17th of December, 2 coats, value 4l.; the goods of 



John Simons; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






JOHN SIMONS
. I live at Camden-wharf, Camden-town, and am a coalmerchant. The prisoner had been in my employ—on Monday last, the 17th of December, I had two coats—I saw them safe between tnree and four o'clock—I missed them about five o'clock—this is one of them (looking at it.)





JAMES MEALING
. I live with Mr. Redpath, a pawnbroker—this great coat was pawned that same evening about six o'clock by the prisoner.

Prisoner. Another man gave it me to pawn for him—I did not steal it.





JOSEPH SKIDMORE
. (police-constable S 160.) I apprehended the prisoner—he said he knew nothing of the coat.





JOSEPH KENDALL
. (police-constable S 98.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction from Mr. Clark's office—(read)—the prisoner it the man that was convicted.




GUILTY
. Aged 19.—


Transported for Seven Years.
