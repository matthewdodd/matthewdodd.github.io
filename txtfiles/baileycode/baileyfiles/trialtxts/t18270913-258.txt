






1844. 
               
                  JAMES 
                  MAGRATH
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 19th of August
, 1 handkerchief, value 2s., the goods of 
                     
                        James Edward 
                        Groom
                     




                  , from his person
            .
JAMES EDWARD GROOM. I was in St. Martin's-court


 between nine and ten o'clock on the 19th of August I had used my handkerchief a few minutes before - I heard a person call, "Stop that gentleman!" I stopped; put my hand to my pocket, and it was gone - I saw the prisoner in custody at the time.


                  JOHN 
                  BOSTON
               



            . I was in St. Martin's-court. I saw the prisoner and another lad - I saw the prisoner take the handkerchief from the gentleman's pocket; I called out, and took it from the prisoner.


                  JOHN 
                  GROOM
               



            . I came up and saw Boston with the prisoner; he gave me this handkerchief.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. This man came up, and said,"You have got that gentleman's handkerchief;" and he had it in his other hand at the time.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 18.




               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
