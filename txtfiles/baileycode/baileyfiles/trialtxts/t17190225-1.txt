







                     James 
                     Cringe 
                  



               
             , of St. Mary White-Chappel


, was indicted for 


               breaking open the House of 
                     
                        
                           Henry 
                           Mason 
                        




                      
                  , and taking thence a Flitch and half of Bacon and 150 Eggs, the Goods of the said Henry Mason
             on the third of February
 last. The Prosecutor deposed that he hearing a Noise about one a clock, rose, and found his House broke open, and that the Prisoner was taken a Hours after with the Goods. The Constable deposed, that Seeing a Light in a Shed, he went to it, and there found the Prisoner and two more with him cleaning the Bacon; they run up Stairs, and two of them jumpt out at the Window and got off, but the Prisoner was taken and confess'd where he stole it. He likewise own'd in Court that he stood by and saw the House broke open, but did not break it. The Jury brought him in 


               Guilty of Felony only
            . 


               Transportation
            .
