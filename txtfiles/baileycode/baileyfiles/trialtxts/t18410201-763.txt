



763. 




DAVID GOODMAN
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
14th of January, 1 handkerchief, value 4s., the goods of 



Martin Driscoll; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






JAMES DRISCOLL
. I am the son of Martin Driscoll, at West Ham. The prisoner lodged with us—I saw him open a drawer on the 14th of January, take out a handkerchief of my father's, put it into his pocket, and go out.





ELIZABETH DRISCOLL
. I am the prosecutor's wife. I charged the prisoner with stealing my handkerchief—he denied it, but after some time he told me he had it.





PETER M'QUILLEN
 (police-constable K 375.) I went after the prisoner and charged him with stealing a handkerchief—he said he knew nothing of a handkerchief—I was present when he made this statement in this deposition, and the Magistrate signed it—this is his writing—(read.)
The prisoner, on being called on for his defence says, "I wanted some money, and as Mrs. Driscoll was not at home, I took out of her drawer the silk handkerchief, bull did not intend to steal it—WILLIAM COTTON."

Prisoner. The prosecutor knows it was not taken with the intention of stealing it.





MARTIN DRISCOLL
. The prisoner denied it to me when I got ont of bed in my shirt—he certainly meant to steal it—my wife gave him in charge.





JOSEPH LAMPRELL
. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got at Mr. Clark's office—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 23.—


Transported for Seven Years.

Sixth Jury, before Mr. Sergeant Arabin.
