





1951. 
               
                  
                     EDWARD 
                     PAYNE
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 17th of March
, 1 kettle, value 2s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     James 
                     Davey
                  




               
            .
JAMES DAVEY. I live in Whitecross-street


. On the 17th of March, I had this kettle to repair; it was taken from my celler-window.


                  EDWARD 
                  HANDS
               



            . I am an officer. I took the prisoner on the 22nd of March; the prosecutor said he had lost a tea-kettle, and found the person who had bought it. I locked the prisoner up, and the next day he made his escape - I did not catch him till Monday week last.


                  BRIDGET 
                  HOWLEY
               



            . I bought this tea-kettle of the prisoner for 1s. - the prosecutor came, and I said I had bought it a good bargain of his brother, which I took the prisoner to be.
Prisoner. Q.Were there not two more lads with em? A. There were two outside.


                  PATRICK 
                  CORCORAN
               



            . I am an officer. I took the prisoner.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I was returning from the doctor's, and saw two lads with the kettle; they asked if I could tell them where to sell it; I went to the witness; some person there said I had stolen it; she said, "It don't much matter if it don't leak; I will give you 1s. for it."



               GUILTY
            . Aged 19. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
