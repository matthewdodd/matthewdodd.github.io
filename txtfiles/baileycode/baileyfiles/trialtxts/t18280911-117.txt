





1673. 
               
                  THOMAS 
                  GAPP
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 6th of September
, 150 lbs. of rope, value 30s., the goods of 
                     
                        James John 
                        Nicholls
                     




                  , the same being in a certain barge, in the navigable River Thames
            .
MR. PHILLIPS conducted the prosecution.
JAMES JOHN NICHOLLS. I am owner of the barge
, Waterloo. On the 6th of September she laid off the Inner Temple


; she was broken into, and 150 lbs. of rope taken away - I missed it on Saturday, the 6th, and saw part of it again on Monday in possession of the watchman - I know nothing of the prisoner; the cabindoor, which I had locked, was broken open - I have a private-mark on it, and can swear to it.


                  JOHN 
                  HILL
               



            . I am a waterman. On Saturday, the 6th of September, I bought a rope of the prisoner at Blackfriars'-bridge - Mr. Nicholls claimed it.


                  JOHN 
                  WILLIAM WARE
               



            . I am a waterman. I bought a rope of the prisoner on the 6th of September - it is good rope.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I keep a marine store shop in St. Saviour's. I bought on Saturday morning 1 cwt. 1 qr. 6 lbs. of old rope - this was in the middle of it; I sold the tare, and the rest I sold at different shops.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 27.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
