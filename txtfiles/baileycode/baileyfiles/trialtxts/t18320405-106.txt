





902. 
               
                  
                     HENRY 
                     DAVIS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 23rd of March
, 1 handkerchief, value 1s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           John Brown 
                           Nasmyth
                        




                     
                  , from his person
            .


                  JOHN 
                  BROWN NASMYTH
               



            . I was on Holborn-hill


 at seven o'clock in the evening on the 23rd of March - I had a handkerchief in my pocket; a person told me it was gone - I turned, and saw it on the ground, and the prisoner in custody close to it - there were several persons round.


                  WILLIAM 
                  HUISH WEBBER
               



            . I am clerk to a solicitor. I saw the prisoner, and another in company, following the prosecutor; I saw the prisoner take this handkerchief - I seized him, and he dropped it.


                  EDWARD 
                  COONEY
               



            . I was on Holborn-hill, and saw the prisoner take the handkerchief out of the gentleman's pocket; he had another boy with him.


                  SAMUEL 
                  DAVIS
               



            . I am an officer. I took the prisoner, and have the property.
Prisoner's Defence. I was walking up Holborn; a boy who was before me picked the pocket - I know nothing of it.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 17. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
