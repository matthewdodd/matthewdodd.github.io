





1691. 
               
                  
                     ANN 
                     COLLIS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the
1st of August
, 1 half-sovereign, 2 shillings, and 1 halfpenny
            , the monies of 
               
                  
                     Freme 
                     Franklin
                  




               
            .


                  FREME 
                  FRANKLIN
               



            . On the 1st of August I was in Globe-road


, about three o'clock in the morning; I found the prisoner's hand in my pocket, and held her till the Policeman came - she was taken to the station, and my half-sovereign found on her.
Prisoner. I was very much in liquor - the prosecutor asked me to go to a house with him; I said I had no house - he then pulled off his leather apron, said he had but one shilling, and he would give me that and his apron - I said I had a half-sovereign, which I got from a gentleman, and he said if I did not give him that he would give charge of me - the Policeman asked how much he had; he said 15s., but he could swear to half a sovereign; he at last swore to 12s. at Worship-street.


                  JAMES 
                  MULLINS
               



            . I am an officer. The prosecutor said the prisoner had robbed him of a half-sovereign and some silver - I asked her for the half-sovereign; she said she had not a quarter of a half-sovereign about her - I found on her this half-sovereign, wrapped in a bit of paper, with lines on it corresponding with a book which the prosecutor had in his pocket, and two shillings and a halfpenny.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 30. - 


               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
