



726. 




FRANCIS EYRES
 was indicted

 for stealing 1 cloak, value 1l.; the goods of 



Elizabeth Mabon; and that he bad been before convicted of felony.






ELIZABETH MABON
. I am a widow, and live in Seymour-place. I went to the prisoner's sister's house, on the 27th of Jan., and hung my cloak behind the door—I was there from ten o'clock in the morning till half-past nine at night—I then missed it—I went and told the prisoner's uncle and aunt of it—this is it.





WILLIAM CRUSH
. I am a pawnbroker. This cloak was pawned by the prisoner on the evening of the 27th of Jan.—he said it was his wife's.

Prisoner. It was from distress; I was out of work three months.





JOSEPH NIFTON
 (police-constable D 118.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got at Mr. Clark's office—(read.)





JOHN LIOMIN
. I was at the trial—the prisoner is the person then tried.




GUILTY
. Aged 24.—


Transported for Seven Years.
