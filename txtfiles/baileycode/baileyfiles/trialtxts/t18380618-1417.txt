



1417. 




THOMAS JOHNSON
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
7th of June, 1 handkerchief, value 6s., the goods of 



John Hancock; and that he had been previously convicted of felony.






CHARLES YEATMAN
. About a quarter past nine o'clock, on the 7th of June, I was standing in my employer's shop, Mr. John Hancock, in the Poultry—I saw the prisoner enter the shop, and take this silk handker chief from the side counter—he walked out with it—I followed him, and brought him back with it in his hand—it is my master's.





GEORGE CHENEY
. I am an officer. I took the prisoner and this hand kerchief.

Prisoner. I was in a state of starvation at the time.





WILLIAM WEBBER
. I am a watchman. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, from Mr. Clark's office—(read)—the prisoner is the man who was convicted.




GUILTY
. Aged 31.—


Transported for Seven Years.
