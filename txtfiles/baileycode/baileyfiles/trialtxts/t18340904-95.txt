





1311. 
               
                  WILLIAM 
                  BREACHER
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 31st of August
, 1 handkerchief, value 2s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  Joseph 
                  Haslehurst
               




            .


                  THOMAS 
                  ANDERSON
               



            . I am a watchman of St. Sepulchre - a little after ten o'clock on the night of the 21st of August, I saw the prosecutor walking up Skinner-street


 with another gentleman - I saw the prisoner and another person just behind the prosecutor - the prisoner and his companion parted - I took the prisoner, and found him tucking this handkerchief into his bosom - I took him up Skinner-street, and asked the prosecutor if he had lost any thing; he felt, and missed his handkerchief.


                  JOHN 
                  MARVIN
               



            . I was constable of the night - the prisoner was brought in with this handkerchief.
JOSEPH HASLEHURST. I am a chemist and druggist
. I was walking on, and the witness spoke to me - I felt, and missed my handkerchief from my right hand pocket - I had used it just before, and put it safe into my pocket inside.
Prisoner's Defence. I was coming up the street, and aboy gave me this handkerchief before the watchman's face- I put it safe in my breast.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 14. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
