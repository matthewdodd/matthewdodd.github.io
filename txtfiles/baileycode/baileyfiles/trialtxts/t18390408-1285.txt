




1285. 




RICHARD DYER
 was again indicted

for stealing, on 
15th of March, 4 spoons, value 4s.; and 1 pair of sugar tongs, value 6d.; the goods of 



Jane Lane; and that he had been previously convicted of felony.






JANE LANE
. I am a widow. The prisoner lodged in my house—I missed these spoons and tongs from a tea-chest down stairs—it was locked, but the key was in it—these are them.





WILLIAM HALL
. I searched the prisoner—he gave me the duplicated the spoons, which was sewn up in his small-clothes.





THOMAS BARFORD
. I am a pawnbroker. I took in these spoons of the prisoner on the 16th of March—I believe I gave him a half-crown, but I cannot swear.





JOHN PARNELL
. I am an officer. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's conviction—I got it at Mr. Clark's office—(read)—he is the man.

Prisoner. I was in distress—I intended to redeem them—Mrs. Lane said she would forgive me if I told her.




GUILTY
. Aged 31.—


Transported for 14 Years.
