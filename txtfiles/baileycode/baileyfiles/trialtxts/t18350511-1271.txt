



1271. 




SAMUEL MITCHELL
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
18th of April, 20 reams of paper, value 20l.; 20 reams of writing paper, value 20l.; 100 books, value 20l.; 20lbs. sealing wax, value 10l.; 100 yards of tape, value 10s.; 500 pens, value 1l.; and 1 inkstand, value 2l.; the goods of 

our Lord the King.—2nd COUNT, stating them to be the goods of



 Sir George Clark, Bart.—3rd COUNT, stating them to be the goods of 



George Arbuthnot, Esquire; to which he pleaded





GUILTY
. Aged 39.—


Transported for Seven Years.
(The prisoner received an excellent character.)
