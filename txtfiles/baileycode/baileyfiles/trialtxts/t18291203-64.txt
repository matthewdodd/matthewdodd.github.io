





OLD COURT. FRIDAY, DECEMBER 4.
Second Middlesex Jury - before Mr. Sergeant Arabin.
64. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM JOSEPH 
                     BANES
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 25th of October, 19 spoons, value 8l.; 1 pair of sugar-tongs, value 5s.; 10 forks, value 3l.; 6 knives, value 30s.; 1 time-piece, value 2l.; 20 books, value 40s.; 2 coats, value 20s.; 1 seal, value 5s.; 1 ring. value 5s.; 1 watch, value 20s.; 1 pipe, value 5l.; 2 polished stones, value 5s., and 1 trinket, value 5s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Thomas Hyde 
                     Villiers
                  




               
            , Esq.
, his master; to which he pleaded




               GUILTY
            . Aged 29. - 


               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
The prisoner was also indicted for forging a receipt for 3l. 3s. in the name of William Jameson, but no evidence being offered he was acquitted.
