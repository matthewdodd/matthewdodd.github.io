





115. 
               
                  
                     ELIZABETH 
                     READ
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 28th of January
, a coat, value 15 s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     James 
                     Pink
                  




               
            .


                  JAMES 
                  PINK
               



            . I am a carpenter
, and live at Homerton


, in the garret; the prisoner slept on the first floor. On the 28th of January, between seven and eight o'clock, my coat was safe; I went out, and missed it at ten at night - she absconded. On the 17th of November, I met her in Brick-lane, and told Gibbs, who took her. She said she had pawned my coat at Miller's, and torn the duplicate up.


                  GEORGE 
                  MOULTEN
               



            . I am an apprentice to Mr. Miller, of Kingsland-road. On the 28th of June, this coat was pawned in the name of 
               
                  Ann 
                  Tayler
               



            , Union-street, Kingsland-road; I did not take it in, but have often seen the prisoner at the shop.


                  ROBERT 
                  COUCH
               



            . I took her into custody. She said she stole the coat, and pawned it at Miller's.
(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 41.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
