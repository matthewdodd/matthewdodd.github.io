





1180. 
               
                  
                     DANIEL 
                     MACK
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 15th of September
, one watch, value 3 l.; one seal, value 6 d.; and one chain, value 1 d., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           John 
                           Mills
                        




                     
                  , from his person
            .


                  JOHN 
                  MILLS
               



            . I live in Cornwell-street, St. George's in the East. On the 16th of August, between five and six o'clock in the evening, I was in Cornwell-street


, returning from a walk, somebody came against me, snatched my watch from my fob, and went off with it. It was produced in three minutes.


                  TIMOTHY 
                  CALLAMAN
               



            . I live in Cow-lane, Shadwell. I was coming out of the Middlesex Society-school, and heard Mills calling Stop thief! my school-fellows and I followed the prisoner who was near Mr. Mills. I saw him throw the watch into a gutter, I picked it up and gave it to Mills.


                  WILLIAM 
                  HENRY HUNT
               



            . I am a constable. I secured the prisoner. Callaman said he was the man who threw the watch away.
(Property produced, and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 19.




               Transported for Life
            .
Fourth Middlesex Jury, before Mr. Common Sergeant.
