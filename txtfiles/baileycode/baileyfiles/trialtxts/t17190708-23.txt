








                     Edward 
                     Swinney 
                  



               
            , of St. Margarets Westminster


 was indicted upon two Indictments, one for 


               feloniously stealing a piece of Caffoy value 4 s.
             the Goods of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Abbot 
                  



                
             and 
               
                  
                     Thomas 
                     Dickson 
                  



                
            , on the 1st of June

 last. The other for 


               feloniously stealing a piece of Caffoy value 2 s.
             the Goods of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Whitehead 
                  



                
             and 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Bennet 
                  



                
            , the same Day. It appeared that the Prosecutors were Chairmen, and that the Prisoner being seen cutting the Lining of the Chairs was pursued and took; that he threw the Goods over the Rails in the Privy Garden, where they were found wrapt up together, and swore to by the Prosecutors. The Fact being fully proved and the Prisoner having nothing to say for himself, the Jury found him 

               Guilty
             of both Indictments.
