







                     Mary 
                     Shepherd 
                  



               
            , of St. Giles in the Fields


, was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing 3 Gold Rings, value 35 s. a Silver Buckle value 1 s. and a Girdle value 2 s.
             the Goods of 
               
                  
                     Simon 
                     Newman 
                  




                
            , on the 19th of December
 last. The Prosecutor deposed, that the Prisoner was his Servant
, and took the Goods out of his Drawer, and went away with them. 
               
                  Henry 
                  Mallard 
               



             deposed, that he went to seek after the Prisoner, and found her in Kent-Street, drunk: that she pull'd one of the Rings off her Finger, and told where she had sold the other two; which they found accordingly: that they brought her away with them, but when she came to St. Paul's Church-Yard she would go no further  without a Coach. The Jury found her 

               Guilty
            . 


               Transportation
            .
