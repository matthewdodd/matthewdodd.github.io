



1753. 




JOHN BAILEY
 was indicted

 for feloniously breaking and entering the dwelling-house of 



William Mulder, on the 
29th of May, at the hamlet of 
Mile-end New-town, and stealing therein 1 pair of trowsers, value 4s.; 1 waistcoat, value 1s.; 2 pairs of boots, value 1s.; 2 brooches; value 3s.; 1 necklace, value 1s.; 2 frocks, value 6s.; 2 pairs of shoes, value 1s. 6d.; 1 penknife, value 1s.; 1 medal, value 1s. 1 magnifying glass, value 6d.; 1 cannon, Value 6d.; 1 spoon, value 3s. 6d.; and 1 farthing; his property; to which he pleaded.
