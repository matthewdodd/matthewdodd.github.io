





1224. 
               
                  
                     HENRY 
                     JOHNSON
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 11th of August
, one shirt, value 3 s.; one coat, value 2 s., and one handkerchief, value 6 d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  John 
                  Kelchea
               




            .


                  JOHN 
                  KELCHEA
               



            . I am a labourer
. I was looking for work, and went to a public-house in Charles-street, St. Giles's, to ask for a lodging; the prisoner came up and said he knew where I could get one at an honest place, he took me to Church-street, St. Giles's


 - the woman of the house was out. I thought I would shave myself, put my bundle on the table while I shaved, and he went out with my shirt, coat, and handkerchief. He was stopped and the duplicates found on him.
Prisoner. Q. Did you see me take them out - A. I missed them the moment he was gone.


                  HENRY 
                  WILLIAM POWELL
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, and live in Bridge-road, Lambeth. On the 11th of August, the prisoner pawned a shirt for 3 s. - my duplicate was found on him.


                  PHILIP 
                  RILEY
               



            . On the 11th of August, I took him in charge, and found three shillings and the duplicate on him of a waistcoat, shirt, and jacket, all pawned that day.
(Shirt produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 30.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
Second Middlesex Jury, before Mr. Recorder.
