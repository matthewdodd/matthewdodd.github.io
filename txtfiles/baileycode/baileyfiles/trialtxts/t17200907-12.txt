







                     Thomas 
                     Higden 
                  



               
            , of St. Brides


, was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing 4 Brass Pulleys 50 Pound Weight of Lead and an Iron Case, in all to the value of 40 s.
             the Goods of the 
               
Governor and Company
 of the New-River-Water


            , on the 27th of July
 last. 
               
                  Thomas 
                  Austin 
               



             deposed, that he put the Goods up after 8 a Clock at Night, and the next Morning they were gone; that they were afterwards found on the Prisoner. 
               
                  James 
                  Tuttle 
               



             deposed, that the Prisoner brought 2 Pulleys to his Shop to sell, went for two more, at Night brought Iron and Lead, whereupon he stopt him. The Goods were produc'd in Court and Swore by Mr. Witherell to be the Company's. The Prisoner deny'd the Fact, but called none to his Reputation. The Jury found him 


               Guilty to the value of 10 d
            . 


               Transportation
            .
