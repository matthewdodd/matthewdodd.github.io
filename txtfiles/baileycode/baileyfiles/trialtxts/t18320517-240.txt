





1367. 
               
                  MARY 
                  BIGNEY
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 28th of April
, 1 quilt, value 4s.; 1 sheet, value 1s.; 1 flat-iron, value 6d.; 1 pair of bellows, value 10d.; 1 saucepan, value 2s., and 1 frying-pan, value 1s. 6d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Elizabeth 
                     Pearce
                  




               
            .


                  ELIZABETH 
                  PEARCE
               



            . I am single
, and live in Payne's-buildings


. On the 26th of April the prisoner came and hired a back room, furnished, at 3s. 6d. a week - she said that she had a husband; I gave her clean sheets, and a pillow-case; I saw her go out in about five minutes - I followed her to a pawnbroker's; she returned in a few minutes, and told my girl she was not coming back till the Friday, as her daughter was very ill - on the 28th of April she came again, and I gave her a washing-tub; she slept two nights in the room.


                  RICHARD 
                  CHARLES
               



             MARSH. I live with a pawnbroker. I have a sheet and flat-iron, which were pawned by the prisoner on the 26th and 27th of April.


                  SAMUEL 
                  THOMAS KNAPP
               



            . I have a pair of bellows, pawned with me on the 28th of April.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. My husband said he would give me the money to redeem them.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 44. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
