






Third Middlesex Jury - before Mr. Common Sergeant.
1012. 
               
                  JOSEPH 
                  CANNON
               




             and 
               
                  JOHN 
                  LANE
               




             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 1st of April
, 2 lbs. weight of cheese, value 1s. 4d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  Christopher 
                  Tutin
               




            .
WILLIAM WHITTINGHAM. I am an officer. On the 1st of April I saw the two prisoners together in the Strand, they passed the window of Mr. Christopher Tutin's shop, in the Strand


 - he is a cheesemonger
. I saw Cannon take a piece of cheese, which he gave to Lane; he passed the shop, turned short round, and took another piece, which he gave to Lane, who put them both into his apron, and wrapped it round his body - I took them both.
Prisoner LANE. He took us into the shop, and asked how many pieces of cheese they had lost, they said one. Witness. They looked afterwards, and said there were two.
CANNON - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 16.
LANE - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 17.





               Transported for Seven Years
            .
