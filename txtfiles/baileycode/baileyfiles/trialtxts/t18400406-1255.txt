




1255. 




CHARLOTTE EVANS
 and 




FRANCES GREEN
 were indicted for

 stealing, on the 
28th of March, 1 quilt, value 4s.; 2 blankets, value 8s.; 1 tea-kettle, value 3s.; 1 sheet, value 5s.; and 1 pillow-case, value 1s.; the goods of 



Susannah Lane.






SUSANNAH LANE
. I am a widow, living in Mary-street, Hampstead-road. On the 28th of March Evans took a furnished lodging at my house

as a married woman, but when she came she brought Green as her sister—I have lost the articles stated—these are them—(looking at them.)





JOHN WELLS
. I am a pawnbroker, living in Adam's-row, Hampstead-road. This tea-kettle I took in of Evans, and the blankets and sheets were taken in of Green, on the 28th of March, by my young man in my presence.

Evans's Defence. I did it through distress.

EVANS—


GUILTY
. Aged 19.

GREEN—


GUILTY
. Aged 30.




Transported for Seven Years.
(There was another indictment against Evans.)
