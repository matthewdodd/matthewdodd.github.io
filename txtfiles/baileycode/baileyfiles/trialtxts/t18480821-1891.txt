




1891. 




THOMAS GLENMAN
, and 




WILLIAM M'QUEEN



, stealing 1 shawl, value 1l.; the goods of 



Thomas Jackson: Glenman having been before convicted.






THOMAS JACKSON
. I live at Hedge-row, Islington—on 18th July I saw a shawl safe on my door-post—I missed it in about a quarter of an hour—this is it (produced).





WILLIAM HIGGINS
. I live at Pleasant-place, Lower-road—I saw the prisoners loitering about Mr. Jackson's shop—they sat down on the step of the next door—I next saw Glenman pass this shawl to M'Queen—they went different ways.





THOMAS MORRIS HARVEY
. I am a pawnbroker—I took the shawl in on the next day.





PETER TERRITT
 (policeman). I produce a certificate—(read—Thomas Glenning, convicted Sept. 1846, and confined six months)—Glenman is the man.

GLENMAN—


GUILTY
. ** Aged 17.—


Transported for Seven Years.


M'QUEEN—


GUILTY
. ** Aged 17.—



Confined One Year.
