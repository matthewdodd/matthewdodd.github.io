



579. 




JAMES BROWN



, stealing 1 handkerchief, value 2s. 6d.; the goods of 




William Amery, from his person; having been before convicted.






WILLIAM AMERY
. I am captain of the barge Worsley, in the London Docks. On 14th Jan., at eight o'clock in the evening, I was in Wellington-street, Southwark—I felt something, missed my handkerchief, and saw the prisoner crossing the road close to me—he put his hand in his breast—I collared him, and told him he had got my handkerchief—he denied it, and, while struggling to get away, threw it into the street—I picked it up, and. gave him in charge—there was no one else near enough to have taken it.





JOHN WESTMORELAND
 (policeman, M 233). I took the prisoner, and produce the handkerchief.





JAMES HENDERSON
 (policeman, L 47). I produce a certificate of the prisoner's conviction)—(read—Convicted July, 1848, of stealing a handkerchief, Confined six months)—I was present—he is the person.




GUILTY
.* Aged 24.—


Transported for Ten Years
,

Before Mr. Baron Alderson,
