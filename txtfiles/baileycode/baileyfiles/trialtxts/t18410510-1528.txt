




1528. 




THOMAS HARPER
 and 




HENRY WOOD
 were indicted for

stealing, on the 
5th of May, 2 1/2 lbs. weight of ham, value 2s., the goods of 



James Rand; and that Harper had been before convicted of felony.






SAMUEL ATTERSALL
. I am shop-boy to James Rand, who lives in Dor-set-street. About eleven o'clock in the morning of the 5th of May the prisoners came in—they asked if I had any potatoes—I showed them some—they laid they were not enough—they asked me to give them a piece of pork or cheese—I said I could not—they went out, and as Harper went out he took a bit of ham off the counter—I followed them—they went into Margaret James's shop—I went and asked Harper for the ham—he denied having it—Wood said, "Here it is"—he unbuttoned his jacket, and gave it me.

Harper. Jem McCarthy took it. Witness. Jem M'Carthy was outside.





MARGARET JAMES
. The prisoners came into my shop, and asked for some cake—the bacon dropped—I said, "Bacon fialls"—Wood picked it up, and put it under his jacket.

Harper's Defence. This boy said, "I have got a halfpenny, I will go for a halfpenny worth of potatoes; "I saw Jem M'Carthy take a piece of ham, and give it to Wood, who put it under his jacket.





SAMUEL COPPING
 (police-constable P 97.) I produce a certificate of Harper's former conviction, which I got from the Clerk of the Peace of Surrey—(read)—he is the person.

HARPER—


GUILTY
.* Aged 15.—


Transported for Seven Years.—Convict Ship.

WOOD—


GUILTY
. Aged 16.—



Confined One Month.
