





835. 
               
                  
                     MARY 
                     TREDDER
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing, on the 23d of October
, a silver table-spoon, value 10s
            , the property of 
               
                  
                     Edward 
                     Cockerton
                  




               
            .
EDWARD COCKERTON sworn. - The prisoner was a chairwoman
 to me; I live at No. 29, Bread-street-hill


: On the 23d of October I missed a tablespoon; the prisoner was asked if she had seen it, and she denied it, and on the Monday following I found it at Mr. Watson's, a pawnbroker, in Watling-street.


                  DAVID 
                  WATSON
               



             sworn. - I am a pawnbroker; I took in a table-spoon from the prisoner on the 23d of October; I lent her 8s. upon it; I am sure she is the same person. - (The spoon produced, and identified by the prosecutor.)
Prisoner's defence. I asked Mrs. Cockerton to lend me a little money, and she said she had none unknown to her husband, and she lent me the spoon till Monday.
Q. (To Cockerton.) Was Mrs. Cockerton without money? - A. No, I am an oilman, and in a retail shop; it is impossible to be without money.
The prisoner called three witnesses, who gave her a good character. 

               GUILTY
            .




               Transported for seven years
            .
London Jury, before Mr. Common Serjeant.
