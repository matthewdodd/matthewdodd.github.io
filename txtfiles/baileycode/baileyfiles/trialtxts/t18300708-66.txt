





1325. 
               
                  
                     JAMES 
                     WALKER
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 8th of June
, 24 yards of muslin, value 3s., the goods of 
                     
                        John 
                        Middleton
                     




                   and another; and that he had been previously convicted of felony
            .



                  JOHN 
                  MIDDLETON
               



            . I keep a muslin warehouse
, in partnership with my brother, in Milk-street, Cheapside


. The prisoner was a stranger; our street door is frequently open. On the 28th of January, about two o'clock in the afternoon, I saw him run out of the warehouse with a piece of muslin - I ran out, caught him, and took it from under his coat, without losing sight of him.


                  JOHN 
                  KIRBY
               



            . I took the prisoner into custody - he did not deny the charge.


                  WILLIAM 
                  JOYCE
               



            . I apprehended the prisoner on the 6th of May, for stealing a ream of paper, for which he was tried; I produce a certificate of his former conviction -(read.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 23. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
