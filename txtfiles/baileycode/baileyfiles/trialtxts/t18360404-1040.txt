



1040. 




WILLIAM HIGGINSON
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
7th of April, 1lb. of bacon, value 7d., the goods of 



Joseph Russell.






JOSEPH RUSSELL
. I am a cheesemonger, and live in High-street, Shadwell. On the 7th of April, about ten o'clock in the day, the prisoner passed my window, and put his hand on the shopboard with his apron own it—a person said he bad taken a bit of bacon—I ran after him, and caught him with it in his hand, four or five yards off—I gave him in charge.

Prisoner's Defence. A boy came running by me, and threw the bacon into the road, and ran off—Mr. Russell laid hold of me—a gentleman said, "It is not him, the boy is gone on"—he immediately said he would make me pay for it.

MR. RUSSELL. It is not tune—he had the bacon is his hand when I collared him, and he threw it the road.




GUILTY
.* Aged 16—


Transported for Seven Years.
