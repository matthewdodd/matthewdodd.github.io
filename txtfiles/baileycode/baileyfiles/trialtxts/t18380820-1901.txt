




1901. 




GEORGE CUTLER
 and 




JOHN DESMOND
 were indicted for

 stealing, on the 
24th of July, I looking-glass and stand value 20s., the goods of 



Thomas Evelyn Jones.






THOMAS EVELYN JONES
. I keep a shop in Woburn Buildings, St. Pancras. I lost a glass from the room behind the shop on the 24th of July—this is it—(looking at one.)





REUBEN RICHARDSON
. I am a baker, and live in Woburn Buildings. About eleven o'clock I saw the two prisoners sitting on a step near the prosecutor's, and then I saw them go into the shop—they were in some time, aid came out-Desmond had the glass, and the other had an apron ready to cover it—they gave it to a bigger boy, not in custody—I followed them down the New Road, and took them.





RICHARD DIXON
. I went after the other boy with the glass—I got it, bill the boy got away from me.

CUTLER—


GUILTY
. Aged 17.—



Confined Three Months.

DESMOND*—


GUILTY
. Aged 15.—


Transported for Seven Years.
