





 37. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     ANDERSON
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 18th of February
, 1 coat, value 30s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  Robert 
                  Keate
               



            ., Esq.


2ND COUNT, stating it to be the property of 
               
                  Samuel 
                  Lumbers
               



            .


                  SAMUEL 
                  LUMBERS
               



            . I live with Mr. 
               
                  Robert 
                  Keate
               



             as coachman
 - his stable is in Market-street


. On the 18th of February I came down from my own room over the stable, and went for some butter - I returned in about five minutes, and met the prisoner about fifteen yards from the stable door, with the box-coat on his shoulder; I asked where he was going with it - he said the servant sent him for it; I collared him, and took him back - it is my master's coat, but he said if I lost it I must make it good.


                  WILLIAM 
                  HACK
               



            . This is the coat - I took the prisoner.
Prisoner's Defence. I saw a coachman, who told me to take the coach to the stand.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 16. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
