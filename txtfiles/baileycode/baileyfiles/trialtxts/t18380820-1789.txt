



1789. 




WILLIAM WARD
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
14th of July, 1 hand kerchief, value 3s., the goods of 



George Pardoe, from his person.






GEORGE PARDOE
. I live in Russell-square. On the 14th of July I was

passing near St. Bartholomew's Hospital—I received information—I felt my pocket, and missed my handkerchief—this is it—(looking at it.)





THOMAS HOOKER
. I live at No. 14 Ward, Christ's Hospital, I was walking near the Hospital on this 14th of July, and saw the prisoner take the handkerchief out of the gentleman's pocket—I gave information—I am sure he is the boy.





JAMES FALLOW
. I live in St. Pancras. I saw (he prisoner running near the hospital square—I took him, with this handkerchief—the prosecutor came up, and identified it.




GUILTY
. Aged 13.—


Transported for Ten Years.
