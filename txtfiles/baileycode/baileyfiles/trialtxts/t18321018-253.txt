





2462. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     McDONALD
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 25th of September
, 2 seals, value 7s.; 1 watch-key, value 6d.; 1 chain, value 18d.; 1 purse, value 2s.; 1 tooth-pick, value 2s., and 1 tinder-box, value 1s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Cross
                  




               
            .


                  WILLIAM 
                  CROSS
               



            . I am a waiter
, and live in the Haymarket


. These things were lost from my bed-room; the prisoner was at work there as a bricklayer's labourer
 - I missed these and various other articles - suspicion fell on the prisoner, and he was taken.
Cross-examined by MR. BARRY. Q. Had you left the door open? A. The prisoner was at work in the room.


                  WILLIAM 
                  ROGERS
               



            . I am a bricklayer. The prisoner was my labourer; the things were lost, and I had suspicion of him; we were at work up stairs at the top of the house.
Cross-examined. Q. How long has he worked for you? A. Only two days.


                  JOHN 
                  HILL
               



            . I am a constable; I took the prisoner in Whitecross-street, on the 27th of September; I found this purse, two seals, a key, a German tinder-box, and a toothpick, on him.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I am innocent of this - I picked it up outside the door; I exposed it publicly in the taproom, and the men advised me to keep it to see if it was advertised.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 32. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
