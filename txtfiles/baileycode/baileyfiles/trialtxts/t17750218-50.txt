





235. (2d M.) 
               
                  ANN, the wife
 of 
                     
                        ROBERT
                         PETTIN
                     







            , was indicted for 


               stealing a scarlet cloak, value eight shillings, two linen shifts, value five shillings, four linen handkerchiefs, value six-pence, two muslin handkerchiefs, value four-pence, and six linen clouts, value sixpence
            , the property of 
               
                  
                     Dorothy
                      Olive
                  




               
            , widow
, January 13
. ||


                  Dorothy
                   Olive
               



            . The prisoner came into my house about five or six in the evening, about the time I lost my goods, I suspected she had taken the things, I apprehended her, and she confessed she had taken them, and directed me to the place where she had pawned them, I found them there, but the pawnbroker could not be sure as to the person that pawned them.
Prisoner's Defence.
I leave myself to the mercy of the court.




               Guilty of stealing to the value of ten-pence
            .
