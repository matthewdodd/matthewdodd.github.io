





1193. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     BENWELL
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 26th of May
, two sheets, value 6 s.; three blankets, value 7 s.; one counterpane, value 2 s.; one bolster, value 3 s.; two pillow-cases, value 1 s.; one looking-glass, value 30 s.; one candlestick, value 1 s., and one decanter, value 1 s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Jane 
                           Gould
                        




                     
                  , in a lodging room
            .


                  ELIZA 
                  GOULD
               



            . I am a daughter of 
               
                  Jane 
                  Gould
               



            , who lives in Belt-street, Ratcliffe


. On the 26th of May I let the prisoner the front parlour furnished - these things were let with it. He left it on the 1st of June without notice; I found all this property gone; I saw no more of him till he was liberated from here last Sessions, and then taken I found duplicates of the things in the bureau.


                  WILLIAM 
                  ANDERTON
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, and live in Cannon-street-road. I have a bolster and two pillows pawned on the 27th of May, in the name of 
               
                  James 
                  Barnes
               



            , Morgan-street. I believe it is the prisoner.


                  THOMAS 
                  PIKE
               



            . I took the prisoner into custody on another charge.
Prisoner's Defence. I was distressed, and got Barnes to pawn them.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 21.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
Fourth Middlesex Jury, before J. Vaillant, Esq.
