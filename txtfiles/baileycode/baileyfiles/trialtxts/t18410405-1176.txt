



1176. 




EDWARD BURNS
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
5th of April, I chair, value 1l. 10s., the goods of 



William Henry Hammond; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






JOHN WARREN
. I live in Chancery-lane, and am foreman to William Henry Hammond, upholsterer, of Bell-yard. On Monday, the 5th of April, about eleven o'clock, I missed a chair—I went out, and pursued the prisoner to St. Giles's church—I gave him in charge to a policemen with the chair, which is my master's—he was walking with it the whole way, and I following him till I got a policeman.





JOHN HOOPER WELLS
. I live at Mr. Hammond's. I saw the prisoner on the other side of the way, in Bell-yard, with the chair on his shoulder—I knew it by the ticket on it—I went and called the foreman.

Prisoner. How do you know me? Witness. I saw your face, and swear to you.

Prisoner's Defence. A man in Portugal-street gave it me to carry for

him, he told me to follow him to Holborn, and wait at a public-house for him.





JOHN ARCHER
. I am a policeman. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction—(read)—he is the man.




GUILTY
. Aged 30.—


Transported for Seven Years.
