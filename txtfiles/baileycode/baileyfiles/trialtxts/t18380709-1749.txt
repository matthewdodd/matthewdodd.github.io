



1749. 




JOSEPH CLARKE
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
4th of July, 3 sovereigns, 2 half-sovereigns, 15 crowns, 859 half-crowns, 1639 shillings, 435 sixpences, and 84 fourpenny-pieces, the monies of 



William Gurney, his master—also, on the 
3rd of July, 1 shilling, the monies of the said William Gurney, his master; to which he pleaded





GUILTY
. Aged 26.—


Transported for Seven Years.
