





1481. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     KEYTON
                  




               
             was (again) indicted for 


               stealing, on the 18th of October
, two coats, value 5l.; five shirts, value 20s.; five pair of pantaloons, value 2l.; five waistcoats, value 20s.; ten handkerchiefs, value 1l.; three pair of stockings, value 5s.; two pair of gloves, value 2s., and one pair of shoes, value 5s., the goods of 
                     
                        John 
                        Horman
                     




                  , in the dwelling-house of George Banks
            .
CHRISTOPHER SEAMAN. I stopped the prisoner, with these clothes, at the sugar-house - He had them in a bundle.
(Property produced and sworn to.)
GUILTY. Aged 26.
Of 


               Stealing, but not in a Dwelling-house
            .




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
London Jury, before Mr. Recorder.
