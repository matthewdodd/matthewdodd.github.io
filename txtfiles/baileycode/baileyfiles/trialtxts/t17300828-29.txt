







                     Robert 
                     Fenton 
                  



               
            , of St. Bennet Sherehog


, was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing 507 Pistoles, French and Spanish, in the Dwelling-House
             of 
               
                  
                     Richard
                      Chancey
                  



               
            , sen.
 and jun. the 3d of June
 last.
The Prosecutor depos'd, He having Advice that the Money was sent by the Carrier, sent the Prisoner to Blossom's-Inn in Laurence-Lane, but could not himself say as to his receiving it.
Chancey Townsend depos'd, That the Prisoner did bring the Money home, and he, this Evidence, counted it, took the Silver from the Gold, and put it into a Drawer in the Compting House, but never saw it after, till he took the Prisoner at Harwich; that he found him at Harwich in Bed, with 480 Pistoles in his Breeches, which lay under his Head; and that he own'd that he had taken the Money, and his Confession was read in Court. The Prisoner having nothing to say in his Defence, the Jury found him 

               Guilty
             of the Indictment.
