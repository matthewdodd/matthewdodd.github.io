





1251. 
               
                  ISAAC 
                  JACOBS
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 1st of May
, 1 handkerchief, value 2s., the goods of 
                     
                        John 
                        Wood
                     



                  , from the person of Jane his wife
            .


                  JANE 
                  WOOD
               



            . I am the wife
 of John Wood. I was in Church-row, Whitechapel


 on the 1st of May, about half-past nine o'clock at night, the prisoner came and took this handkerchief out of my basket. I was running at the time, and collared him immediately, he begged of me to let him go.
Cross-examined by Mr. BARRY. Q. Where was this? A. Near a sugar-warehouse - there were some ballan-singers there - I was running past, my handkerchief was picked up - I lost two sixpences from my basket.


                  ROBERT 
                  COMBER
               



            . I heard a cry of Stop thief! the prisoner was running - he had got from the prosecutrix. I collared him and took him to the watch-house.


                  THOMAS 
                  POWELL
               



            . I saw a gentleman pick up the handkerchief. I shewed it to the prosecutrix and took it to the watch-house. I returned and saw something shining on the ground. and found two sixpences - that was between nine and ten o'clock at night.


                  THOMAS 
                  HAILES
               



            . I heard a cry of Stop thief! and found the prisoner in Comber's hands.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I heard an alarm and was stopped.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 20.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
