








                     Robert 
                     Davis 
                  



               
            , of Islington


, was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing 2 Cows, value 5 l.
             the Property of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Middleton 
                  



                
            , the first of April

 last .
He was likewise indicted a 2d time for 


               feloniously stealing 3 Cows, value 4 l. 10 s.
             the Goods of 
               
                  
                     Richard 
                     Hoare 
                  



                
             , the 1st of April

 last.


                  William 
                  Middleton 
               



             depos'd, That he lost his Cows out of his Ground at Wilsdon



 , and found them in the possession of the Prisoner in Tibal's-Lane , in a Field; that the Prisoner goes by the Name of the New-Farmer there, having lately taken a little spot of Ground about 2 or 3 Acres, in which he had 6 Cows, 2 of them were stolen from him, and 3 from Mr. Hoare, but whose the other was he could not tell.
Both the Prosecutors swore the Cows to be theirs, and that they were taken out of their Grounds. They added, That before the Justice, the Prisoner pretended he bought the Cows upon the Road the 27th of March, whereas they were not lost till the 1st of April; both the Facts being plainly prov'd, the Jury found him 

               guilty
             of both Indictments.
