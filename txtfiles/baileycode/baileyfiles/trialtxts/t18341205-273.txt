



273. 




MARY ANN PANTONY
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
5th of November, 2 gowns, value 12s.; 5 napkins, value 3s.; 1 sheet, value 1s.; 2 aprons, value, 1s.; 1 handkerchief, value 6d.; the goods of 



Maria Slape.






MARIA SLAPE
. I am single, and have lived servant to Mr. Thomas for two years. The prisoner was a char-woman there—on the 25th of October, I went into the hospital, and was there five weeks—I came out in November—I had left my clothes all safe in my box, but it was not locked—on returning home I found every thing gone out of my box—I missed all the articles stated in the indictment—the prisoner took my place during my absence.





GEORGE CLARKSON
. I am a pawnbroker, and live in Clark's-place, Islington. I have a gown, five napkins, one sheet, and two aprons, pawned by the prisoner on the 2nd, 28th, and 30th of October.





RICHARD CHARLES
. I am a pawnbroker, I have a gown and handkerchief pawned by the prisoner.





WILLIAM HARRIS
. I am a policeman. I apprehended her 1st of December, and found two duplicates on her.
(Property produced and sworn to.)

Prisoner. I throw myself on the mercy of the Court—I was very much distressed.




GUILTY
. Aged 49.—


Transported for Seven Years.
