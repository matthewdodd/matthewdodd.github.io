BENTLEM DOUGLAS
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
2nd of March, 1 pillow-case, value 6d.; 1 table-cloth, value 6d.; 1 scarf, value 5s.; and 4 spoons, value 7s. 6d.; the goods of 



Mary Douglas.






MARY DOUGLAS
. I am a widow, and live in Albion-street, Waterloo-town, Bethnal-green. The prisoner is my son—I have three boys and one girl—the prisoner is the eldest—he lived at home with me—on the 2nd of March I missed the articles stated in the indictment—I spoke to him about them—he said he had taken them away because he had got into bad company—the property was found at the pawnbroker's.





WILLIAM RAWLINS
. I live with Mr. Bradley, a pawnbroker, in Cable-street I produce a scarf, pawned on the 4th of March, in the name of John Douglas, I believe, by the prisoner.





WILLIAM SOWERBY
. I am a pawnbroker. I have a table-cloth and pillow-case, pawned in the name of John Douglas, on the 2nd of March.





JAMES GIEL
. I am a pawnbroker. I have four tea-spoons, pawned in the name of Bentlem Douglas, but I did not take them in—it was on the 8th of March.





CHARLES JUTSUM
. I am a pawnbroker in Whitechapel-road. I have a brooch pawned for 1s. by the prisoner.






JACOB WOLFF
. I am a policeman. I took the prisoner into custody.

Prisoner. I hope you will send me to the Penitentiary.




GUILTY
.* Aged 13.—


Transported for Seven Years.
