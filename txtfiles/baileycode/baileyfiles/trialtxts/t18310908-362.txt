





1889. 
               
                  ANN 
                  WILLS
               




             was again indicted for 


               stealing, on the 25th of August
, 1 spoon, value 2s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  Silas 
                  Boreham
               




            .


                  SILAS 
                  BOREHAM
               



            . I live in South-street, Manchester-square, and am a butcher
 - the prisoner was in the habit of keeping houses for gentlemen. On the 25th of August she came to me, and said Mrs. Allen had got a few friends at No. 45, Manchester-street, and asked me to lend her a table-spoon - I said I had no table-spoon, I had an odd teaspoon, which she might have; she took it, and said she would return it in the evening - it was then about half-past eleven o'clock.


                  WILLIAM 
                  FOWLE
               



            . This spoon was pawned with the others.(Property produced and sworn to.)

Prisoner's Defence. They were not pawned with an intent to defraud.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 38.




               Transported for Seven Years
             for each offence.
There was another indictment against the prisoner.
