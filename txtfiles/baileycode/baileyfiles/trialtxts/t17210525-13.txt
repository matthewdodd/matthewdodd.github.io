







                     Thomas 
                     Pearce 
                  



               
            , of St. Botolph without Aldersgate


, was indicted for 


               privately stealing a Brass Porridge pot value 7 s. in the Shop of 
                     
                        
                           William 
                           Goodman 
                        




, on the 12th of this Instant May
. The prosecutor deposed, that he lost his Pot from his shop Window; that he did not see the Prisoner take it, but saw it in his Arms. 
               
                  Thomas 
                  Forster 
               



             deposed that he saw the Prisoner take the Porridge Pot off the Prosecutor's Shop Window; that he told him of it, followed and took the Prisoner with the Goods upon him. 
               
                  Thomas 
                  Holden 
               



             deposed that he caught the Prisoner with the Pot upon him. The Prisoner said that he was very much in Drink, but knew nothing at all of it. The Jury considering the matter, found him 


               Guilty to the value of 10 d.
            



               Transportation
            .
