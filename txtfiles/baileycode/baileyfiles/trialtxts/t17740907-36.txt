





598. (M.) 
               
                  
                     MARY
                      ANN SEARS
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing a brown silk gown, value 5 s. a white silk gown, value 5 s. two dimity petticoats, value 2 s. a white muslin cloak, value 5 s. and a linen sheet
            , the property of 
               
                  
                     Jane
                      Wilkinson
                  




               
            , spinster
, Aug. 11th
. ||


                  Jane
                   Wilkinson
               



            . I live in Gerard-street


. On the 11th of August I was out of my house from eleven till two; on my return I missed the things mentioned in the indictment (repeating them); I found the things at the pawnbrokers.


                  Robert
                   Minns
               



            . The prisoner brought the sheet, muslin cloak and apron to pawn with me on the 11th of August.
John Priestman. I am pawnbroker; I received two gowns and petticoats of the prisoner. (The goods were produced in Court and deposed to by the prosecutrix.)
Prisoner's Defence.
A woman I never saw before met me at the top of the Haymarket, and sent me to pawn them.



               Guilty
            . 


               T
            .
