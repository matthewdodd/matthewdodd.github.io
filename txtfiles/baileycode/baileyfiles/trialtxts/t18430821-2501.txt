



2501. 




ANN LACEY
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
6th of July, 1 shawl, value 7s., the goods of 



Ann May; and that she had been before convicted of felony.







ANN MAY
. I live in Union-court, Westminster. On the 6th of July I was asleep, and was awoke at two or three o'clock—I missed this shawl, looked out of the window, and saw the prisoner put it under her arm—she was going away with it.





BENJAMIN BAKER
. I am a pawnbroker. The prisoner pledged this shawl on the 6th of July.

Prisoner's Defence. I never saw the shawl.





ROBERT SUTTLE
 (police-constable B 97.) I produce a certificate of the prisoners former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clark's office—(read)—she is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 36.—


Transported for Seven Years.
