





Fourth Middlesex Jury - Before Mr. Common Sergeant.
1820. 
               
                  HENRY 
                  CLAYTON
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 1st of August
, 1 watch, value 4l., 1 seal, value 6d., and 1 key, value 1d., the good of 
                     
                        William 
                        Gray
                     




                  , from his person
            .
WILLIAM GRAY. I am a ship-carpenter
. On the 1st of August I was standing at the end of Field-alley, Shadwell-market


, speaking to a ship-mate; I found the prisoner before me, who said some words which were unintelligible - he then made a grasp and took my watch; I seized him, but he got away - we pursued, and about fifteen yards before we came up with him, I heard something fall on the ground; we did not stop, but pursued and took him - we brought him back to where I heard something fall, and it was my watch.


                  JOHN 
                  SKUES
               



            . I am a watchman. On the morning of the 1st of August I took the prisoner from Gray; he took me to the place, and there was this watch, I saw it picked up.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I was coming up Cox's-lane, and heard a cry of Stop thief! this person caught hold of me.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 22.




               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
