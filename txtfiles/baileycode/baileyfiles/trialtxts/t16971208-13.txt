







                  William 
                  Hudson alias 
Philips




            , of the City of London, Labourer
, was Indicted for 


               Feloniously Marrying two Wives; The first 
                     
                        Mary 
                        Rogers
                     



                   at Lamb's-Chappel in the Ward of Cripplegate


, on the 9th of January, in the 4th Year of the King and late Queen; and afterwards the 30th of November in the 9th Year of His Majesty's Reign at the place aforesaid, did take to Wife 
                     
                        Elizabeth 
                        Priestgrove
                     



, for which two Testificates were produced in Court, and the Evidence were positive as to the last Marriage, and Mary Rogers his first Wife lying in Childbed, could not appear. The Prisoner denied that ever he was Married to Rogers, but it was fully proved, the Jury found him 

               Guilty
            .
