






1402. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     RAGAN
                  




               
             and 
               
                  
                     CATHERINE 
                     RAGAN
                  




               
             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 14th of May
, 1 watch, value 2l.; 1 key, value 2d.; 1 jacket, value 10s.; 4 pairs of trousers, value 6d.; 1 frock, value 6d.; 1 pillow, value 6d.; 1 shoe, value 1s.; 1 handkerchief, value 1s., and one 5l. Bank note
            , the property of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Davis
                  




               
            .


                  JOHN 
                  DAVIS
               



            . I am a mariner
, and came home in the George the Fourth Indiaman. I came on shore on the 7th of May, and was paid off on the 13th, at the India-house; I was lodging at Mrs. Burk's in West-street, Poplar


, and on the 14th my chest was broken open, which stood in her back place - I lost a waistcoat and all the articles stated, also a 5l. note - I had received two, and given one to my landlord; I put the other into my chest - I locked my chest, and saw all the property safe on the Saturday night- I went to bed at half-past eight o'clock - when I got up in the morning my chest was broken open, and the mistress reported to me that the property was at the station-house; I found it there - I did not know the prisoners.


                  BRIDGET 
                  KELLY
               



            . I live at this house - it is a lodging-house. I saw the two prisoners in the backhouse, on a long stool, close by the chest - I then saw 
               
                  Catherine 
                  Ragan
               



             going out with a bundle, and I told Mr. Burk of it.


                  MARGARET 
                  BURK
               



            . My husband keeps this house. On the 14th of May, between nine and ten o'clock at night, Kelly told me there was a man and woman sitting in my back house, or shed, and in about five minutes I saw the woman go out with a bundle - I took the candle, went into the back house, and saw 
               
                  John 
                  Ragan
               



             sitting on the chest; I told the servant to fetch the Policemen - they came, and asked what was the matter; I said there was a chest broken open, which contained, to the best of my knowledge, a 5l. note, a watch, and some clothes - they asked if I had any suspicion; I told them I had, and they went away - I had seen the prisoners, but could not call them by name.


                  FRANCIS 
                  RASCALLAN
               



            . On the 14th of May I was sent out on an errand about ten o'clock - I saw a great concourse of people running; I saw the two Police-constables running very fast - I followed them to where the prisoners lived; they told me to knock at the door, which I did, and 
               
                  Catherine 
                  Ragan
               



             opened it; we went in - one of the officers searched her, and took her to the watch-house; the other Police-constable and I staid in the house - he told me to feel in a tub of soap-suds, and I put my hand in, and found this watch.


                  CHARLES 
                  WYKES
               



            . I am a Policeman. I took 
               
                  Catherine 
                  Ragan
               



            , searched her, and found this 5l. note in her pocket - she said 
               
                  John 
                  Ragan
               



             gave it to her.(Watch produced and sworn to.)



                  John 
                  Ragan
               



            's Defence. This woman is innocent - I gave her the property, and told her to take it home.


                  Catherine 
                  Ragan
               



             put in a written Defence, stating that the other prisoner had given her the property to carry home, and that on arriving at home he gave her the 5l. note.
J. RAGAN - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 32.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
C. RAGAN - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 30.




               Confined Three Months
            .
