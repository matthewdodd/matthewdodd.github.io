            , spinster, were indicted for 


               stealing two linen shirts, value 4 s. one cotton jam, value 5 s. one linen shift, value 1 s. and one linen apron, value 1 s. 6 d.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     Robert
                      Casey
                  



               
            , November 11

. ++


                  Elizabeth
                   Casey
               



            . I am wife of the prosecutor. I lodge at Mr. Green's at Chelsea



. On the 21st or 23d of November I lost some wet things I had hanging upon the lines to dry.
- Southwell, a headborough at Chelsea, produced the things mentioned in the indictment. A pawnbroker produced the jam and white apron, which were brought to him by Matthews; suspecting her he stops her.


                  Richard
                   Smith
               



            , a pawnbroker, deposed that he took in one of the shifts of Matthews.
Southwell. When I took the prisoner into custody I desired Littlejohn to tell me what she had done with the things; she owned she got over the wall and took the things while Matthews staid to watch.
The prisoners said nothing in their defence.
Both 


               guilty 10 d.
