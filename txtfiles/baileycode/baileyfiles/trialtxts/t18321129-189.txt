





190. 
               
                  
                     CHARLES 
                     STEWART
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 12th of November
, 4 silver spoons, value 15s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Howard
                  




               
            .


                  FREDERICK 
                  HOWARD
               



            . I am the son of William Howard - he lives at Fulham


. The prisoner was in his employ for fourteen days, to clean shoes and sweep the garden
, two or three hours every morning; these spoons were missing on the 15th of November.


                  EDWARD 
                  MURVEN
               



            . I am shopman to Mr. James Stewart, a pawnbroker, at Putney. I have three spoons, which were pawned by the prisoner - I knew him before.
Cross-examined by MR. DOANE. Q. Is not your master the prisoner's grandfather? A. Yes - I asked the prisoner who they were for - he said Mrs. Brown, of Fulham, a housekeeper.


                  GEORGE 
                  GEVERS
               



            . I am in the service of a pawnbroker at Knightsbridge. This spoon was brought by the prisoner; I stopped him with it: he said he brought it for Mrs. Brown.(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 13. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
