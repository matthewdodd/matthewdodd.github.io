





1003. 
               
                  
                     LEWIS 
                     HENRY
                  




               
             was again indicted for 


               stealing, on the 24th of December
, 4 pairs of boots, value 3l. 3s.; 2 pairs of shoes, value 14s., and 1 bag, value 1s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Edward 
                     Cheaper
                  




               
            .


                  EDWARD 
                  CHEAPER
               



            . I am a boot-maker
. On the 24th of December the prisoner, whom I had seen once before, came and selected four pairs of boots, and two pairs of shoes; he directed me to send my lad with him to Langbourn-court, Fenchurch-street - I gave him an invoice of the goods; and gave my lad goods, and a receipt for the money, to deliver to the prisoner - he fully understood it was forready money, and said if I wrote a receipt, there would be a gentleman there who would pay the lad; they went away together - the lad returned with out money or boots - I apprehended the prisoner this day fortnight.


                  EDWARD 
                  GIBBS
               



            . I am the prosecutor's errand-boy. I was to go to Langbourn-court with the prisoner; master gave me a receipt to give the gentleman for the money - the prisoner heard that; I was not to part withthem without the money - the prisoner took me down Jewry-street


, and gave me a shilling to get changed, as he wanted some halfpence, and said he would hold the bag the while; I went into a public-house - they could not give me change, and when I came out the prisoner was gone with the goods.
Prisoner. The prosecutor has known me a long time; I have been frequently at his shop.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 22.




               Transported for Seven Years for each offence
            .
