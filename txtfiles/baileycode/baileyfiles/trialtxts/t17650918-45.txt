





468. (L.) 
               
                  Joseph 
                  Waggan 
               



             was indicted for 


               stealing one cloth great coat, value 20 s.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Hyde 
                  




                
            , Esq
: August 17
. *


                  John 
                  Bone 
               



            . I am coachman to Mr. Hyde, at Hummerton


; I left my great coat, my master's property, upon the chariot. in the coach-house, on the 10th of August, and the next morning it was gone.


                  Mary 
                  Madden 
               



            . I live in Rag-fair. I bought this great coat of 
               
                  John 
                  Tinlin 
               



             (the prisoner came with him) for 15 s. 6 d. (Produced in court, and deposed to by Bone, as his master's property).


                  John 
                  Tinlin 
               



            . The prisoner asked me to sell the coat for him, and desired me to get 16 s. for it: I had known him about a fortnight.
Prisoner's Defence.
I met with a man near Clapton with a great coat; he asked me 18 s. for it: I bid him 12 and bought it for 14 s. I live in Kingsland-road, and buy and sell old cloaths.



               Guilty
            . 


               T
            .
