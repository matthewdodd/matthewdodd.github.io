



1836. 




RICHARD CALVERT



, stealing 1 pair of boots, value 7s. 6d.; the goods of 



Edward William Humberston; having been before convicted.






EDWARD WILLIAM HUMBERSTON
. I keep a shop in Leather-lane. On Saturday night, 12th Oct., as I was taking in my goods, I saw the prisoner take a pair of boots off the rail outside my window—he ran away; I ran after

him—I only lost sight of him as he turned the corner—I came nearly up to him, but did not catch him—these are my boots (produced).





JOHN SHEEHAN
 (policeman, A 442). The prisoner was stopped, and I came up and took him into custody—these boots were on the ground at his feet.

Prisoner's Defence. On my way home, some man came running after me, and laid hold of me; he dropped these boots at my feet.





ALEXANDER TURNER
 (police-sergeant, G 56). I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction—(read—Convicted Oct., 1848, having been before convicted, confined one year)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 21.—


Transported for Seven Years.
