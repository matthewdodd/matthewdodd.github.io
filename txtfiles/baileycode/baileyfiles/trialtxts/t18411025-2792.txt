



2792. 




RICHARD CANTLIN
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
24th of September, 1 handkerchief, value 2s., the goods of 



Henry Remington, from his person; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






HENRY REMINGTON
. I am a merchant, and live in St. Thomas the Apostle. On the 24th of September, about eight o'clock in the evening, I was going down Newington-causeway—I felt something at my pocket, and missed my handkerchief—I turned sharply round, and saw the prisoner close at my back—he passed by me—I went and caught him, and said, "You have taken my handkerchief—he pulled my handkerchief out of his pocket, and gave it me—this is it.

Prisoner. I kicked against something, and picked up this handkerchief. Witness. It is not possible—I felt him at my pocket distinctly, which made me turn round.






JAMES TIDMAKSH
 (police-constable M 51.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from the office of the Clerk of the Peace of Surrey—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 18.—


Transported for Ten Years.
