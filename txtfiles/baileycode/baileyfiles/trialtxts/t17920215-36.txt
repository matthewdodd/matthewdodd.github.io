





142. 
               
                  
                     THOMAS 
                     BARTON
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 7th day of November
, one linen shirt, value 7 s.
             the goods of 
               
                  
                     George 
                     Ansell
                  




               
            .


                  GEORGE
                   ANSELL
               



             sworn.
I live in Long-alley, Moorfields


: on or about the 8th of November, my servant asked me if I had taken away a shirt: the shirt was found at Mrs. Purse's, in London-wall.



                  WILLIAM
                   WAKEFIELD
               



             sworn.
I produce a shirt, which I received on the 7th of November, for 5 s. it was pledged by 
               
                  George
                   Barton
               



            .
(The shirt produced, and deposed to.)
Prisoner. That shirt was left with me by a person in Kent, a friend of mine; and I wanted money, and pledged the shirt.



               GUILTY
            .




               Transported for seven years
            .
Tried by the London Jury before Mr. COMMON SERJEANT.
