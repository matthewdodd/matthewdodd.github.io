




1386. 



WILLIAM YOUNG
 was again indicted

 for stealing, on the 
23rd of April, 1 cloak, value 2l.; and 1 coat, value 2l.; the goods of 



William Charles Laurie.






ELIZA JONES
. I am in the service of Mr. Laurie—he livesin Wilmington-square. On the 23rd of April I heard a knock at the door—the prisoner came with a note, and asked me to take it to my master—I went up and took it, and when I came down the prisoner was gone, also a coat and cloak.

Prisoner. It was dark when she came up, and she had no candle in her hand.





WILLIAM CHARLES LAURIE
. I received the note, and came down—I missed my coat and cloak—these now produced are them.





WILLIAM MURDOCK
. I produce two cloaks pawned by the prisoner, one on the 10th of April, and this of Mr. Laurie's on the 23rd.




GUILTY
.—


Transported for Seven Years more.
(There was another indictment against the prisoner, and six or seven other charges were made before the Magistrate.)
