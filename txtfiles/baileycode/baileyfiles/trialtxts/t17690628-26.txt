





365. (L.) 
               
                  
                     Alice 
                     Johnson 
                  , otherwise wife of 
                     John 
                     
Farr




            , was indicted for 


               stealing a pewter dish, value 2 s. two pewter plates, value 2 s. two copper sauce-pans, value 2 s. a copper cover, value 2 d. a copper stew-pan, an iron gridiron, a flat iron, a pair of linen sheets, two blankets, one counterpane, one bolster, one pillow, a copper tea-kettle, and a coffee-pot
            , the property of 
               
                  
                     Elizabeth 
                     Fowler 
                  




                
            , widow
, April 15
. ||


                  Elizabeth 
                  Fowler 
               



            . The prisoner lived in my house, after she was gone these goods were all missing. I found them again at two pawnbrokers. (Several of the articles produced in Court and deposed to)
Prisoner's Defence.
She would have made it up for sixteen shillings.
Prosecutrix. She wanted to make it up, but I was not willing.




               Guilty 10 d.
