



436. 




WILLIAM JONES, alias
 Crow

, was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
17th of Dec., at 
St. Clement Danes, 4 watches, value 5l. 10s.; 3 rings, 18s.; 10 breast pins, 3l. 5s.; 1 penholder, 2s.; 1 brooch, 3s.; and 1 pencil-case, 5s.; the goods of 



William Edwin Barrier; to which he pleaded





GUILTY
. Aged 18.—


Transported for Ten Years.

(



Thomas Lloyd, a piece broker, of Whitcomb-court; 



George Edwards, a bit and spur maker; and the prisoner's mother, gave him a good character.—There was another indictment against the prisoner.)
