





1491. 
               
                  
                     SAMUEL 
                     DOWNES
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 14th of May
, 1 pair of trousers, value 4s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           George 
                           Redwood
                        




                     
                  ; and that he had been before convicted of felony
            .


                  GEORGE 
                  REDWOOD
               



            . I slept at No. 2. Cooper's-garden


, on the night of the 14th of May - the prisoner slept in the same room. I got up at six o'clock in the morning; the prisoner was gone, and I missed my trousers; I found them afterwards at Mr. Attenborough's.


                  STEPHEN 
                  GLADING
               



             (Police-constable N 57). The prosecutor charged the prisoner with stealing his trousers -I took him in Cooper's-gardens; he told me where they were pledged; I went, and found them.


                  WILLIAM 
                  PAWLEY
               



            . I live with Mr. Attenborough. These trousers were pawned there by a man; I cannot say whom.(Property produced and sworn to.)


                  FRANCIS 
                  FEATHERSTONE
               



             (Police-constable N 88). I have a certificate (which I got from Mr. Clark's office) of the former conviction of the prisoner on the 7th of December, 1830; I was a witness - I know he is the person.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 22. - 


               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
