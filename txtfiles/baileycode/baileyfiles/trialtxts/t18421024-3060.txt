



3060. 




GEORGE BENTLIFFE
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
6th of October, 1 coat, value 5l., the goods of 



John Smith; and that be had been before convicted of felony.






JOHN DARTNALL
. I am coachman to Mr. John Smith. On the 8th of October my coat was on the box of my master's carriage, in the London-road—about twenty minutes to seven in the evening I had occasion to get off the box to buy some glass, and left my coat quite safe—I got on my box again, in about two minutes, and did not miss it till I got into Hart-street, Long Acre—this is it.

Prisoner. He said at the office, that he did not know whether it dropped or not. Witness. I am certain I never dropped it—it could not have dropped off the box.





JAMES BROOK
. I am a policeman. I was on duty in the Waterloo-road at a quarter before seven o'clock on Saturday evening, 8th of October, and saw the prisoner with this coat on his back—he crossed the road walkmg—I asked where he got it from—he made no answer—I took him in charge.

Prisoner's Defence. I saw the coat lying in the road, took it up, and put it on, and was going to take it to the station.






JAMES CUDDY
. I am a policeman. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from the office of the clerk of the Peace for Surrey—(read.) I am certain he is the man—I was present at his trial.




GUILTY
. Aged 21.—


Transported for Seven Years.
