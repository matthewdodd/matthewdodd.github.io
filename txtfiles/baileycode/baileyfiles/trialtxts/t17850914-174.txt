





894. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     GOLDSMITH
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing, on the 6th day of July
 last, one cloth great coat, called a box coat, value 4 s.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     Richard 
                     Gee
                  




               
            .


                  RICHARD
                   GEE
               



             sworn.
I am a coachman
, I drive my own coach, on the 6th of August last I put my coach on the stand, between one and two, at the My-pole, in East Smithfield


; I gave my horses some corn, and went into the Black Horse public-house, to get my dinner; I returned in three quarters of an hour, and missed my coat; I was informed by a poor w standing with a child, that she saw a man take it off the box; I went to Rosemary lane, and the prisoner was pointed out to me, and I took him into a public-house; the person that bought the coat came in instantly, and said in the hearing of the prisoner, that he had bought a coat of him; a constable was fetched, and the coat produced, which was mine; the prisoner denied selling the coat to the man.
- HARWOOD sworn.
Here is the coat.
(Deposed to.)


                  ISAAC
                   CELLINDER
               



             sworn.
On the 6th of August last I bought this great coat of the prisoner, between one and two, I live in Rosemary-lane; this is the coat; he said he was out of place, and had lived with a gentleman in the Minories, and that was the reason of selling it: I gave four shillings for it.



               GUILTY
            .




               Transported for seven years
            .
Tried by the second London Jury before Mr. RECORDER.
