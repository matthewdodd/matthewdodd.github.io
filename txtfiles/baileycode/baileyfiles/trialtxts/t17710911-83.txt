





641 (M) 
               
                  
                     Thomas
                      Dodd
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing one silver watch, value 4 l.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     James
                      Camm
                  




               
            , July 24th
.


                  James
                   Camm
               



            . I am servant
 to Mrs. Walker; I lost my watch the 18th of July from the bed's head; the prisoner was a fellow servant
 with me; he had been discharged from the service two days before I missed it. I took him before Sir 
               
                  John
                   Fielding
               



            , he acknowledged there he had taken it, and we found it at a pawn broker's, I used to hang my watch up at my bed's head at night; I left it one morning, and when I went to bed at night it was gone.
- Holt. I live in Marybone. This watch was pledged with me (producing it;) I believe by the prisoner at the bar, in the name of Dodd.
Prisoner's Defence.
He lent me the watch, the night before; I pawned it for want of money; I was drinking with him at the Bull, and wanted money; he lent me the watch.
For the Prisoner.


                  Edward
                   Jones
               



            . I have known 
               
                  Thomas
                   Dodd
               



             from a child; I knew his parents before they were married; he was a dutiful son; I have not known him this year or two.



               Guilty
            , 


               T
            .
