WILLIAM BELLAMY
 was indicted

 for stealing on the 
10th of July, 1 coat, value 15s.; 1 waistcoat, value 5s.; 1 pair of trowsers, value 10s.; and 1 handkerchief, value 1s.; the goods of 



Sarah Ross; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






SARAH ROSS
. I am a widow, and live at Sydenham, in Kent, The prisoner was in my service as pot-boy—he left on the 10th of July—I went to look for my son's clothes, to go to church, and missed them—a coat, waistcoat, and a pair of trowsers—this is the property,





JOHN ALLEN
. I produce the coat and waistcoat pledged, I believe by the prisoner, on Monday, the 11th of July.





SAMUEL WRIGHT
 (police-constable P 172.) I took the prisoner, and found these trowsers on him—I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from the Surrey Sessions-house—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
.* Aged 17.—


Transported for Seven Years.
