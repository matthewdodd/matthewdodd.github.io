



816. 




JOHN HAWKINS
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
3rd of November, 1 bolster, value 7s.; 2 sheets, value 6s.: 2 knives, value 2s.; 2 forks, value 2s.; 1 pillow, value 2s.; 1 quilt, value 2s.; 1 pair of snuffers, value 1s.; and 1 pillow-case, value 1s.; the goods of 



William Gullick.






MARY GULLICK
. I live at No. 7, Clifton-place, New North-road, and am the wife of William Gullick. I let a lodging to the prisoner for his mother—he was to come to see her—it was the second floor back room—he hired it on the 31st of October—he took it by the week—he absconded on the 3rd of November—I then missed all the property—I never saw him again till I saw him at Worship-street, and I was sent for—I have since recovered a coloured counterpane and a blanket.





EDWARD EDWARDS
. I am a pawnbroker, and live at No. 36, Aldersgate-street, in the employ of Mr. John Walter—I have a quilt and blanket.
(Property produced and sworn to.)

Prisoner. My mother gave me these things to pledge, telling me she intended to replace them.




GUILTY
. Aged 19.—


Transported for Seven Years.
