





Third London Jury - Before Mr. Sergeant Arabin.
1819. 
               
                  
                     HENRY 
                     WICKS
                  




               
             was indicted 


               for stealing, on the 4th of August
, 1 handkerchief, value 2s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Henry Edward 
                           Turner
                        




                     
                  , from his person
            .


                  HENRY 
                  EDWARD TURNER
               



            . I am a clerk
. About two o'clock, on the 4th of August, I was in Cheapside


 - before I had got to Cheapside I had felt a push against me, and when I got there I wanted my handkerchief, and missed it from my coat pocket; I turned round and saw the prisoner putting it into his own pocket - I collared him, took it from him, and took him to Guildhall.


                  CHARLES 
                  RICHARD BULL
               



            . I am a porter. I saw the prisoner take this handkerchief from Mr. Turner's pocket - he put it into his own pocket; Mr. Turner collared him, and said, "You have got my handkerchief" - he said "It was that boy;" there was no other boy.
Prisoner. Q. Why did you not take me? A. The gentleman took you before I could come up.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I picked it up.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 18.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
