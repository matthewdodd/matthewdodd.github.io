





240. 
               
                  MARY 
                  McSWEENEY
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 21st of November
, 1 Prayer-book, value 8s., and 1 pinafore, value 6d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  William 
                  Dixon
               




            .


                  JANE 
                  DIXON
               



            . I am the wife of William Dixon, a tailor
: we live in Wych-street


 - I have employed the prisoner occasionally - I missed my Prayer-book and a pinafore on the 21st of November - I accused her of taking them - she said she had not got them, that I might search her lodgings, and do as I pleased, and laughed at me.


                  ELIZA 
                  PAINE
               



            . The prisoner brought this pinafore and book to my mistress, Mrs. Pearce, No. 3, White Horseyard (who keeps a broker's shop). about a fortnight ago'; she asked 1s. for them - my mistress detained her, and would have sent for a constable, but she turned round and ran away - I knew her before, and am certain of her person; we lent her no money.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I knew nothing about them till they were shown to me. 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 15.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
