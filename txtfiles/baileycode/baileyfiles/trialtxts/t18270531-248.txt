





1261. 
               
                  MARY 
                  BROWN
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 27th of April
, 5 pewter pots, value 7s., the goods of John Hatchard; and 1 pewter-pot, value 1s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  James 
                  Goff
               




            .


                  JOHN 
                  HATCHARD
               



            . I keep the Marquis of Granby public-house, at Pimlico. I lost these pots.


                  THOMAS 
                  MORRIS
               



            . I am servant to Mr. Hatchard. On the 27th of April, about twenty minutes past eight o'clock, I was collecting pots - a person came to me. I went and saw the prisoner going down Raneleigh-walk, and asked if she had any pots; she said No. I turned her clothes aside, and found five.


                  JOHN 
                  SOUTHERWOOD
               



            . I took her in charge; she said she lived in King-street, and then that she had no lodging - I took her to the watch-house, and found Goff's pot on her.
JOHN GOFF. I keep the Crown and Anchor public-house, Ebury-street, Pimlico
 - this pot is mine.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 57.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
