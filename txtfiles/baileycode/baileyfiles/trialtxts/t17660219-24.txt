





138. (M.) 
               
                  Anne, wife
 of 
                     
                        Joseph 
                        Morley 
                     



                  , otherwise 
                        
Anne Waley




            , spinster
, was indicted for 


               stealing two linen bed-curtains, value 2 s. 6 d. two linen sheets, value 2 s. 6 d. a copper tea-kettle, value 2 s. and a man's hat, value 12 d.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     Charles 
                     Jeenes 
                  




                
            , February 11
. ||


                  Charles 
                  Jeenes 
               



            . I keep a pork-shop
 in Golden-lane, the prisoner was a lodger of mine; she came to me as a married woman, and has since declared she is not married. I have lost many things, but I charge her with nothing but what we found and brought here; they were pledged in her name.


                  Daniel 
                  Getens 
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, I took these things in of the prisoner at the bar; I lent her the same money upon them as is mentioned in the indictment.
Prisoner's defence.
I did not quit my lodgings, I pawned them for want.



               Guilty
            . 


               T
            .
