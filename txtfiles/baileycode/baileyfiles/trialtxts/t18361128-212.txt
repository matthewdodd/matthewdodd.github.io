



212. 




GEORGE JAMES WICK
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
21st of September, 1 gown, value 5s.; 1 cap, value 4d.; 1 apron, value 2d.; 1 pair of shoes, value 1s. 3d.; and 1 pair of stockings, value 3d.; the goods of 



Henry Curnell.






ELIZABETH CURNELL
. I am the wife of Henry Curnell. The prisoner

is my son by a former husband—on Sunday, the 20th of November, I sent him for some flour, and he ran away with 4 1/2d. which I gave him, and did not return—and on Monday morning, when I went down stairs, I found the street door open, and missed these things from up stairs—they are very old—I have six children living by my former husband.





CHARLES DORSET
. I am an officer. I found the prisoner about three o'clock, and found the apron, and cap, and duplicate of the gown on him.





STEPHEN WHITAKER
. I am a pawnbroker, in Long-lane, Smithfield I produce a gown, which was pawned by the prisoner—I gave him this duplicate for it.




GUILTY
. Aged 14.—


Transported for Seven Years.

Before Mr. Recorder.
