



204. 




ROBERT WEST
. was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
16th of November, 49 yards of carpet, value 4l., the goods of 



Thomas Reeve and another.






CHARLES REEVE
. I am shopman to my brothers, Thomas and William Reeve, pawnbrokers in Gray's-inn-lane—I saw these forty-nine yards of carpet inside the shop on the 16th of November, about half-past six o'clock, and missed it about seven that evening—I went to Mr. Lander's, a pawn-broker in Gray's-inn-road, and found the prisoner there, in the act of pawning it—that now produced is the same.

Cross-examined by
MR. PAYNE. Q. What time did you get to the pawnbroker's? A. About a quarter past seven—the prisoner was at the counter offering it to pledge.





JOHN CAYFOR
. (police-constable E 97.) I took the prisoner.





ROBERT KENNY
. I am shopman to Mr. Lander—about a quarter past seven o'clock that evening the prisoner brought this carpet to me.

Cross-examined. Q. Did the prisoner bring the carpet? A. Another man brought it, he left the shop immediately—the prisoner offered it to pledge.




GUILTY
. Aged 25.—


Transported for Seven Years.
