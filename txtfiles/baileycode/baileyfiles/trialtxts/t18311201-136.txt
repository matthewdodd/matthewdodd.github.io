






136. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     CORBETT
                  




               
             and 
               
                  
                     AGNES 
                     CORBETT
                  



               
             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 21st of October
, 160 yards of printed cotton, value 7l.; 9 pairs of stockings, value 2l., and 3 yards of silk, value 8s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  John Wharton 
                  Soppet
               




            .
MESSRS. PHILLIPS and BODKIN conducted the prosecution.
JOHN WHARTON SOPPET. I am a linen-draper
, and live in High-street, Marylebone. John Corbett was in my employ for about eight months, as shopman
; I then discharged him - I have five other shopmen; I never saw 
               
                  Agnes 
                  Corbett
               



             at my house.


                  ARTHUR 
                  MINTON
               



            . I am a Police-constable. On the 21st of the October I saw Agnes Corbett near Bryanstone-square; she was very tipsy, and attracted my notice - I took her, and found on her twenty-seven duplicates, a note, and a shop-bill of Mr. Soppet's - I went to Mr. Soppet's the same evening, and I went to the different pawnbrokers - I went to the attic of a house in Hatton-wall, where I found a number of articles; I have no knowledge of who lived there - 
               
                  Agnes 
                  Corbett
               



             said she lived in Hatton-wall, but I do not think she said the number; this is the note I found on her.


                  ISAAC 
                  HUGHES PUGH
               



            . I am in the service of a pawnbroker, in Aldersgate-street. I have nine yards and a half of cotton, pawned by a woman for 4s.; I cannot say who, but this is the duplicate I gave.


                  FRANCIS 
                  HENRY PARKER
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, and live in Skinner-street, Somers'-town. I have a piece of cotton, pawned on the 3rd of October, for 4s. 6d., by a woman; I cannot say who - I gave her this duplicate.


                  ROBERT 
                  LINWOOD
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have fourteen yards of printed cotton, pawned on the 14th of October, for 6s., by a female, in the name of 
               
                  Ann 
                  Helps
               



            ; I gave her this duplicate.


                  JAMES 
                  MASTERS HOWIE
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have twelve yards of cotton, pawned on the 20th of October, by a woman, in the name of Sarah Hill, and to the best of my belief, 
               
                  Agnes 
                  Corbett
               



             is the person who pawned it - I gave her this duplicate.


                  JOSIAH 
                  PEARCE
               



            . I am in the service of a pawnbroker. I have a pair of stockings, and thirteen yards of cotton, pawned by a female on the 20th and 24th of September; I gave her these duplicates.
MR. WHITAKER. I am a pawnbroker, and live in Long-lane. I have fourteen yards of printed cotton, pawned on the 20th of October, for 6s.; I believe by the female prisoner - I gave her this duplicate.


                  JOHN 
                  DAVIS
               



            . I am in the service of a pawnbroker, in Exmouth-street. I have twelve yards of cotton, pawned on the 20th of October, by a woman, I believe it was the female prisoner.


                  WILLIAM 
                  HOOPER
               



            , I am in the service of a pawnbroker. I have twelve yards of printed cotton, pawned on the 22nd of September, to the best of my knowledge by the female prisoner.


                  JOHN 
                  WILLIAM ADLINGTON
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have a piece of cotton pawned on the 5th of October, for 5s.; this is the duplicate that was given, but I did not take it in.


                  JOSEPH 
                  HALL
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have three pieces of cotton, and two pairs of stockings pawned; one on the 3rd of October by the female prisoner, and the other by another woman.


                  WILLIAM 
                  ROBINSON
               



            . I am a pawnbroker in Theosald's-road. I have a grown, pawned by a woman; I do not know who - I gave her this duplicate.


                  JAMES 
                  TRAIL
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have a pair of stockings pawned by a female; I do not know who.


                  WILLIAM 
                  KING
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have two pairs of stockings, pawned on the 26th of September, by a woman the name of Ann Green - I gave her this duplicate.


                  FREDERICK 
                  PRIEST
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have a piece of cotton, pawned in the name of Ann Adams.


                  JAMES 
                  HAYES
               



            . I am in the service of a pawnbroker. have two pair of stockings, pawned on the 15th of September by a man in the name of 
               
                  Matthew 
                  Jenkins
               



            .


                  WILLIAM 
                  HILL
               



            . I am landlord of the house in Hatton-garden; the two prisoners lodged in a back room there on the second floor as man and wife - the woman took the room; the officer searched the same room.


                  ARTHUR 
                  MINTON
               



            . I found in that room a piece of gros de Naples, and a great variety of other articles in a box.
MR. SOPPET. Several of these articles have my mark on them, and the others I believe are mine.
COURT. Q.Some of these are remnants? A. Yes, and if they had been sold the fag would have been left on; but it is torn off these - this piece of silk has my mark on it, and has not been sold; those articles found in the box are ribbons and little things which I cannot swear to, but I had similar articles - I always attend the shop myself.
Cross-examined by MR. BARRY. Q. You cannot tell what goods you may sell in any one day? A. No; I have occasion to go out to purchase goods - I cannot say that 
               
                  Agnes 
                  Corbett
               



             has not been there and made purchases.


                  JAMES 
                  HUNT
               



            . I am cashier to the prosecutor. I am there every day and take down what the customers pay; I know John Corbett, but I never recollect seeing Agnes.


                  JOHN 
                  CORBETT
               





               GUILTY
             - Aged 29.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
AGNES CORBETT - 

               NOT GUILTY
            .
