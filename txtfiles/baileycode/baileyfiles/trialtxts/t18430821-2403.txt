



2403. 




JOHN WOOD
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
5th of July, 1 handkerchief, value 1s., the goods of 



Joseph Lamb; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






JOHN LAMB
. I am the son of Joseph Lamb, who keeps a sale-shop.

About the 5th of July the prisoner came to sell some clothes—I missed a handkerchief—it was safe when the prisoner came—when my father came home he missed it—this is it.





JOHN COX
. About the 5th of July the prisoner gave me this handkerchief, and asked me to pawn it, which I did, at Mr. Grant's.





SAMUEL GUMNERS
 (police-constable B 131.) I apprehended the prisoner. This handkerchief was given up to me at Queen-square—the pawnbroker said Cox brought it.
(Property produced and sworn to.)

Prisoner's Defence. There was another boy went into the shop; I did not see any handkerchief; when I came out I saw the boy again, and he sold it to me for 4d.; I had no money, and asked Cox to pawn it for me; he pawned it for 9d., and I gave him 1 1/2 d.





JAMES LIGHT
 (police-constable B 128.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clark's office—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 15.—


Transported for Seven Years—Convict Ship.


Sixth Jury, before Edward Bullock, Esq.
