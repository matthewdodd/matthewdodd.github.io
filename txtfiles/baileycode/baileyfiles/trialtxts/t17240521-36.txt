







                     Robert 
                     Draper 
                  



               
            , of St. Dunstan's in the East


, was indicted for 


               breaking the House of 
                     
                        
                           Sarah 
                           Townley 
                        



                      
                  , and stealing the Money of 
                     
                        
                           Charles 
                           Townley 
                        



, the 12th of April
 last. 
               
                  Ebenezer 
                  Pendril 
               



             depos'd, That the Prisoner having been Journeyman to a Barber
, to whom he himself was Apprentice, (whose House adjoined to the Prosecutor's) used to come to him, and had enticed him to assist him in robbing the Prosecutor, and that he did let him into his Master's Cellar, where they pull'd down Part of a Brick-Wall, and the Prisoner got into the Counting-house of the Prosecutor, and got some Money, what he could not tell, but he gave him some of it. 


               The Fact being plainly prov'd as to the Felony, the Jury found him guilty; but the Burglary not being so plain, they acquitted him of that
            .
