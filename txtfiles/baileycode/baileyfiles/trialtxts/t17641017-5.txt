








548, 549. (M.) 
               
                  
                     Robert 
                     Powell 
                  



                
             and 
               
                  
                     James 
                     Rice 
                  



                
             were indicted for 


               stealing one pair of leather breeches, value 2 s. one pair of cotton stockings, value 1 s. one pair of leather Shoes, value 5 s. one pair of metal shoe-buckles, value 1 s. the property of 
                     
                        
                           Daniel 
                           Holderness 
                        



                      
                  ; one waistcoat, the property of 
                     
                        
                           John 
                           Smith 
                        



                      
                  ; and one other waistcoat
            , the property of 
               
                  
                     James 
                     Pinches 
                  



                
            ; September 19

. *


                  Daniel 
                  Holderness 
               



            . I lost a pair of breeches, a pair of shoes, and a pair of buckles; Powell was taken up, and confessed he stole them, as we were carrying him to prison: he had my breeches on when taken: he said the other prisoner was a confederate with him.


                  John 
                  Smith 
               



            . I lost a scarlet waistcoat, which the prisoner had on when taken: I know nothing against Rice, except what Powell said.
Powell's Defence.
This young man (meaning Rice) was not with me when I took the things.
Powell 

               Guilty
            .
Rice 

               Acquitted
            .
(M.) They were a second time indicted, for 


               stealing a wooden box, value 2 s. a pair of leather breeches, value 5 s. a pair of cotton stockings, value 1 s. the property of 
                     
                        
                           Robert 
                           White 
                        



                      
                  ; a pair of leather shoes, value 2 s. and a pair of iron shoebuckles, plated with silver
            , the property of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Elwood 
                  



                
            ; September 19

. *


                  Robert 
                  White 
               



            . I lost a box with the things mentioned; I had a Suspicion of Powell; he was apprehended, and upon being charged with taking the things mentioned, confessed he did take them; he said, Rice had part of the things. For my part, I know nothing against Rice.


                  John 
                  Elwood 
               



            . I heard Powell confess he stole the things mentioned; my shoes are on his feet now. I know nothing against Rice.
Powell 

               Guilty
