





1065. 
               
                  
                     LAWRENCE 
                     HALLORAN
                  , alias 
                     
LAWRENCE HYNES HALLORAN
                  , alias 
                     
WILLIAM CHARLES GREGORY





            , was indicted for 


               that he, after the 1st of July, 1802, to wit, on the 9th of January, in the 57th year of his Majesty's reign
, feloniously did forge and counterfeit the hand-writing of a certain person, to wit, Sir 
                     
                        
                           William 
                           Garrow
                        




                     
                  , Knt.
, the said Sir William Garrow, then being a member of the Commons House of Parliament of the United Kingdom, and being entitled to send letters by the post to places within the said United Kingdom, free from the duty of postage, in the superscription of a letter to be sent, and which was afterwards sent, by the post, to wit, from London to Broseley, in the county of Salop, in order to avoid the payment of the duty of postage upon and in respect of the said letter, against the statute
            .
ELEVEN OTHER COUNTS, varying the manner of laying the charge.
To which indictment the prisoner pleaded




               GUILTY
            . Aged 53.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
First Middlesex Jury, before Mr. 
               
                  Baron 
                  Graham
               



            .
