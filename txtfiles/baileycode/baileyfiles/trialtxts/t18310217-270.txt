





696. 
               
                  
                     JAMES 
                     CLARK
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 28th of December
, 1 coat, value 2l. 10s., and 1 hat, value 10s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Thomas 
                     Ellis
                  




               
            .
THOMAS ELLIS. I am a sawyer
, and live in Crown-street, Soho


 - the prisoner lodged in the same house, and slept with me; he was errand-boy
 to Mr. Matthews. On the 28th of December I got up first in the morning, and left my coat and hat in the room - I returned at five o'clockin the evening; they were gone, and so was the prisoner -I found him last Wednesday week; I asked him about my coat and hat, and he said he had sold them to a Jew for 10s.


                  JANE 
                  EDWARDS
               



            . I live in the house . The prisoner told me that 
               
                  Thomas 
                  Ellis
               



             had sent him for his coat and hat - that he was at his uncle's, and was tipsy ; he went up stairs , got them , and took them away.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 16. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
             .
