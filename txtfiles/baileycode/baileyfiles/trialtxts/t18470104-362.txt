



362. 




CHARLES KELLY
 was indicted

for stealing 1 handkerchief, value 3d. the goods of 



Charles Hibble, from his person; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






CHARLES HIBBLE.
 I live at No. 24, King-street, Westminster. On the 19th of Dec., about twelve o'clock, I was going down Holborn—I was speaking to somebody—I turned round, and missed my pocket-handkerchief—this is it—I had it safe about half an hour before—I am sure this is mine.





THOMAS BRADLEY
 (City police-constable, No. 269.) I was watching a poulterer's shop on Holborn-hill—I saw the prisoner take this handkerchief from the prosecutor's pocket—he had it in his right hand—another officer took him by the collar, and directly the took him he threw it away.





WILLIAM SICKLE
 (City police-constable, No. 290.) I saw the prisoner take the handkerchief from the gentleman's pocket—this is the handkerchief.





JOHN DAVIS
 (City police-constable, No. 551.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction at this Court—(read—Convicted 21st Sept., 1846, and confined three months.)




GUILTY.† Aged 14.—


Transported for Seven Years.
