



2909. 




JAMES PRICE
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
27th of June, I watch, value 15s., the goods of 



William Price.






WILLIAM PRICE
. I live with my father, this watch is mine—I left it on the 27th of June in a drawer in the bed-room—I missed it, and my brother (the prisoner) was suspected—we asked him about it, and he denied it, but he afterwards confessed about it—my mother went and found it at the pawnbroker's.





GEORGE SMALLBONE
. I am a pawnbroker—I took in this watch of Thomas Newman Croker, at my shop, for 8s., it is worth 15s.





THOMAS NEWMAN CROKER
. My mother gave me the ticket of this watch, and I got it out of pledge—I wanted money, and I pawned it again at Smallbone's.





ANN CROKER
. Mrs. Lambourne gave roe a duplicate of the watch—she said her little boy had picked it up.





HARRIET LAMBOURNE
. My son gave me the ticket of this watch, and I gave it to Mrs. Croker, who is my sister.





THOMAS LAMBOURNE
. The prisoner gave me the ticket of the watch for a hare-skin—he said he had won the watch at a raffle.




GUILTY. Aged 15.—Recommended to mercy by the Jury
—


Transported for Seven Years—Convict Ship.
