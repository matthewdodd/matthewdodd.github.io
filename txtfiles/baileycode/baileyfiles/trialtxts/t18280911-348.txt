





1901. 
               
                  JOHN 
                  WALKER
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 20th of August
, 3 frocks, value 5s.; 5 petticoats, value 2s.; 1 pelisse, value 4s., and 1 pinafore, value 2d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  John 
                  Hicks
               




            .


                  ELIZABETH 
                  HICKS
               



            . I am wife of John Hicks - we live in Noble-street, St. Luke's


. I lost three frocks, six petticoats, a pelisse, a pinafore, and some other things out of my room where I live and sleep; they had been in a box; I wanted a night-gown for the child, and went to the box and missed them, on the 20th of August - I had seen them in the course of the day; the prisoner had lodged with me on the 2d of August, but I don't know where he lodged after that - these are the articles.
Prisoner. Q. Will you swear they are your's? A. Yes; they belong to the child, who is my grandson.


                  THOMAS 
                  KIRKWOOD
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have a flannel petticoat which I took in of the prisoner, to the best of my belief, on the 20th of August; I gave this duplicate.


                  CHARLES 
                  WORLEY
               



            . I am shopman to a pawnbroker in Old-street - I have a frock and four petticoats pawned with me by the prisoner.
JAMES - . I am an officer; I took the prisoner.


                  JAMES 
                  STANTON
               



            . I am a pawnbroker - I have two frocks, a petticoat and pinafore, pawned by the prisoner on the 20th of August.(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 30.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
