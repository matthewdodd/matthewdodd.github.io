







                     William 
                     Wright 
                  



               
             of Isleworth


, was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing a Leaden Cistern value 30s.
             the Goods of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Gumley 
                  




                
            , Esq
; on the 28th of November
 last. 
               
                  John 
                  Davis 
               



             deposed, that 
               
                  William 
                  Corner 
               



             ask'd him one Morning if he would go along with him and the Prisoner Wright to fetch a Cistern belonging to  the Prisoner; and afterward the Prisoner askt him himself; that he went with them about 9 or 10 at Night, that they went in the back way, moved it into a piece of Garden-Ground, cut it into 4 pieces and threw it into some Water to hide it; and that this Evidence discovered where it was when he was taken up. Another Evidence deposed that the Prisosoner's Mother kept the Prosecutor's House; that he found the waste Pipe of the Cistern in the Prisoner's House; that the Prisoner then deny'd the taking of it, and said he found the Pipe on the Road; and that Davis told him (this Evidence) where it was, and he found it accordingly. Another Evidence deposed that the Prisoner told him he thought he had as much Right to it as Mr. Gumley. The Prisoner in his Defence said that he had it from the House where his Uncle left it, and that it was his Uncle's House. John Davis being called again, deposed, that the House and Cistern did belong to the Prisoner's Uncle Mr. Crosby, who sold it to Mr. Gumly. The Jury found him 

               Guily
