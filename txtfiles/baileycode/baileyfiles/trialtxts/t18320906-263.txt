





1992. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     BRIDLEY
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing' on the 15th of August
, 1 pair of shoes, value 4s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           George 
                           Clarke
                        




                     
                  ; and that he had been before convicted of felony
            .
GEORGE CLARKE. I am a shoemaker
, on Ludgate-hill


. On the 15th of August, about half-past nine o'clock, while my back was turned, these shoes were taken.


                  WILLIAM 
                  BROWN
               



            . I live in Whitmore-place, Hoxton, and am a shoemaker. I was in Mr. Clarke's shop on the 15th of August; he was busy - I saw the prisoner come in, and run off with a pair of shoes, which were two yards within the door; I pursued, and in Stationers'court secured him, and saw him throw them down - I held him, and brought them back.


                  SAMUEL 
                  COLLIER
               



            . I live in Kirby-street, Hatton-garden, and am a professor of music. I was at the top of Stationers'-court; the prisoner ran up the court, and Brown after him - I saw him throw down the shoes, and picked them up; he was taken, and I followed him into the shop with them.


                  MICHAEL 
                  LYON
               



            . The shoes were delivered to me, with the prisoner.(Property produced and sworn to.)


                  LEONARD 
                  COX
               



            . I am a Policeman. I have a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, and know him to be the man - I was present at the time - (read.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 21. - 


               Transported for Life
            .
