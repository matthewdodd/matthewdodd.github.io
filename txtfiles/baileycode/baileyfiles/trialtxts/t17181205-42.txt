







                     William 
                     Crookshanks 
                  



               
             , of the Parish of St. Andrews Holbourn


, was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing a Bed, Curtains, Fire-Irons, Sawce-pans, and divers other Houshold Fuirniture
            , the 1st of November
 last, the property of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Powel 
                  




                
            : it appear'd that the Prisoner being a Lodger in the Prosecuters House, and while his Landlord and Landlady were gone out, gutted the Room, and sold the Goods, the fact was plainly prov'd, but the Prisoner pleaded he had been drinking on Crispins Day, being a Shoemaker
, went home very drank and remembered nothing at all of the matter, neither how he got Home at Night, nor how-he carried out the Goods next day, nor of the selling of them, nor anything else, however the Jury not with standing his ignorance and bad memory, found him 


               guilty to the value of 39 s.
            



               Transportation
            .
