






713. 
               
                  
                     RICHARD 
                     KAYNE
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing, on the 12th day of June
, one man's cloth great coat, value 7 s. and one cloth surtoot coat, value 13 s.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     Francis 
                     Flaxmoor
                  




               
            .


                  FRANCIS
                   FLAXMOOR
               



             sworn.
I am a hackney coachman
 , I lost my property in Houndsditch


, the 12th of June, on a Sunday; I was getting off my box, I rang at a bell, and the box coat and a surtoot coat were both on the box, I missed them , and took the prisoner with the coats on his arm, he begged me to let him go, he said, he picked them up in the street, I am sure I left them on the box when I got off, they are my property.
PRISONER's DEFENCE.
I picked them up in Hounsditch, in the road near to a coach, says I, to myself, they are two coats, I walked on and heard him call stop! stop! and I immediately turned towards him, and walked two or three paces, he said they are my coats, I said very likely, they are not mine.



               GUILTY
            .




               Transported for seven years
            .
Tried by the second Middlesex Jury before Mr. ROSE.
