





1835. 
               
                  EDWARD 
                  JONES
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 22d of August
, 24 yards of cloth, value 24s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  Robert 
                  Watt
               




            .



                  ELIZABETH 
                  WATT
               



            . I am the wife of Robert Watt, linen-draper
, of Crawford-street, Marylebone


. On the 22d of August a little girl came and told me something was taken from the door - our young woman ran after the prisoner and cried Stop thief! he was brought back with the cloth; I then missed it from the door - I had seen it about half an hour before.


                  SARAH 
                  CANNON
               



            . I saw the man take it from the door; he told me not to tell; they ran away with it; I did not see the prisoner stopped; I saw him when he was taken, in about a quarter of an hour.
COURT. Q. What did the prisoner do? A. The other man gave him this cloth - it was the other told me not to tell.


                  JAMES 
                  SPARKS
               



            . I took this cloth and the prisoner in York-street, about two hundred yards from Mr. Watt's shop - he was running with it.
Prisoner. Q. Did you take me with the cloth? A. No; he threw it down just before I took him; the young woman took it up, and gave it me in the shop.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. A man asked me to carry a bundle, and he gave it into my hands - we turned up Crawford-street; I heard the cry, and thought there was something wrong - I threw down the bundle and ran; I said a man gave it to me to carry.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 23.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
