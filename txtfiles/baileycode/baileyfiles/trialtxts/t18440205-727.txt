



727. 




THOMAS SEXTON
 was indicted

 for stealing 2 1/2 lbs. weight of brass, value 2s., 6d.; and 1 metal pillar, 2s. 6d.; the goods of 



William M'Donald; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






WILLIAM M'DONALD
. I keep a broker's shop, in Great Wild-street. On the 1st of Feb. I placed a metal gas-pillar outside my door—it was two feet six inches from the pavement, and tied with a string—I missed it—the policeman brought it to me—this is it.





ROBERT ADAMS
 (police-constable E 109.) I saw the prisoner with this pillar, in Belton-street, Long-acre, between six and seven o'clock in the evening, on the 1st of February—he said he picked it up in Long-acre—he might be about 500 yards from the prosecutor's.

Prisoner's Defence. I picked it up.





CHARLES GRAHAM
 (police-constable S 21.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got at Mr. Clark's office—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 18.—


Transported for Seven Years.
