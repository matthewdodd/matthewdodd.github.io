



2393. 




BENJAMIN HAYDON
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
24th of October, 4 loaves of bread, value 1s. 5d.) the goods of 



Thomas Sully; and that he had been previously convicted of felony.






WILLIAM PARTRIDGE
. I live with my father in Bell-street, Miller-street. On the 24th of October I saw the prisoner go into Mr. Sully's shop on his hands and knees, and crawl out again with four loaves under his arm—he had no shoes on—I went in and told Mr. Sully.

Prisoner. It was not me—it was another boy. Witness. I am certain it was him.





THOMAS SULLY
. I ran out and caught the prisoner with my loaves—I saw him drop them.





THOMAS LENNY
. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction from Mr. Clark's office—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
*. Aged 14.—


Transported for Seven Years.
