



3475. 




JOHN WILLIAMS
, was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
27th of October, 5 handkerchiefs, value 6s.; 1 pair of trowsers, value 10s.; 1 night-gown, value 1s.; and 1 shirt, value 6d.; the goods of 



William Flexman.






MARY HARMON
. I am servant to Mr. William Flexman, of Gloucester-street, St. George the Martyr. I saw the prisoner, on the 27th of October, go out of the passage—I followed him, and he dropped this bundle, containing these things, which are my master's—a young man took him to the station-house, and the officer fetched me.

Prisoner. I did not drop them. Witness. Yes—I never lost sight of him.





SAMUEL BUTLER
. I live in Kingsgate-street, and am a porter. About seven o'clock in the morning I saw the prisoner running, and drop the bundle—I took him to the station-house.




GUILTY
.* Aged 14.—


Transported for Seven Years.

ADJOURNED TO MONDAY, NOVEMBER 28, 1836.
