







                     Richard 
                     Rymer 
                  



               
            , of St. Dunstan in the West


, was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing a Silver Spoon, value 10 s.
             the Goods of 
               
                  
                     Jabes 
                     Collier 
                  




Gent
, on the 14th of July
 last. It appeared that the Prisoner knockt at the Prosecutor's Door, and askt the Maid how the Water came in, pretending to be a Servant to the New River Company: that she went down with him and shewed him the Pipe and Cock where it ran into the Cistern in the Kitchin, where was a Silver Porringer and 3 Spoons; that he took up one of the Spoons which the Maid perceiving, askt him what he had got, and he reply'd, a Scure to open the Pipe, saying, that the Water should come in as well as ever it did: holding his Hand which had the Spoon in it behind him; but she seized his Hand and took the Spoon from him, and calling out, when the Clerks came to her Assistance they searcht him, but found nothing else upon him. His Confession before the Justice was read in Court. The Prisoner in his Defence said that he was very drunk, and did not know where he was. The Jury considering the matter, found him 


               Guilty to the value of 10 d.
