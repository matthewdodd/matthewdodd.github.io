





OLD COURT. THURSDAY, JULY 7.
First Middlesex Jury, before Mr. Sergeant 
               Arabin
            .
1465. 
               
                  HENRY 
                  SALMON
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 17th of June
, 1 watch, value 2l.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     James 
                     Brookman
                  




               
            .


                  WILLIAM 
                  SMITH
               



            . I am servant to James Brookman, an upholsterer
, at Islington


. On the 17th of June the prisoner came from Mr. Owen, a polisher, and was at work in our front shop for about two hours; this watch hung under the mantel-piece in that shop - it was missed about half an hour after he left; he had been alone in the shop.


                  WILLIAM 
                  SHEEHY
               



            . I am a Policeman. I was on duty in Liquorpond-street on the 25th of June - the prisoner came and said, "Policeman, I am waiting for you;" I said, "What for?" he said, "I want to give myself up to you;" I said, "What for?" he said, "I have taken a watch and hat from Mr. Owen, of Liquorpond-street;" I took him to the watch-house - he said he had pawned the watch at Fleming's, Fleet-market.


                  WILLIAM 
                  CLARK
               



            . I am shopman to Mr. Fleming. I have a watch, pawned on the 17th of June, in the name of 
               
                  Henry 
                  Bennett
               



             - I do not know who by.(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 19. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
