




1237. 




WILLIAM BROWN
 and 




CHARLES GILES



, stealing 1 hand kerchief, value 1s.; the goods of 



William Thorpe, from his person: Brown having been before convicted: to which
BROWN pleaded




GUILTY
. Aged 18.—


Transported for Seven Years.






WILLIAM THORPE
. I live at 18, Barnsbury-row, Islington. On 21st May, about four o'clock, I had my handkerchief safe—the officer gave me information, and I missed it—this is it (produced)—it is marked.





ALFRED GREEN
 (City-policeman 376). I saw the prisoners following Mr. Thorpe—Brown took this handkerchief from his pocket; Giles was covering him—they ran down Bow-lane—I overtook them, and took them to the station—Brown pulled this handkerchief out of his pocket, and said it was his own—I had watched them following three gentlemen for half an hour.

GILES—


GUILTY
.* Aged 15.—



Confined Three Months.
