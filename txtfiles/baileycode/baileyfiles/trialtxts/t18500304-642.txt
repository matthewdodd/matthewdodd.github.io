




642. 




WILLIAM ARMITAGE
 and 




WILLIAM LANGLEY



 stealing 1 pair of breeches, 1 pair of braces, and 1 towel, value 7s.; the goods of 



William Martin: Armitage having been before convicted.






WILLIAM MARTIN
. About one o'clock on Sunday morning, 17th Feb., I was alarmed by the policeman—I came down and missed a pair of breeches, a pair of braces, and a towel which were safe when I went to bed on Saturday night on the palings near the store-house, out of doors—these are them—they are all mine.





BENJAMIN TRAWIN
 (policeman, K 295.) I was on duty about one o'clock that morning in Fisher-street, Barking—I went round the back yard of Mr. Martin—I saw Langley outside the warehouse, and Armitage passing through a hole in the palings—Langley saw me and dropped these things—I jumped over the palings, seized him and Armitage, and took them to the station—I returned and called Mr. Martin.

Langley. I never dropped these; they fell off the rails.





BENJAMIN TREWIN

re-examined. I produce a certificate of Armitage's eonviction at this Court— (read—Convicted Dec, 1848, and confined one year)—be is the person.

ARMITAGE—


GUILTY
. Aged 18.—


Transported for Seven Years.


LANGLEY—


GUILTY
. Aged 19.—



Confined Eight Months.
