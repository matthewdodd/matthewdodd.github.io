, near the Royal Exchange


; The Evidence was as followeth, viz. Massey having a Shop under St. Bennet Finck Church, which he left and went from commonly at Six or Seven at Night, the same was broken open and robb'd, the Watchmen finding it open at Eleven at Night; it also appeared, that this Massey having sometime before Christmass turned away a Boy, whom he found to be an ill Servant; Savage, Bradshaw, and Watt, meeting his said Boy in Fleet street, and inquiring of him, to direct them where they might rob a Shop, he bargained for Half a Crown in hand, to be made up 10 s. after the Robbery, to help and direct them to his late Masters shop: Mr. Massey's Shop was accordingly broken up and robb'd;
