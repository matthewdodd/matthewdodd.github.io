                  Richard 
                  Randall 
               



            . I live the backside of Castle-Street near Rag-Fair


; the Prisoners broke my door open, and took these things away about eleven o'clock in the day time - I took Collet in Petticoat-Lane, and when she was before the Justice she confessed every thing.
- Randall. I sell old clothes
; when I went out about ten in the morning, Collet met me and wished me good luck. I returned about two, for a little girl told me in the Fair that my house was broke open. I came home and found I was robbed. - I suspected the Prisoner, because she was seen to come out of my house with her lap full of things. I never had any of the things again.
The Justice before whom she was carried deposed; that she made her confession freely, and told every particular fact, and was willing to sign it: that he told her if it was wrote it must be read in evidence against her, and she said she would freely do it.
