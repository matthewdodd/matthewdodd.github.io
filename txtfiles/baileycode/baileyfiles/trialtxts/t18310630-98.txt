





OLD COURT. SATURDAY, JULY 2.
First Middlesex Jury, before Mr. Sergeant Arabin.
1286. 
               
                  
                     THOMAS 
                     MACDONALD
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 17th of May
, 1 handkerchief, value 2s. 6d., the goods of 
                     
                        William Bernard 
                        Cooke
                     




                  , from his person
            .
WILLIAM BERNARD COOKE. I am an engraver
. On the 17th of May, about seven o'clock in the evening, I was at Limehouse


, and felt a pull at my coat pocket - I looked round, and saw my handkerchief in the prisoner's hand; he dropped it, and ran away - I followed, and took him about twenty yards off, without losing sight of him.
WILLIAM MAY. I am an officer. I received the prisoner in charge, with the handkerchief.(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 15. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
