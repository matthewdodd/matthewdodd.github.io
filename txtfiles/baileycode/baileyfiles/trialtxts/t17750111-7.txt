





101. (M.) 
               
                  
                     BRIDGET
                      SINGLETON
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing a silver filigree measure, value two shillings, a linen table cloth, value three shillings, a linen napkin; value one shilling, two yards of thread lace, value three shillings, and a linen shirt, value three shillings
            , the property of 
               
                  
                     Rebecca
                      Hankey
                  




               
            , widow, December 21st
. ++


                  Rebecca
                   Hankey
               



            . I did live in Piccadilly


, I now live in Rathbone Place; the prisoner was my servant
. I missed the things mentioned in the indictment: suspecting the prisoner, I searched her box, and there I found most of the things mentioned in the indictment. (They were produced in court, and deposed to by the prosecutrix.
Q. How long had she lived with you?
Hankey. About two months: she is very young, but about seventeen.


                  James
                   Aldersey
               



            . I am a pawn-broker: the prisoner pawned this table cloth, (producing it) at my shop, (it was deposed to by the prosecutrix.


                  Charles
                   Grubb
               



            . I was sent for to search the prisoner's box; I found in it the pawnbroker's duplicate of the table cloth.
Prisoner's Defence.
The shift was given me at a place I lived at two years ago, I never saw the table cloth.
The prisoner called three witnesses, who gave her a good character.



               Guilty
            , 


               T
            .
