



417. 




JAMES MAWSON
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 22nd of December, 1 tea-pot, value 2s. 6d., the goods of 



John Dallor; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






JOHN DALLOR
. I am a broker, afld live at Battle-Bridge. On Monday evening I missed a metal tea-pot, which I had seen safe a quarter of an hour before in the shop—this is it.





THOMAS HOBBS KING
. I was on duty at Battle-Bridge on this evening—I saw the prisoner, and stopped him—I asked what he had under his coat—he said a tea-pot, and that he found it under the wall.





JAMES WELLS
. I am a policeman. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clark's office (read)—the prisoner is the man.




GUILTY.* Aged 19.—


Transported for Seven Years.
*The prisoner has been convicted five times.
