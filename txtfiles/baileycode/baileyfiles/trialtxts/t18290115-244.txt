





512. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     CRAIGEE
                  




               
             was again indicted for 


               stealing, on the 1st of January
, 1 piece of printed cotton, value 15s.; 1 shawl, value 6s.; 2 gowns, value 6s.; 1 clock, value 3s.; 1 pin, value 3d.; 9 yards of stuff, value 4s., and 1 pair of trousers, value 2s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  John 
                  Crawley
               




            .


                  SARAH 
                  CRAWLEY
               



            . I am the wife of 
               
                  John 
                  Crawley
               



            . I live in the same house with Mr. Penny; we have the second-floor room, and the prisoner slept on the first- floor; this property was in a box in my room, it was locked, but the key was in it: I and my mother went out on the 1st of January, and left the prisoner in care of the place, when we returned he was gone, and the property.


                  SAMUEL 
                  STEPHENS
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have a pin pawned with me on the 13th of December, by a man dressed like a soldier - I believe it was the prisoner.


                  JOHN 
                  TERRY AUSTIN
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have one shawl and this piece of cotton, which belong to this prosecutor.


                  LAWRENCE 
                  NOTLEY
               



            . I have one clock and one gown pawned by Roberts.


                  MARY 
                  ROBERTS
               



            . I pawned them by the prisoner's direction.
(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 24.




               Transported for Seven Years for each offence
            .
