





229. (M.) 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Sidnell 
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing two cloth coats, value 10 s. two cloth waistcoats, value 5 s. one pair of stone shoe buckeles, set in silver, value 4 s. and one pair of stone knee buckcles, set in silver, value 2 s.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Horton 
                  




                
            , Esq
; April 6
. *


                  John 
                  Horton 
               



            , Esq; The prisoner was my servant
. I went out on the 6th of April, and did not return till morning, at which time I missed my cloaths mentioned in the indictment; the prisoner was missing; he was taken; and charged with taking the things mentioned; and acknowledged he did.


                  Francis 
                  Rochford 
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. The prisoner brought these buckles on the 6th of April. (Produced in court, and deposed to by prosecutor.)


                  William 
                  James 
               



            , and 
               
                  Thomas 
                  Liney 
               



            , two pawnbrokers, produced each a coat and waistcoat, which they deposed they took in of the prisoner. (Deposed to by prosecutor.)
Mr. Dawson. Upon enquiring after the prisoner, I found he was gone to Gravesend; I went there and found him; he had paid half his passage to go to Holland; I charged him with taking the things mentioned; he acknowledged he had taken them; he has been about nine months in the first regiment of foot-guards; he behaved well as a soldier. The prosecutor is an officer in the same regiment.
The prisoner said nothing in his defence.



               Guilty
            . 


               T
            .
