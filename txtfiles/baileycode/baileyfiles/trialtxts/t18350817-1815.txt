



1815. 




WILLIAM BRIDGES
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
25th of July, one pair of half-boots, value 3s.; and one pinafore, value 1s.; the goods of 



Samuel Sprague, from the person of 



Frederick George Sprague.






MARY ANN SPRAGUE
. I am the wife of Samuel Sprague. I have a little boy six years and a half old—I sent him out with his boots and pinafore on—I went out and found him crying, without his boots and pinafore—we sent and found the prisoner—the little boy pointed him out—these are the boots.





WILLIAM SAMUEL CALVER
. I am a pawnbroker. I took in these boots of the prisoner—I asked him where be brought them from—he said from his mother's.




GUILTY
. Aged 13.—


Transported for Seven Years.
