





1626. 
               
                  
                     THOMAS 
                     HAYNES
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 31st of May
, 1 hat, value 5s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Samuel 
                     Gravenor
                  




               
            .


                  JAMES 
                  WEARING
               



            . I was in Brick-lane


 on the 31st of May, and saw the prisoner take a hat from Mr. Gravenor's shop, opposite Hanbury's brewhouse; I did not pursue him, but he was taken afterwards - I lost sight of him; I know he is the person.


                  JOHN 
                  KNIGHT
               



            . I saw the prisoner run away with the hat; I cried Stop thief! I know he is the boy.


                  ROBERT 
                  WARE
               



             (Police-constable K 170). I was at my own door at ten minutes past eight o'clock; I heard a cry of Stop thief! and saw the prisoner run by and another boy after him, with the hat; I pursued, and took the prisoner - the other boy told him he had stolen the hat.


                  JOHN 
                  HUMPHRIES
               



            . This is Mr. Samuel Gravenor's hat - I had it to sell for him.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 18. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
