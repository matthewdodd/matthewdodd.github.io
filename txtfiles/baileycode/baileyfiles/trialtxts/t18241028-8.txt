





London Cases, First Jury, Before Mr. Sergeant 
               Arabin
            .
1261. 
               
                  
                     HENRY 
                     KING
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 30th of September
, a handkerchief value 3 s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Francis 
                           Janvrin
                        




                     
                  , from his person
            .


                  FRANCIS 
                  JANVRIN
               



            . I am a merchant
. On the 30th of September, between three and four o'clock in the afternoon I was in Lime-street


, and felt a person at my coat pocket. I turned round and seized the prisoner with my handkerchief in his hand - he cried and begged for liberty.
Cross-examined by MR. PRENDERGAST. Q. He held it openly in his hand - A. Yes; he had not time to conceal it, for I turned round instantly.
(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I said I had picked it up; some boy went off directly. I never asked for mercy.
MR. JANVRIN. He had not time to pick it up.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 17.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
