





205. 
               
                  
                     JOSEPH 
                     DORMAN
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 2d of December
, 1 cloak, value 25s., and 12 yards of lawn, value 15s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Dent
                  




               
            .


                  WILLIAM 
                  HERBERT
               



            . I was going along Crawford-street


 on the 2d of December, at a quarter before six o'clock, and saw the prisoner and several boys round Mr. Dent's shop; he and another crawled on their knees, and took a cloak and something else out of a broken window; I had a full opportunity of seeing the prisoner - I went and told the shopman.


                  HENRY 
                  ROPER
               



            . I am shopman to Mr. 
               
                  John 
                  Dent
               



            . The witness came and said some boys had taken a cloak from the window which was broken about three feet from the door - I ran on the Quebec-street, and saw four boys on the opposite side the way; I heard one of them say." This won't do, we will have a b-y fight about it;" I ran over, and they all run down a mews - I pursued and overtook the prisoner, who happened to be the last, and to be carrying a bundle which contained this cloak and this piece of lawn which had been in the window in the morning.(Property produced and sworn to.)


                  RICHARD 
                  HARTLEY WALL
               



            . I was desired to take the prisoner.
Prisoner's Defence. I was running along; this gentleman took me back to the shop, and said I stole a cloak. 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 12.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
