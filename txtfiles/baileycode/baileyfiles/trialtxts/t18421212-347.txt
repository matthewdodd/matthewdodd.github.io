



347. 




ANN LOWE
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
10th of Dec., 2 dead fowls, value 4s. 6d., the goods of 



Thomas Halton and another; and that the had been before convicted of felony.






RICHARD STEEET
. I am a porter. On Saturday, the 10th of Dec., I was employed at Mr. Thomas Halton's, in Newgate-street—Peek called me, while I was at the end of the shop, and I saw two women going out—I was told to follow them—they parted, one went to the right, and the other to the left—I took one, and took her back 'to the shop—she is not here—I then went and took the prisoner, and found these two dead fowls under her cloak—I took her back to the shop—they are Mr. Halton's fowls.

Cross-examined by
MR. BALLANTINE. Q. Did you see her in the shop? A. I saw two women going out—I could not swear it was her, I only saw her back.





THOMAS HALTON
. Street came back with the prisoner—these are my fowls—I have one partner.

Cross-examined. Q. How do you know them? A. They were sent to we for sale—I never saw them till they were brought back.





THOMAS PEEK
. I am salesman to Mr. Halton. I missed two fowls—I saw them brought back by Street about a quarter of an hour after—they are just such as I missed them—I saw the prisoner in the shop.





OLIVER DEATH
 (City police-constable, No. 201.) I produce a certificate oi the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clark's office—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 44,—


Transported for Seven Years.
