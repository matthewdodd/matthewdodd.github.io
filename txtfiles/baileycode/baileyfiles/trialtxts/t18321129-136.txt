





137. 
               
                  
                     JAMES 
                     WOODCOCK
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 8th of November
, 12 brooms, value 3s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Ezekiel 
                           Delight
                        




                     
                  ; and that he had been before convicted of felony
            .


                  WILLIAM 
                  THOMAS
               



            . Mr. Delight is an oilman
. On the 8th of November I saw the prisoner take a bundle of brooms from his door; I told the prosecutor of it - I went after the prisoner, and wanted him to come back, but he would not - I was forced to get another person to help me.
Prisoner. I did not take them at all. Witness. I took him with them on his shoulder.


                  EZEKIEL 
                  DELIGHT
               



            . These are my brooms.


                  WILLIAM 
                  TOVEY
               



             (Police-constable E 113.) I took the prisoner.
JAMES WILMOT. I have a certificate, (which I got from Mr. Clark's office,) of the conviction of James Reeves, on the 1st of December, in the second year of His present Majesty's reign - he was sentenced to six months in the House of Correction; I took him, and know the prisoner is the person.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 23. - 


               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
