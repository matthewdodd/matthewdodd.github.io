





1384. 
               
                  
                     JAMES 
                     KENT
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 23rd of April
, 1 coat, value 10s.; 1 waistcoat, value 2s.; 1 pair of trousers, value 10s., and 1 sheet, value 3s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Thomas 
                     Kent
                  




               
            .


                  THOMAS 
                  KENT
               



            . I live in Satchwell's-rents, Bethnal-green


 - the prisoner is my son. On Easter Monday I went to work early in the morning; the prisoner came to me afterwards; I gave him leave to go home, and do some work - he took the key of my apartments; I returned a little after eleven o'clock in the forenoon; I found the key in the door - I went in, and missed a suit of clothes, which had been in a box in my room; I saw him again on the Thursday afterwards; I got an officer, and he was taken - I found a duplicate on the mantelpiece in the room.


                  ELIZABETH 
                  AGAR
               



            . I am the wife of Thomas Agar - we live in Cottage-court; I can see the steps of the prosecutor's door from our house. On Easter Monday I was standing on the steps of our door, and saw the prisoner come down the steps of his father's door with a bundle; he turned to the right, towards Church-street.
PHILIP COOK. I was in Church-street, Bethnal-green, on Easter Monday. I met the prisoner (who lives within three doors of me) going with a bundle towards Mr. Cassles, the pawnbroker.


                  HENRY 
                  HUDDY
               



             (Police-constable H 72). I took the prisoner on his father's premises.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 14. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
