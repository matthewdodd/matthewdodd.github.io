





261. (M.) 
               
                  Mary, wife
 of 
                     
                        John 
                        Murphy 
                     







            , was indicted for 


               stealing one cloth coat, value 10 s. one pair of worsted breeches, value 4 s. one pair of cloth breeches, value 1 s. and one cloth waistcoat, value 4 s.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     Charles 
                     Hay 
                  




                
            , May 30
. ||


                  Charles 
                  Hay 
               



            . I am a taylor
, and live in St. Martin's-lane. I lost the things mentioned in the indictment about a month ago, they were found again in the prisoner's custody.


                  Mary 
                  Pierce 
               



            . The prosecutor is a lodger with me; another lodger came down stairs and asked me, if I had sent any body up; and said, a strange woman was gone down with a bundle. I and my girl, named 
               
                  Hannah 
                  Hicks 
               



            , went out to see for her. She found her, and she was brought back with the things upon her.


                  Hannah 
                  Hicks 
               



            , I found the prisoner in the Strand and asked her, what she wanted at our house? she said, she wanted a mantua-maker. I took hold of her cloak, and called for assistance, and the people came about her, and we carried her back with the things mentioned in the indictment upon her.
Prisoner's Defence.
I deal in old cloaths, and had got a little drop of drink, and do not know that ever I saw that girl.



               Guilty
            . 


               T
            .
