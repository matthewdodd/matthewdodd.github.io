





239. 
               
                  
                     MARGARET 
                     HARTY
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 13th of October
, 6 bonnets, value 11s.; 6 picture-frames, value 7s.; 6 pictures, value 16s., and 1 dress, value 15s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Edward 
                           Russell
                        




                     
                  ; and that she had been before convicted of felony
            .


                  SUSAN 
                  RUSSELL
               



            . I am the wife of Edward Russell - we lodged in Tower-street, Seven-dials


. The prisoner lodged on the same floor; she hired the room on the Saturday, and on the Friday following she left - I missed the property the next morning; my husband is a clerk, but has not been with me for nine years.


                  ELLEN 
                  FOLEY
               



            . I live in Peter-street, Wardour-street, and deal in clothes. The prisoner came to me seven or eight weeks ago, and said she knew me, and knew my husband - I bought three bonnets of her; she came the week after, with another, which I would not buy - they were found in my window.


                  SAMUEL 
                  RUSSELL
               



            . These three bonnets are mine; I lost six, and six picture-frames and a dress - these are all that are found.
JAMES WARD (Police-constable C 172). I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction - I was present, and know she is the person.
Prisoner's Defence. I know nothing at all about the things; my husband took the room.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 35. - 


               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
