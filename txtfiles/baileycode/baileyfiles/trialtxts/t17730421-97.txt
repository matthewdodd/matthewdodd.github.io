





481. (L.) 
               
                  
                     JOHN
                      EMERTON
                  



               
             was indicted for 


               stealing a leather bridle with an iron bit plated with silver, value 10 s.
             the property of 
               
                  
                     Robert
                      Spencer
                  




               
            , March 5th
. ++


                  Richard
                   Breakspear
               



            . I am servant to Mr. Spencer, he is a horse dealer
: the bridle hung up in a stable; I saw it the 20th or 21st of February; I did not miss it till I saw it advertised in the paper; my master went up to the gentleman, and found the bridle; he is now in the country.


                  Joseph
                   Ray
               



            . On the 25th of February the prisoner brought this bridle (producing it.) to me to sell; I stopped it on suspicion, and advertised it; Mr. Spencer came and I delivered it to him.
Breakspear. I am sure it is my master's property; it came home the 20th or 21st of February.
Prisoner's Defence.
I was coming from the country and met with a man coming up, and he asked me to buy this bridle; I bought it for 9 d. when I came to London I went to sell it, and the gentleman stopped it.



               Guilty
            . 


               T
            .
