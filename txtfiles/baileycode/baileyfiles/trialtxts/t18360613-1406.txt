



1406. 




WILLIAM JONES
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
28th of May, 1 pair of boots, value 5s., the goods of 




Stephen Pollard.






STEPHEN POLLARD
. I am a shoe-maker, and live in Stafford-place South, Pimlico, and have a shop in Ormond-yard, York-street, St. James's On Saturday afternoon, the 28th of May, I was in the shop, and saw the prisoner take these boots from my door and run off—I ran after him, calling "Stop thief!"—he dropped the boots—I caught hold of him, and gave him to a policeman—I did not lose sight of him after he dropped them.

Prisoner. He told he Magistrate he saw a hand take them, but could not swear to me, Witness. No; I said I saw him take them.





JOHN MILLINGER BRIDE
. I am a policeman. The prosecutor gave the prisoner into my charge with the boots.

Prisoner. Q. Did not the Magistrate say the man who stopped me should be summoned? A. Yes—I served him with a summons, but he did not appear.
(Property produced and sworn to.)




GUILTY
.* Aged 17.—


Transported for Seven Years.
