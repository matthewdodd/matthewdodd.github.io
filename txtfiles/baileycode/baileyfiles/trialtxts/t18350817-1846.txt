



1846. 




JOHN WATSON
 was indicted

 for stealing 17 pairs of shoes, value 25s.; and one handkerchief, value 9d. the goods of 



Richard Lamb, his master, and that he had been before convicted of felony.






RICHARD LAMB
. I lived at No. 9, Prince's-street, Turk-street, Bethnal-green, and am a shoemaker—the prisoner was in my service Five or Six weeks—he left me on the 25th of July; about six o'clock that day I missed the shoes and handkerchief, I went and took him into custody—the shoes have not been found—this handkerchief is mine.





THOMAS SPENCER
 (policeman N 121.) I heard a cry of "Stop thief!" that night, and took the prisoner, who had this handkerchief round his neck, and in his pocket was 2s. 1 1/4d.





PHILIP CHETWOOD
 (police-constable G160.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got at Mr. Clark's office—the prisoner is the person. (read)


Prisoner. The persons where lodge urged me to do it—they live in Hackney-road—their name is Macklin.




GUILTY
. Aged 17.—


Transported for Seven Years.
