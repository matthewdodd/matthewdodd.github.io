



238. 




EDMUND LEFEVRE
 was again indicted

 for stealing, on the 
25th of October, 1 shin, value 6d.; 15 caps, value 5s.; 8 shirts, value 2s.; 3 handkerchiefs, value 9d.; 1 pair of stockings, value 6d.; 1 curtain, value 3d.; 2 rollers, value 2d.; and 5 yards of net, value 3d.; the goods of 



John Trapp.






MARY ANN TRAPP
. I am the wife of John Trapp, pf Charles-street. On the night of the 24th of October I left a child's shift and other linen hanging in my garden—I missed it the following morning.





WILLIAM MURRAY
. I am a policeman. On the morning of the 25th of October, I apprehended the prisoner—in the station-house his cap fell off—I found in it a child's shirt or shift, which Smith has.





WILLIAM SMITH
. I am a policeman. When the prisoner's cap fell off I took it up, and found this shift in it, wet—I asked him where he brought it from—he said, from Chatham, where be brought the rabbits from.





WILLIAM CROWLEY
. I am a policeman. I went to the garden at the back of Mr. Trapp's house, and found a bundle of wet linen in a shed there tied up. (Property produced and sworn to.)




GUILTY
 Aged 31.—


Transported for Seven Years.
