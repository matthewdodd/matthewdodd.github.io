






1690. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     JONES
                  





               
             and 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     THOMPSON
                  





               
             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 26th of June
, 1 waistcoat, value 5s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Plant
                  




               
            .


                  THOMAS 
                  FARRANT
               



            . I am a Police-constable. I was in Oxford-street


, on the 26th of June, between five and six o'clock in the evening; I saw the prisoners, and watched them - I saw them go to the prosecutor's door; Thompson stood looking in, while Jones took out two or three pins from the waistcoat, and took it down - I took Thompson, and Rice took Jones, with the waistcoat.


                  WILLIAM 
                  RICE
               



            . I took Jones, and took this waistcoat from under his coat.


                  WILLIAM 
                  VIVIAN ROE
               



            . I am shopman to Mr. 
               
                  William 
                  Plant
               



            . This is his waistcoat, and was on the rail at his door.
JONES - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 21.
THOMPSON - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 26.





               Transported for Seven Years
            .
