





237. 
               
                  
                     CHARLES JOHN 
                     FOX
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 17th of November
, 1 set of bed-furniture, value 2l. 5s.; 1 counterpane, value 10s.; 3 pillowcases, value 4s., and 1 towel, value 1s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           John 
                           Thomas
                        




                     
                  ; and that he had before been convicted of felony
            .



                  JOHN 
                  THOMAS
               



            . I live in Compton-street, Chelsea


; the prisoner lodged a fortnight with me. I had a large clothes-chest there, which was broken open, and a set of bed-furniture, a counterpane, and the other articles stated, taken from it; I was informed he had gone out, and took him in a public-house, offering the counterpane for sale.


                  JOHN 
                  BREEZE
               



            . My sister and I keep a public-house. On the 17th of November the prisoner came in, and offered some pillow-cases and a napkin to me, and to others who were in the tap-room; the prosecutor came in - he was then tying up his things.


                  JOHN 
                  HIGGS
               



            . The prisoner left a bundle of things at my house, between seven and eight o'clock. I keep the Pitt's Head, the corner of Old-street.(Property produced and sworn to)


                  JOHN 
                  ROBINSON
               



             (Police-constable G 57). I produce a certificate of the former conviction of the prisoner; I was a witness against him, and know he is the man.
The prisoner pleaded distress.



               GUILTY
            .* Aged 58. - 


               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
