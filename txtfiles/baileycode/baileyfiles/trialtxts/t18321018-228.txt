





2435. 
               
                  
                     MARY 
                     WILLIAMS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing on the 3rd of October
, 1 pair of boots, value 30s.; 1 waistcoat, value 10s., and 2 handkerchiefs, value 3s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Benjamin 
                     Wurr
                  




               
            .


                  BENJAMIN 
                  WURR
               



            . I live at Chelsea, and work at a stable
. I met the prisoner on the 2nd of October, I went with her to George-street, St. Giles'


, and went to bed with her - when I awoke in the morning she was gone; I missed my boots, waistcoat, and two handkerchiefs.
Prisoner. He told me to pawn them for what he had promised me, as he had but 21/2d. Witness I did not; she took them while I was asleep - I do not know whether she lodged there.


                  WILLIAM 
                  WOODMAN
               



             (Police-constable F 9). On the 23rd of October, between five and six o'clock in the morning, I stopped the prisoner in Drury-lane with a bundle, containing a pair of boots, a waistcoat, and handkerchief - the other handkerchief was tied round her thigh; she said they were her own, and that she had pawned a ring to get them out of pawn.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. He gave me them to pawn, and said William should fetch them back; I have known him two years.


                  BENJAMIN 
                  WURR
               



            . I knew her two years ago, when she lived at the White Hart, Holborn - she never pawned any thing for me; I never lived with her - I was sober.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 23. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
