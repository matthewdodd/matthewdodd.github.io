



1730. 




MARY ANN SWEENEY
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
24th of June, 1 necklace, value 5s., the goods of 




Thomas Gully, from the person of a certain female child of his unbaptized.






JULIA GULLY
. I am the wife of Thomas Gully, of Alban's Court, Chapel Street—he is a toy maker. I sent my baby out with a coral necklace on, the 24th of June—she came back without it—this is it.





ELIZABETH CAPON
. I am thirteen years old. I know the necessity of speaking the truth—I went out with Gully's child, and the prisoner in Chapel-street—she asked me to let her carry the child—it had the necklace on—I did not miss it till I got home—no one but the prisoner had the power of taking it—the child was not in my sight all the time the prisoner had it—she asked me to go up the back yard, which I did.





DAVID BLACKMORE
 (police-constable K 182). I went to search the prisoner's house—I found this necklace on her person—she denied that she had it.

Prisoner The lady said if I would give her the beads she would let me go.






JULIA GULLY
. I said if she would give me the beads I did not wish to hurt her.




GUILTY
. Aged 14.—


Transported for seven Years.
