



1895. 




THOMAS SWEENEY
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
3rd of July, 1 pair of boots, value 10s., the goods of 



Alexander Wilson and others.






GEORGE WATSON
. I am shopman to Alexander Wilson and others, of Cromer-street. Last Saturday night, at eleven o'clock, some one gave me information—I looked about, and saw the prisoner with a pair of boots in his hand, near the shop-door—I went out, overtook him, and saw him with the boots produced in his hand—they are my masters.





CHARLES SKINNER
. I am in the prosecutors' employ. About eleven o'clock on Saturday night I saw the prisoner drop the boots outside the door, I picked them up and brought them back.

Prisoner's Defence. I know nothing about them.




GUILTY
.* Aged 32.—


Transported for Seven Years.
