



1250. 




VELENTINE GANLY
 was indicted

 for stealing 1 penny, 3 halfpence, and 2 farthings the monies of 



George Collis, from the person of 



Elizabeth Collis; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






ELIZABETH COLLIS
. I am the wife of George Collis, of Clifton-street, Fitzroy-square. On the 6th May, about nine o'clock, I was with my mother in Tottenham-count-road, I felt something at my pocket and saw the prisoner draw his hand out—my pocket was turned inside out and my money gone—I called out, and followed the prisoner across the road—I called out, "Stop thief;" and he was stopped, I did not lose sight of him—I am sure be is the man—I had some halfpence in my pocket—that was all I had.





ELIZABETH JUPE
. I was with last witness in Tottenham-court-road when she lost the money—she pointed out the prisoner to me, he was secured.





RICHARD NEALE
 (policeman.) I received the prisoner in charge, took him to the station, and found nothing on him.

Prisoner's Defence. I passed the woman and she ran after me, and called stop thief; I bad nothing belonging to her, and did not touch her





ELIZABETH COLLIS

re-examined. There was nobody else near that I noticed—I had not put the halfpence into my pocket two minutes.





ANDREW WILLIAMSON
 (policeman.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction—(read—Convicted, August 1846, confined six months)—I was present at the trial—he is the person.




GUILTY
.(†) Aged 22.—


Transported for Seven Years.
