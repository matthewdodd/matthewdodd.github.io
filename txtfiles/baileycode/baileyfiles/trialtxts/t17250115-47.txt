







                     William 
                     Enfield 
                  



               
            , of Tottenham High-Cross


, was indicted for 


               breaking and entring the House of 
                     
                        
                           William 
                           Goodwin 
                        




                      
                  , no person being therein, and taking from thence two Coats and Pair of Breeches value 20 s. a Pair of Stays value 10 s. and other Things, the Goods of William Goodwin, on the 17th of Dec.
 last, about 9 in the Morning
            . It appear'd that 
               
                  John 
                  Archer 
               



             met the Prisoner in the Field, with the Goods upon his Back. The Prisoner enquired the Way to Walthamstow. They Pass'd, and Archer going a little farther, found that Goodwin's House was broke open: Upon which he turned back, pursued and took the Prisoner, who confess'd the Fact. 


               Guilty 4 s. 10 d
            . 


               Transportation
            .
