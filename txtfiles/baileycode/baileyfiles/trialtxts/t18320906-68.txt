





1794. 
               
                  
                     ALFRED 
                     APPLEFORD
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 11th of July
, 1 handkerchief, value 4s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           John Coxon 
                           Picken
                        




                     
                  , from his person- and that he had been before convicted of felony
            .
JOHN COXON PICKEN. I am in the employ of Messrs. Whitakers, of Ave Maria-lane. On the 11th of July, between ten and eleven o'clock at night, I was at the Holborn end of Farringdon-street


, and perceived a few boy
s playing on the pavement - as I passed them I felt a twitch at my pocket; I turned round, and caught hold of a lad, who said, "It was not me, Sir - it was that boy," pointing to the prisoner - I ran, and caught hold of the prisoner, but could not find my handkerchief - I gave him in charge; I had used it a few minutes before; it was found on him at the watchhouse.


                  WILLIAM 
                  LEE
               



            . I am an officer. The prisoner was brought to the watch-house - I found the handkerchief inside his clothes.(Property produced and sworn to.)


                  THOMAS 
                  PROTHERO
               



            . I have a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clark's office - (read) - I gave evidence against him, and am certain of him.
Prisoner's Defence. I saw another boy walking along - he threw the handkerchief down; I ran and picked it up.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 12. - 


               Transported for Life
            .
