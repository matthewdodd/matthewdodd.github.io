



2496. 




THOMAS GRIFFITHS
 was indicted

 for breaking and entering the shop of 



John Throgood Roffway, on the 
8th of Aug., at 
St. Michael, Wood-street, and stealing therein, 8 paintings and frames, value 28l.; his property, and that he had been before convicted of felony.






JOHN THROGOOD ROFFWAY
. I am a house painter, and have a shop at No. 9, Huggin-lane, Wood-street. I left my shop on the 8th of Aug.—I afterwards found that these paintings were gone—these are mine.





ROBERT RAMSEY
. I am shopman to Mr. Roffway. On the evening of the 8th of Aug., I left the shop locked up quite safe—I came back about nine o'clock the next morning—I found the back door open—it had been opened by a key, and these paintings were gone.





ROBERT KENDREW
. I am a pawnbroker. On the morning of the 9th of Aug., the prisoner pawned this painting for 30s.—on the Saturday following I saw an account in the paper that the painting was stolen—I sent to the prosecutor who identified it.

Prisoner. The master said he did not think it was me—they took me to the station, found the duplicate on me, and then the master swore I was the person, and this witness came the next day and swore I was the man. Witness. A man brought the prisoner to the shop—he doubled his fist at him and threatened him—I desired him to take him to the station, and tickets were found on him which correspond with the name he gave me, George Lovell.





ROBERT PEARSON
 (City police-constable, No. 547.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from Mr. Clark's office—(read)—the prisoner is the man.




GUILTY
. Aged 24.—


Transported for Ten Years.
