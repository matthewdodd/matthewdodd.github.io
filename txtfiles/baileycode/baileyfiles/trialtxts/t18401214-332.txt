




332. 




CHRISTOPHER WELCH
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
26th of November, 1 pair of boots, value 4s.; 1 jacket, value 3s.; 1 waistcoat, value 1s.; 6d.; 1 pair of stockings, value 1s.; and 1 handkerchief, value 6d.; the goods of 



George Thomas Dawson: and 1 pair of boots, value 3s., the goods of 



Eliza Ven; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






GEORGE THOMAS DAWSON
. I am a chimney-sweep. I lodge in Church-lane, St. Giles's—I took the prisoner in from charity, on the 22nd of November, and gave him lodging and victuals—when I got up in the morning on the 26th, he and the articles stated were gone.





EDWARD BARKER
 (police-constable E 124.) I took the prisoner, and found these articles on him.





JAMES BOWEN
. I am a pawnbroker. I took in this pair of boots of the prisoner on the 28th.





ELIZA VEN
. These boots are mine—the prosecutor lodged with me.

Prisoner. On the 25th of November the prosecutor handed me the key to open his master's cellar—when I got up in the morning he told me to put on his things, but instead of opening the cellar, I walked on to Aylesbury for work.





CHARLES POCOCK
 (police-constable F 116.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, from the Sessions-house, Clerkenwell.—(read)—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
. Aged 21.—


Transported for Seven Years.
