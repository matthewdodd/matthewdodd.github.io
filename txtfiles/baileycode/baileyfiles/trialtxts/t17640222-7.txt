Mr. Alderwick, a pewterer, in Fetter-lane, deposed, the prisoner brought three pint pewter pots to his shop to sell; he stopt them, and sent word to the two prosecutors, whose names were on the pots. (The pots were produced in court, and deposed to by the respective owners, 
               
                  Nicholas 
                  Best 
               



            , and 
               
                  William 
                  Bennit 
               



            , victuallers, in the Haymarket.
Prisoner's Defence.
I have always got my bread honestly; a woman gave me these pots to sell: I thought no harm at all; I carried them, and they stop'd me.



               Guilty
