





1157. 
               
                  
                     ROBERT 
                     BREM
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 1st of August
, one pair of half-boots, value 3 s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     George 
                     Turner
                  




               
            .


                  GEORGE 
                  DEAN
               



            . I am foreman to Mr. 
               
                  George 
                  Turner
               



            , who is a shoemaker
 at Ratcliff-highway


. On the 1st of August I heard a cry of Stop thief, looked out of the second floor window, and saw Jackson following the prisoner. The boots were stolen from the door.
(Property produced and sworn to.)


                  FRANCIS 
                  JACKSON
               



            . I am a beadle. About eight o'clock in the morning I saw the prisoner and others watching different shops. I saw the prisoner take these boots from Turner's shop, and put them under his jacket. I followed and secured him. He threw them down.
Prisoner's Defence. I have neither father nor mother, and do not know where to get bread.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 14.




               Transported for Seven Years. - 



                     Penitentiary
                  
.
Third Middlesex Jury, before J. Vaillant, Esq.
