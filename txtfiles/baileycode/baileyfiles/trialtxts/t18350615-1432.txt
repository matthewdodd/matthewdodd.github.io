



1432. 




JOHN YORKE
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
27th of May 1 handkerchief, value 2s., the goods of 



James Mears, from his person; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






JAMES MEARS
. I am a shopman. On the 27th of May, I was in Fleet-street, at half-past nine o'clock in the evening—I felt my pocket picked—I turned and laid hold of the prisoner, with my handkerchief in his hand—he dropped it, and I gave him into custody.

Prisoner. You said, "I think you have picked my pocket?" Witness. No such thing; I never said such a word.





WILLIAM HOWELL
. I took the prisoner into custody.





HENRY BAKER
. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's conviction. which I got at Mr. Clark's office—I know he is the man (read.)




GUILTY
. Aged 18.—


Transported for Fourteen Years.
