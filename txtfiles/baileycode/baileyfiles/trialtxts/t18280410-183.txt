





975. 
               
                  JAMES 
                  JOHNSON
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 28th of February
, 1 watch, value 4l.
            , the goods of 
               
                  John 
                  Cargill
               




            .
JOHN CARGILL. I am a mariner
, and belong to the Truewhich lay off Hermitage-stairs; the prisoner was engaged on board that vessel. On the 28th of February, I sent him on shore with my things to wash; he did not return - I went down and missed my watch from a drawer in my cabin; I had put it there about eight o'clock that morning; he was found at the Crooked Billet, public-house, with two women.


                  GEORGE 
                  DEVERELL
               



            . I am an officer, and took him in charge. I heard two women had been with him at the Crooked Billet - I went there and took them; the duplicate of the watch was found there, under the seat where he had been sitting.


                  ROBERT 
                  LINWOOD
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, and live in Ratcliff-highway; the prisoner pawned this watch, on the 28th of February.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I met a young man, and we went to have some beer. I heard the Theatre had fallen down. went to look at it; then went to another house, and was taken.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 19.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
