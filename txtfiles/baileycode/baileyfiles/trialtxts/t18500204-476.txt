




476. 




WILLIAM PAGE
 and 




GILBERT HEWSON



, stealing 2 pairs of stockings, value 2s.; the goods of 




Henry Thomas Hardin: Page having been before convicted.






HELEN HARDIN
. I am the wife of Henry Thomas Hardin; we keep a draper's shop in Church-street, Woolwich. On 30th Jan. we had some stockings outside the door—we missed two pairs—these are them—this ticket was on them.





BENJAMIN FISHER
 (policeman, R 272). On 30th Jan. I watched the prisoners—I saw them go to the prosecutor's shop, and each of them took a pair of stockings—I took them with them.





WILLIAM GLADWIN
 (policeman, R 122). I produce a certificate of Page's conviction at this Court—(read—Convicted April, 1849, having been before convicted—Confined one month and whipped)—Page is the person—he bears a very bad character—he has been in prison twenty times, and has drawn away about twenty different lads—Hewson has been in custody four or five times.

PAGE—


GUILTY
. Aged 17.—


Transported for Seven Years.


HEWSON—


GUILTY
. Aged 15.—



Confined Three Months.
