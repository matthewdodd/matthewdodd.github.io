



442. 




WILLIAM JONES
 was indicted

for stealing 1 gown, value 10s.; 1 shirt, 4s.; and 1 pair of stockings, 1s.; the goods of 



Thomas Hall; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






ANN HALL.
 I am the wife of Thomas Hall—the prisoner name to lodge with us early in Dec.—he left on the following Wednesday—on the following Sunday I missed a gown and a shirt from a box—these now produced are them.

Prisoner. I took the shirt out of pawn. Witness. I can swear to the make of it—I made it myself.





THOMAS HALL.
 I have seen this shirt—I wore it—it is mine—the stockings are mine also.





JOHN MAY
 (police-constable V 281.) I produce the shirt—I found it on the prisoner's back—the stockings were sent back to the prosecutor—the gown was found at the pawnbroker's.





SAMUEL ODELL.
 I am a pawnbroker—I produce this gown—it was pawned at our place by a man—I cannot say who.





WILLIAM HILL.
 I live iu Spring-grove, Wandsworth-road—I had a duplicate which I bought of the prisoner, but. I lost it—it was for a gown.

Prisoner. I bought the two duplicates over at Westminster—I took the shirt out of pawn on the following Staturday.





ALFRED SPICE
 (police-sergeant V 41.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction at this Court, by the name of William Lapham—(read—Convicted the 23rd of Feb., 1846, and confined six months)—the prisoner is the person—there were two other cases against him, but the parties would not attend.




GUILTY. Aged 18.—


Transported for Seven Years.
