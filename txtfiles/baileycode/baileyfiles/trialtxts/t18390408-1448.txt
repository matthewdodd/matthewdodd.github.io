



1448. 




THOMAS HOY
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
10th of April, 4 pairs of boots, value 1l. 2s., the goods of 



William Freeman.






WILLIAM FREEMAN
. I am a shoemaker, and live in Greenwich-road. I was sitting in my back parlour on the 10th of April, and in consequence of something ray wife told me I ran out of doors—I saw the prisoner rather running—I pursued him, and took hold of his collar—I said, what have you been taking? and at that moment I saw a boot fall from his person—I took it up, and with the boot and the prisoner I proceeded towards the station-house—Mr. Judson my neighbour came up—I wished him to go and see if there were not some more boots—be said, "If you will go I will hold the boy"—I went back, and close to where the prisoner had been standing when I took him, I found seven more boots—they were all mine.





JOHN JUDSON
. I am a licensed victualler. T was standing at my door cleaning a bird-cage—I saw the prisoner running along—I ran after him with the prosecutor—I saw one boot drop from the prisoner—I took him to the station-house.

Prisoner's Defence. I went up the gateway, and the gentleman came and said, "Have yon stolen any thing?" I said, "No"—he took me op the gateway and found the boot.




GUILTY
. * Aged 17.—


Transported for Seven Years.
