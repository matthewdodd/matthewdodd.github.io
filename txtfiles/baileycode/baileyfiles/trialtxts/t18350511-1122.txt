



1122. 




RICHARD HICKS
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
11th of April, 1 coat, value 30s., the goods of 



Richard Iverson.—2nd COUNT, stating it to he the goods of 



John Weston.






RICHARD IVERSON
. I am servant to Mr. John Weston, of High Holborn. On the 11th of April I attended my master's carriage to Finebury-circus—while I was there I received an alarm, and missed my livery great-coat, which had been provided for me by my matter.





WILLIAM NEWTON
. I was near the carriage, and saw the prisoner take the great-coat from the back of the carriage—the officer took him.





PETER KELLY
. I am an officer. I followed the prisoner, and took him when he had run about two hundred yards.
(Property produced and sworn to.)

Prisoner's Defence. A young man asked me to carry it to Moorgate.




GUILTY
.* Aged 19.—


Transported for Seven Years.
