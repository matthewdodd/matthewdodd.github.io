





660. 
               
                  WILLIAM 
                  ELMORE
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 3d of March
, a looking glass, value 36 s.
            ; the goods of 
               
                  John 
                  Forrs
               




            .
JOHN FORRS. I live in Cannon-street-road


, and am a cabinet maker
; the prisoner was a stranger to me; on the 3d of March, this looking glass was in my passage; I was in the front kitchen, and saw the prisoner walk away with it under his arm; I looked in the passage, and found it was gone; I followed and took him with it under his arm - he pleaded distress; the officer found some silver in his pocket.


                  JOHN 
                  EDWARDS
               



            . I am an apprentice to Mr. Forrs. I heard the glass had been stolen - I went out, and saw the prisoner who had then be taken; I know it to be my masters.


                  HENRY 
                  FARMER
               



            . am an headborough. I took the prisoner; and found two half crowns, one shilling and sixpence, in one pocket, and one penny farthing in the other; he said he did it through distress.
Prisoner's Defence. A young man gave me the glass to carry for him.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 21.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
