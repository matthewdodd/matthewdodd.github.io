





Third London Jury, before Mr. Common Sergeant.
960. 
               
                  
                     GEORGE 
                     SOWERBY
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 8th of May
, 1 handkerchief, value 3s., the goods of 
                     
                        Philip 
                        Hardwick
                     




                  , from his person
            .


                  THOMAS 
                  LONG
               



            . I live in Ely-court, Holborn, and am a watchman. On the night of the 8th of May, I was in Holborn


, and saw the prisoner following the prosecutor; there were others with the prisoner - I saw the prisoner pick the prosecutor's pocket of his handkerchief; I took him, he dropped the handkerchief, and I took it and him to the watch-house.
Prisoner. He stated that there was a person with me who ran away. Witness. There was a person with him, but the prisoner picked the pocket - I caught him with it in his hand.


                  PHILIP 
                  HARDWICK
               



            . I live in Russell-square. I lost this handkerchief in Holborn on the night of the 8th of May - I was told I had lost it, and then missed it - I had used it during the afternoon.
Prisoner's Defence. The other person took it, and threw it down.


                  John 
                  Mason
               



             and 
               
                  Eliza 
                  Shepherd
               



            , of Windsor-street, gave the prisoner a good character.



               GUILTY
            * Aged 19. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
