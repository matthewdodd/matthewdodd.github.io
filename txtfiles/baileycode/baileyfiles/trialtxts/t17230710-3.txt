







                     Sarah 
                     Mace 
                  



               
            , of St. Buttolph's Bishopsgate


, was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing divers Wearing Apparel, value 5l. in the Dwelling-house
             of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Rhodes 
                  




                
            , the 19th of June
 last. It appear'd by the Evidence, that the Prosecutor and his Wife being abroad, the Door was broken open, and the Goods stolen, and 2 Petticoats which were the Prosecutor's and stolen at that Time, were found upon the Prisoner when she was apprehended: That when the Fact was commited, she was seen to go up to the Prosecutor's Lodgings, and to come down again with a Pail of Dirty Water, which Pail she left at the End of the Alley, and went away. The Prisoner deny'd the Fact, and said the Prosecutor had before sent her to pawn them for her Daughter's Life, and that she had lent her them at the Time when she said she was robb'd; but this was deny'd by the Prosecutor. The Jury found her 


               guilty to the Value of 39 s.
            



               Transportation
            .
