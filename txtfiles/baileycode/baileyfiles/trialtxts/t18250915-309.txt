





1533. 
               
                  JOHN 
                  CLARK
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 11th of July
, a handkerchief, value 3 s., the goods of 
                     
                        John 
                        Gibbs
                     




                  , from his person
            .
JOHN GIBBS. I am in the employ of the Excise
, and live in Kinder-street, St. Georges's, in the East. On the 11th of July, between four and five o'clock in the afternoon, I was going from the Excise-office, Tower-street, home - I stood in Rosemary-lane


, about two minutes, to look at something, and felt a hand at my right hand coat pocket: I put my hand down and missed my handkerchief - I turned round and said to the prisoner, "You have picked my pocket;" he was the nearest person to me, and was close to me - he said, "I have not;" I laid hold of him, and in pulling his coat open, found the handkerchief concealed under his left arm; I took him to the watch-house - it happened on the north side of the way, eight or ten doors from the Minories.


                  JOSEPH 
                  STONE
               



            . I am a constable, and the prisoner was delivered to me; he allowed that the handkerchief was in his possession; he said, it was given to him by another person - the spot the prosecutor has described, is in the City; it extends fourteen or fifteen houses down.


                  JOHN 
                  LACY HAWKINS
               



            . I am a marshalsman; this spot is in the City.
Prisoner's Defence. It was given into my hand by a young man.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 17.




               Transported for Life
            .
