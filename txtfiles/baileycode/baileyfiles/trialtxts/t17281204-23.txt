







                     Thomas 
                     Powell 
                  



               
            , of St. Paul's Covent-Garden


, was indicted for 


               assaulting 
                     
                        
                           William 
                           Cooper 
                        




                      
                  , on the Highway, on the 15th of November
 last, and taking from him a Hat value 4 s.
            

The Prosecutor depos'd, That on the 15th of November, about 9 or 10 at Night, as he was going in at his own Door, the Prisoner came up to him, and snatch'd his Hat off, and running away with it, he cry'd out, stop Thief, upon which he was soon apprehended.
Mr. Bease depos'd, That he saw the Prisoner run away with the Hat, and being pursu'd, he flung it away soon after, whereupon this Deponent secur'd him.
James Glonfield depos'd, That he saw the Prisoner take off the Prosecutor's Hat, and run away with it, and pursuing him, saw him fall over a Heap of Rubbish, and then fling the Hat away; the Fact appearing plain, the Jury found him 


               Guilty of the Felony, but acquitted him of the Robbery
            .
