





1384. 
               
                  
                     SAMUEL 
                     DEAYTON
                  




               
             was again indicted for 


               stealing, on the 9th of July
, 1 coat, value 20s. and 1 waistcoat, value 8s. the goods of 
                     
                        
                           Ann 
                           Welsh
                        




                     
                  , his mistress
            .
MR. DOANE conducted the Prosecution.


                  ANN 
                  WELSH
               



            . I live in the parish of St. Pancras


 - three days after the loss of my money I missed a black coat and waistcoat, of my late husband's; they had been in the cupboard, in my kitchen, in which the money was deposited.


                  JAMES 
                  WELLS
               



            . I took the prisoner - I searched him, but found nothing; but I found, in a box, which he said was his, this duplicate, in a waistcoat pocket - it is for a coat and waistcoat, pawned in the name of 
               
                  Samuel 
                  Deayton
               



            , 46, Middlesex-street; the prisoner said, he knew nothing about them.


                  ROBERT 
                  MILTON
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have the coat and waistcoat, I cannot say who pawned them, but I gave the person this duplicate.
Prisoner's Defence. I know nothing about these things - I have been at the pawnbroker's, but it was to pawn my own property.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 19. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
