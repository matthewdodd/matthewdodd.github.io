






1331. 
               
                  HENRY 
                  SAMPSON
               




             and 
               
                  WILLIAM 
                  HAYNES
               




             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 4th of May
, 1 handkerchief, value 3s., the goods of 
                     
                        Thomas 
                        Gotobed
                     




                  , from his person
            .


                  THOMAS 
                  GOTOBED
               



            . I live in Russell-street, Bloomsbury. On the afternoon of the 4th of May I was walking down Gate-street, Lincoln's Inn-fields


; a person touched my coat pocket; I turned, and saw the two prisoners in custody of Goldstone - Sampson had my handkerchief concealed in his jacket; he gave it me himself - I delivered it to the officer.


                  JAMES 
                  GOLDSTONE
               



            . I am an umbrella-maker, and live in Gate-street. On the 4th of May I was in my shop, and saw the prosecutor pass, followed by the prisoners -I saw Haynes lift up the prosecutor's pocket; I left my shop to go and caution him, but before I got to him Haynes took the handkerchief, and gave it to Sampson; I took them both - by my direction Sampson gave up the handkerchief.
JOHN GALLAHER. I am a Police-constable. I took the prisoners.
(Property produced and sworn to.)
SAMPSON - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 18.
HAYNES - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 18.





               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
