



815. 




ALFRED BRYAN



, stealing 1 handkerchief, value 2s.; the goods of 



David Innis Ure, from his person; having been before convicted.






ALFRED LAMB
. I am a wine-merchant. I was in Crutched-friars on 11th March, about a quarter-past four o'clock, and saw the prisoner take the handkerchief out of the prosecutor's pocket, and put it into his own—I took hold of him—he took it out, threw, it down, and said another boy dropped it.





DAVID INNIS URN
. This is my handkerchief; I had it in my pocket—Mr. Lamb came and informed me that I had lost it.





GEORQE CROUCH
 (City policeman). I took the prisoner; I found this other handkerchief on him.

Prisoner's Defence. I was walking along—a young man took the handkerchief, and gave it to me—when I was taken, I threw it down—the young man ran away.





MICHAEL HAYDON
. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's conviction at this Court—(read—Convicted April, 1849, having been before convicted, unfitted twelve months)—I was present; he is the person—I stopped him on London-bridge on 30th Dec, and found six handkerchiefs in his hat—he then had one month.




GUILTY
 Aged 16.—


Transported for Seven Years.
