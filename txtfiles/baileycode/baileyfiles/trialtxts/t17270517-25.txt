







                     Richard 
                     Prickloe 
                  



               
            , of St. Dunstan's Stepney


, was indicted for 


               privately and feloniously stealing 10 pound of unwrought Silk, value 10 l.
             on the 21st of April
 last, the Goods of persons unknown.


                  Thomas 
                  Wood 
               



             depos'd, That the Prisoner being a Servant
 to his Partner Mr. Gale, was interested with Silk, which Silk being embezzl'd; they searched his Trunk on Suspicion, and found a parcel of Silk there, which he the prisoner could not account for: Upon this, taking him before a Magistrate, he confest he had followed that vile practice for some Time, and had sold several parcels of Silk to one 
               
                  William 
                  Read 
               



            , of Whitegate-Alley, who knew it to be stolen, and encouraged him and his Fellow Servant in the Villany. The Fact appearing plain, the Jury found him 

               guilty
             of single Felony.
