







                     George 
                     Hogrel 
                  



               
            , of St. Anns Westminster


, was indicted for 


               feloniously stealing a silver Salt, value 10 s. 8 d.
             the Goods of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Canaway 
                  




                
            , on the 7th of Nov
, ber last. The Prosecutor depos'd, that the prisoner (being a Fidler) was sent for, to play at his House, at which time he took an opportunity to convey away the Salt. That missing the Salt the next Day, his Wife, upon enquiry, found it at a Goldsmiths, where the prisoner had sold it, by which means the Person that stole it was discover'd. The prisoner said he was drunk, and knew nothing of the Matter, till the next Day coming to himself, he found the Salt in his Pocket. 


               Guilty to the value of 4 s, 10 d.
