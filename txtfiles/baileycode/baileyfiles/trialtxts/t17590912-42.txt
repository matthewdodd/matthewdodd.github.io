





289. (M.) 
               
                  
                     Mary 
                     Cooper 
                  



               
            , widow
, was indicted for 


               stealing 4 cotton gowns, value 10 s. 2 linnen sheets, value 6 s. 2 petticoats, 1 pair of stays, one linnen table-cloth, and 2 linnen aprons
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Jane 
                     Fox 
                  




                
            , widow
, Aug. 8
.*
Jane Fox. I live in Whitechaple


 parish. I had left my house in the care of 
               
                  Eleanor 
                  Bright 
               



            , the goods mentioned were taken out of a chest in my room. The prisoner was taken up with one of the gowns on her back, and she confessed where she had carried the rest of the things; produc'd in court and depos'd to.


                  Eleanor 
                  Bright 
               



            . I had the care of Mrs Fox's house. I went out on the rejoicing night, when I returned the house was broke, and the goods mentioned were gone.


                  Thomas 
                  Jigget 
               



            , the Constable, Deposed to the taking up the prisoner, and finding the things by her direction.
The prisoner said nothing in her defence.



               Guilty
            .
