



1170. 




WILLIAM POLLARD
, jun. was indicted

for stealing, on the 
21st of April, 2 gowns, value 5s.; 1 shawl, value 2s.; and 1 shift, value 1s.; the goods of 



William Pollard.






WILLIAM POLLARD
. I am the prisoner's father, and am a basket-maker, and work in Fleet-lane—I have four children—the prisoner lived at home with me. On the 21st of April he boarded and lodged with me—my daughter and wife missed this property on the morning of the 22nd, and on the 23rd, at night, when he came home, I gave him in charge for stealing it.





GEORGE MEEK
. I live at Mr. Clarges, a pawnbroker, in Aldersgate-street. I have a gown and shawl pawned on the 21st of April for 2s.—I cannot swear to the prisoner—I have seen him at our shop.






JOHN LEACH
. I am shopman to Mr. Martin, pawnbroker, on Snow-hill. I produce a gown and shift pawned by the prisoner—the shift on the 18th, and gown on the 21st of April.





ELIZABETH POLLARD
. I am the prisoner's mother—this property is all ours—I never gave the prisoner permission to pawn any thing.




GUILTY
. Aged 20.—


Transported for Seven Years.
(The prosecutor stated that the prisoner had been in the habit of robbing him for five years, and that he had been ruined by attending private theatres.)
