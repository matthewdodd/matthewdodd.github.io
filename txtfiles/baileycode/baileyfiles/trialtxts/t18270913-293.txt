





1879. 
               * THOMAS
               



             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 29th of August
, 2 shoes, value 6s.,
             the goods of 
               
                  James 
                  Darvill
               




            .
JAMES DARVILL. I am a shoemaker
, and live at Uxbridge. On Wednesday morning, the 29th of August, I missed two shoes off my counter - I had seen them on the 27th; the witness Thomas brought them to me.


                  RICHARD 
                  THOMAS
               



            . I am a tailor, and live at Uxbridge. On the 29th of August the prisoner came into my shop, and was in the act of taking a very large parcel off the counter - he came to sell matches. I saw these shoes sticking out of his pocket; I took them from him - he said they were his - I gave him in charge.


                  JOHN 
                  TAYLOR
               



            . I am a constable, and took him in charge.(Property produced and sworn to.)
The prisoner being a native of Otaheite, had the evidence communicated to him by an interpreter, through whom he pleaded distress.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 19.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
* The prisoner had no other name.
