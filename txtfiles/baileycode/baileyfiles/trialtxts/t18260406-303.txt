






876. 
               
                  ABRAHAM 
                  BATES
               




             and 
               
                  WILLIAM 
                  BRIGGS
               




             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 8th of March
, 1 handkerchief, value 3s. the goods of 
                     
                        David 
                        Richardson
                     




                  , from his person
            .


                  DAVID 
                  RICHARDSON
               



            . I was in Leadenhall-street on the 8th of March, and a person told me my pocket was picked; I missed this handkerchief, which I had safe two minutes before.


                  ROBERT 
                  HESKETH
               



            . I was in Leadenhall-street on the 8th of March, and saw the two prisoners near the prosecutor - Briggs drew the handkerchief from his pocket, and handed it to the other; it was then thrown on the ground - I secured them, and called to the prosecutor. I found a watch on Bates.
BATES' Defence. I saw this young man passing a cart, and the officer came and said I had taken the handkerchief.
BRIGGS' Defence. I had not had the handkerchief.
BATES - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 16.
BRIGGS - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 17.





               Transported for Seven Years
            .
