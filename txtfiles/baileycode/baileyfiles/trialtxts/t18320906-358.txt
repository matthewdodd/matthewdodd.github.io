





2089. 
               
                  
                     ELIZA 
                     BROWN
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 26th of July
, 1 table-cloth, value 20s.; 12 handkerchiefs, value 1l.; 21 napkins, value 3l.; 2 shifts, value5s., and 2 aprons, value 3s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Willshen
                  




               
            .


                  FRANCES 
                  WILLSHEN
               



            . I am the wife of 
               
                  William 
                  Willshen
               



             - we live in Middlesex-street


. The prisoner was living in our house as servant
; she lived there five days - she went away without notice, and then I missed these articles.


                  SIMON 
                  TAMPION
               



            . I am a shoemaker; I lodge in the house of the prosecutor. I saw the prisoner go out at ten minutes past seven o'clock, on the 26th of July - she had a bundle with her, and never returned.


                  JOSEPH 
                  TURWEN
               



            . I am a pawnbroker. I have a tablecloth pawned by the prisoner on the 1st of August.


                  CHARLES 
                  MAYHEW
               



             (Police-constable E 152.) I have some other articles, which I got from different pawnbrokers.(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 38. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
