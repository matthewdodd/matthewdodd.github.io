



1203. 




JOSEPH FARNORTH
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
18th of April, 1 coat, value 2l., the goods of 



Mary Wilds—2nd COUNT, stating it to he the goods of 




William Green.






WILLIAM GREEN
. I am coachman to Miss Mary Wilds, of Clarges-street. About two o'clock, on the 18th of April, as I was going from the stable to the door, I saw the prisoner on the step behind the carriage—I stopped my horse directly I saw him, and told him to be off—he jumped off, and took my coat with him across the street, and was putting it into a bag that he had—I an sure he is the man—he was stopped by three persons—he struggled, and got from them twenty yards, and was then taken again.





WILLIAM TODD
. I was in Clarges-street. and saw the prisoner take the coat from the dickey behind—I pursued—he dropped it at the top of Sun-court, and I took him.





WILLIAM WHITING
 (police-constable, C 81) I took the prisoner, and have the coat.

Prisoner. I was going along the street, and this coat fell off—I took it up.




GUILTY
.* Aged 19.—


Transported for Seven years.
