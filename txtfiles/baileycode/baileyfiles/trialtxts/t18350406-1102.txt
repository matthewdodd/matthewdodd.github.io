



1102. 




HENRY THOMPSON, alias
 Preston

, was indicted

for stealing, on the 
21st of February, at 
St. Mary, Lambeth, Surrey, 1 waistcoat, value 6s., the goods of 



William Hickinbottom; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






WILLIAM CLARKE
. I am in the employ of William Hickinbottom, a pawnbroker, in New-cut, Lambeth. On the 21st of February the prisoner was brought in with this waistcoat, which had been inside the shop—it if my master's.





EDWARD LANGLEY
. I am a policeman. On the 21st of February I saw the prisoner, in company with another, pawing the prosecutor's shop—I had my eye on them for some time—I saw the prisoner reach his arm inside

the door, snatch down the waistcoat, and walk away with it under his arm—the other remained standing in front of the shop—I secured the prisoner with the waistcoat under his arm.

Prisoner. The other boy took the jacket, and gave it to me.





ROBERT GOOSE
. I am a policeman. I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction, which I got from the office of the Clerk of the Peace, at Westminster (read)—I was a witness against him—he is the boy who was convicted in the name of 


Joseph Preston—he was whipped three times, and confined for eighteen months.




GUILTY
.* Aged 15.—


Transported for Fourteen Years.

Third Jury, before Mr. Justice Gaselee.
