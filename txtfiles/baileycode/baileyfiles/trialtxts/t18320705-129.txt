





1552. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     HARDING
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 11th of June
, 1 pair of boots, value 4s. 6d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     John 
                     Cotterell
                  




               
            .
JOSEPH HUMPHREYS. I live with Mr. John Cotterell, a shoemaker
, in Leather-lane


. I saw the prisoner unhook these boots, and take them down, between ten and eleven o'clock in the morning, on the 11th of June - they had been inside the shop; he had not got past the shop window- when I collared him he dropped them; I called my master, who sent for the Policeman.


                  RICHARD 
                  BAYLIS
               



             (Police-constable G 67). I took the prisoner.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. I saw them under the window, and took them up.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 17. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
