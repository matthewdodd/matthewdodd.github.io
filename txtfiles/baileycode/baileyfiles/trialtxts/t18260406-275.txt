





Middlesex Cases, Fourth Jury, Before Mr. Common Serjeant.
856. 
               
                  TRIPHEMA 
                  BARTHOLOMEW
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 16th of February
, at Paddington, 1 sheet, value 4s.; 1 table-cloth, value 2s., and 1 blanket, value 5s., the goods of 
                     
                        William 
                        Daniels
                     




                  , in a lodging-room, in his dwelling-house, against the statute
            .
WILLIAM DANIELS. I live in Bray-street, Paddington


. The prisoner came to lodge at my house on the 16th of February - the articles stated were let with the room; she remained with me till the 2d of March - I then gave her notice to quit; when she went she took the key of the room-door, and a bonnet belonging to my wife; I got an officer to open the door, and missed the articles.


                  THOMAS 
                  PROBERT
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, and live in the Edgware-road. I took a blanket in pledge of the prisoner - I have a sheet and table-cloth - the ticket for the sheet is my writing; I do not know who pawned them.(Property produced and sworn to.)


                  MICHAEL 
                  MORRIS
               



            . I am an officer. I produce the duplicates of the blanket which I found in the prisoner's room.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 17.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
