



1310. 




RICHARD LETTS
 was indicted

 for stealing 1 hat, value 1s.; 1 pair of shoes, 6s.; 4lbs. weight of beef, 4s., 1 loaf of bread, 4d.; and 1 tobacco-box, 8d.; the goods of 



William Cowland; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






WILLIAM COWLAND
. I live at Pleasant Retreat, Brixton-hill. I am a brick maker—the prisoner came to lodge with me on the 27th of Feb.—he lodged there twp nights, and left just before four o'clock in the morning, on the 1st of March—I came down and missed my hat, my shoes, 4lbs. weight of beef, a 2lb. loaf, and a tobacco-pipe—no one else had been there—I found the prisoner in April, at the Three Goats' Heads, in the Walworth-road—he had my hat on his head—he had left his own hat on the table at my house—this is my hat.


prisoner. Q. How do you know it? A. By a place I broke in front of it—I have worn it for months—I can swear to it, but it was not in the condition in which it is now—the prisoner wore it a month or upwards before I found it.

prisoner. This hat I bought myself; there were lodgers in the house when I went out. Witness. They were in bed—there was only my son in the house, and one lodger, who has been with me three or years—I can swear the door was locked after the prisoner—I found it fast.





GEORGE EDWARD SPURRELL
 (police-constable 257.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction at Kingstom—(read—Convicted 19th Oct. 1841and confined twelve month)—the prisoner is the man.




GUILTY
. Aged 35.—


Transported for seven Years.

(There was another indictment against the prisoner.)
