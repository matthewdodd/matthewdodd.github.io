







1055. 
               
                  
                     MARY 
                     ROBERTS
                  



               
             and 
               
                  
                     ANN 
                     SLANEY
                  




               
             were indicted for 


               stealing, on the 21st of February

, 1 gown, value 10s.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Mary 
                     Hughes
                  



               
            ; and 
               
                  
                     PHOEBE 
                     JUGGINS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               feloniously receiving the same, well knowing it to be stolen
            .
MARY HUGHES. I am single
, and keep a chandler shop
 in St. Ann-street



. This gown hung in my yard on the 21st of February, to dry: I saw Roberts and Slaney come to the shop, and have some bread and dripping - I looked out after they had left, and the gown was then safe - I missed it about one o'clock.


                  LEONARD 
                  GEORGE NEEDES
               



            . I am a pawnbroker, and live in Bridge-road, Lambeth. I have a gown pawned by Juggins, in company with Slaney, on the 21st of February - I lent 4s. on it; she said it was for her mother.


                  CHARLES 
                  ROBERT BEDWELL
               



            . I am an officer. On the morning of the 21st of February I was on my beat -I saw Slaney and Roberts walking with a basket, but I do not know what they had - I went on the 22nd to Slaney's house, and said I wanted her; she said to me directly,"You must take the other girl" - I said, "What girl?" she said, "Roberts" - I went with her to Roberts, and took her; I found 1s. 5d. on her, but no duplicate.


                  GEORGE 
                  GRIFFITH HOWELS
               



            . On the 23rd of February I was on duty - Joggins came to me, to the watch-house, and said she believed she was wanted for stealing agown, which two girls had stolen two days before - I took her.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Slaney's Defence. A water-cress man gave it to me to pawn.
SLANEY - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 17.
JUGGINS - 

               GUILTY
            . Aged 19.





               Transported for Seven Years
            .
ROBERTS - 

               NOT GUILTY
            .
