





1638. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     MARRIOTT
                  




               
             was again indicted for 


               stealing, on the 26th of October
, 1 chaise, called a stanhope, value 30l.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     William 
                     Snow
                  




               
            .


                  JOHN 
                  SNOW
               



            . I am the son of 
               
                  William 
                  Snow
               



            . On the 19th of October the prisoner hired this stanhope for one month, at 3l. a month - it was worth 30l.; it has never been returned.
Prisoner. When I hired it I asked you the price, and you said it would be 30l. if I kept it. Witness. He never said a word about the price.


                  WILLIAM 
                  SNOW
               



            . I found the stanhope at Mr. Robinson's, advertised for sale; it had not been returned - I have not received a farthing for the hire.


                  HENRY 
                  AGATE
               



            . I am a clerk at Robinson's repository. The prisoner brought this stanhope on the 26th of October, for sale; I advanced him 10l. on it - it has not been sold; it is now in our possession - the prisoner has not applied for it.
Prisoner's Defence. I hired it to let out; the price was to be 30l. if I bought it - I have tickets to prove it was let out on hire - when I was taken I was allowed three days to settle this, but could not find friends.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 46.




               Transported for Seven Years, to commence from the expiration of the former sentence
            .
