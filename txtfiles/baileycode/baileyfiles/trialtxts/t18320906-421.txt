





Fourth Middlesex Jury, before Mr. Sergeant Arabin.
2152. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     SMITH
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 8th of August
, 1 handkerchief, value 1s., the goods of 
                     
                        
                           John 
                           Smith
                        




                     
                  , from his person
            .
JOHN SMITH. I live in the Commercial-road, and am a captain of a vessel
. On the 8th of August, at half-past ten o'clock in the evening; I was walking in Grenada-terrace, Commercial-road


 - I felt a tug at my pocket, I turned and saw the prisoner with my handkerchief in his hand; I said, "You scoundrel what are you about?" he immediately ran away - a young gentleman took him; I went up and found him in possession of the officer: I took up my handkerchief.


                  THOMAS 
                  SMITHERS
               



             (Police-constable K 101). I was on duty, and saw the prisoner running - I took him; this handkerchief was picked up.
Prisoner. Q. Did I turn the corner with it? A. I did not see you drop it; I saw you run and turn one corner: they were pursuing you - I did not lose sight of you.(Property produced and sworn to.)



               GUILTY
            . Aged 15. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
