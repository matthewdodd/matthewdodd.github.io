



1199. 




CHARLES NASH
 was indicted

for stealing, on the 
1st of March, 3 blankets, value 12s.; 2 sheets, value 6s; 2 pillow-cases, value 5s.; and 1 table-cover, value 2s.; the goods of 



Hannah Proudfoot.






HANNAH PROUDFOOT
. I am a widow, and live in Great Chart-street, Hoxton. The prisoner lodged with me from Thursday till Monday—he was to pay 7s. a week—I let the articles stated with the lodging—he was taken from my house on another charge, and then I missed these things.





JOHN CHAMBERS
. I am servant to Mr. Thimbleby, a pawnbroker. I have a blanket, two sheets, a pillow-case, and a table-cover, which were pawned on the 1st and 2nd of March, by the prisoner.
(Property produced and sworn to.)




GUILTY
. Aged 28.—


Transported for Seven Years.
(There were two other indictments against the prisoner.)
