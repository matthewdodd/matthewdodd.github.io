





245. 
               
                  
                     THOMAS 
                     JONES
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 28th of November
, 1 pair of trousers, value 4s. 6d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Samuel 
                     Dias
                  




               
            .


                  SAMUEL 
                  DIAS
               



            . I live in Brick-lane


. These trousers were hanging on a pole, tied round with a rope - I did not see them taken, but my attention was drawn by my servant - the prisoner was brought back after I gave information.


                  FRANCES 
                  FLOOD
               



            . I live with the prosecutor - I cannot swear to the prisoner, but I saw a hand go up, and the trousers pulled down - I gave information, and saw the prisoner brought back to the house afterwards.


                  LEWIS 
                  HARRIS
               



            . The prosecutor on Wednesday, the 28th, called at my master's, and said he had lost a pair of trousers - the prisoner afterwards came and offered the trousers for sale, and I gave information.



                  THOMAS 
                  FENTON
               



            . I was sent by Harris to the prosecutor, with the prisoner and the trousers.(Property produced and sworn to.)
Prisoner's Defence. My brother came home and asked me to go to buy him a waistcoat - I was going down Petticoat-lane, and saw a man with these trousers; he asked 6s. for them, and taking some of my brother's money, I went to pawn them, to get the money back.



               GUILTY
            .* Aged 21. - 


               Transported for Seven Years
            .
