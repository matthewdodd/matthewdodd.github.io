





443. (M.) 
               
                  
                     Elizabeth 
                     Procter 
                  



               
            , spinster
, was indicted for 


               breaking and entering the dwelling-house of 
                     
                        
                           John 
                           Lambert 
                        




                      
                  , on the 3d of Sept
. about the hour of one in the night, and stealing two shillings in halfpence, his property
            . +
The prosecutor is a victualler
 at Radcliff, he deposed, that he went to bed, and left his house safe, on the 3d of this instant, he heard a noise between 4 and 5 in the morning, he went down, and heard somebody walk down before him into the cellar; he opened the door and called thieves, his neighbours came, he found a cellar window, which had 5 iron stancels, one was broke, and saw the prisoner in the cellar betwixt 2 butts; he missed 2 s. in half-pence; she owned she got in, and upon hearing him come down stairs, she went and hid herself in the cellar; she had been at the till, and it was cut; a knife was found in the cellar, where she stood.




               Guilty of stealing 2 s. 
            

acquitted of the burglary. 


               T.
