



1172. 




HENRY JONES
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
9th of April, at 

St. Bridget, otherwise Bride, 1 watch, value 3l.; 1 watch-chain, value 5s.; 1 watch-key, value 2l.; 1 seal, value 5s.; 4 rings, value 4l.; 3 lockets, value 3s.; 1 snuff-box, value 1s.; 1 flask, value 8s.; 1 breast-pin, value 5s.; 1 vinegrette, value 15s.; 2 spoons, value 6s.; 1 purse, value 1s.; 6 threepences, 4 twopences, 50 pennies, 7 sovereigns, 3 half—sovereigns, and 1 £5 Bank-note; the property of 



Henry Wix, his master, in his dwelling-house; and afterwards, about the hour of four in the night of the 
9th of April, burglariously breaking out of the same dwelling-house; to which he pleaded





GUILTY
. Aged 15.—


Transported for Ten Years.
