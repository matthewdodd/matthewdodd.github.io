





1314. 
               
                  
                     JOHN 
                     LAWRENCE
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 7th of April
, 1 yard of floor-cloth, value 4s., the goods of 
                     
                        Samuel 
                        Ames
                     




                  ; and that he had been before convicted of felony
            .
MARY BRANBURY. I live near Mr. 
               
                  Samuel 
                  Ames
               



            ' shop, at Islington


. On the 1st of April I saw the prisoner shake a piece of floor-cloth; he shook it a second time, then put it down by his side, covered it with some cloth, and took it away from the stone coping.
RICHARD REYNOLDS. I work for Mr. Ames. Branbury's husband informed me of this; I pursued the prisoner about one hundred yards; he had this floor-cloth, and was walking off - he gave it to me, and I took him.


                  WILLIAM 
                  HOLT
               



             (Police-constable N 17). I took the prisoner.(Property produced and sworn to.)


                  JAMES 
                  CHAMBERS
               



            . I am a Police-constable. I produce a certificate of the conviction of the prisoner, which I got at Mr. Clark's office; I was a witness, and know he is the man.
Prisoner. I was in distress.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 55. - 


               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
