





830. 
               
                  
                     WILLIAM 
                     DAVIS
                  




               
             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 27th of February
, 2 iron hinges, value 2s. 6d.
            , the goods of 
               
                  
                     Samuel 
                     Batten
                  




               
            .


                  PETER 
                  CLARK
               



            . I am a gardener. I saw the prisoner near Mr. Batten's garden, at Dalston


, with a pair of hinges; Mr. Batten saw him, and told me to keep in sight, while he went to see if his hinges were gone, which they were - I followed the prisoner to near the canal-bridge, and then asked where he got the hinges; he said he found them in Dalston-lane - I asked him to come back: we met Mr. Batten, who identified them.
Prisoner. Q. Did I say I picked them up? A. Yes, you did, and you gave them up.


                  SAMUEL 
                  BATTEN
               



            . My garden is in Kingsland-road I was with Clark, and saw the prisoner; I thought he had got my hinges, which I had seen safe about an hour before - I looked, and they were gone; he was then pursued and taken; my hinges had been at a house for sale, as I did not want them.
Prisoner's Defence. I had returned from the King's Head public-house, through the garden ground; I saw these hinges, and took them up.



               GUILTY
            . Aged 21.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
