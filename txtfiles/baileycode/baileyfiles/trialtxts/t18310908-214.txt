





First Middlesex Jury, before Mr. Justice Patteson.
1741. 
               
                  JOHN 
                  BROWN
               




             was indicted for 


               stealing, on the 4th of August
, 1 watch, value 3l. 10s.; 2 seals, value 2l. 2s.; 2 rings, value 1l., and 2 keys, value 10s., the goods of 
                     
                        William Henry 
                        Smith
                     




                  , in his dwelling-house
            .


                  JOHN 
                  PHIPPEN
               



            . I am a Policeman. On the 4th of August, at half-past four o'clock in the afternoon, I saw the prisoner run out of Mr. Smith's shop, and run along the street - I followed him a long way, and caught him; he had his hand in his waistband - I pulled it out, and he had this watch and seals.


                  WILLIAM 
                  HENRY SMITH
               



            . I am a butcher
, and live in Back-road, Shadwell


. On the 4th of August, about four o'clock in the afternoon, I went out, leaving this watch in the room behind the shop; I was fetched in about three quarters of an hour, and they were gone - I gave three guineas and a half for the watch four years ago, one for each seal, and 10s. for the keys; one is only gilt.(Property produced and sworn to.)




               GUILTY of stealing to the value of 99s. only
            . Aged 18.




               Transported for Seven Years
            .
