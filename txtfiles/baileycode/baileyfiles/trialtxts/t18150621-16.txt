





697. 
               
                  
                     SAMUEL 
                     GILBERT
                  




               
             was indicted for having 


               feloniously disposed of, and putting away, twenty forged and counterfeit one-pound notes, purporting to be the notes of the 
                     
Governor and Company of the Bank of England




                  , with intent to cheat the said Governor and Company
            .
To this indictment the prisoner pleaded
NOT GUILTY.
SECOND COUNT. Charging him with having forged notes in his possession, knowing them to be forged.
To this indictment the prisoner pleaded




               GUILTY
            , aged 27.




               Transported for Fourteen Years
            .
London Jury, before Mr. Recorder.
