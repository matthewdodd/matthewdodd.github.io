



1526. 




JAMES M'CLAY
 was indicted

 for stealing, on the 
27th of April, 1 chronometer and case, value 40l., the goods of 

our Lady the Queen.—2nd COUNT, stating them to be the goods of the 

principal officer of her Majesty's ordnance; to which he pleaded





GUILTY
. Aged 24.—


Transported for Seven Years.
