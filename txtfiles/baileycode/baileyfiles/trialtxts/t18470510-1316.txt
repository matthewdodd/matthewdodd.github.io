



1316. 




WILLIAM LANE
 was indicted

 for stealing 3 pairs of booth, value 1l.; the goods 



Henry Muggeridge; and that he had been before convicted of felony.






JAMES HILLS
. I am assistant to Mr. Henry Muggeridge—he lives Tooting—I saw some booth safe on a board outside his shop, on the 24th April, about the middle of the day—I missed them about four o'clock—the are them, they are marked, and are my master's.





THOMAS NEWMAN
. On the 24th of April I was walking near Mr. Muggeridge's shop, and saw the prisoner with these three pairs of boots under his arm—I went into the shop, and inquired if he had bought them—they said, "No"—I went after him, and fetched him back to the prosecutor's shop with the boots.





JOHN FENN
 (police-constable, R 320.) I produce a certificate of the prisoner's former conviction at this Court, by the name of John Brown—(read—"Convicted the 15th of Dec., 1845, and confined nine months ")—the prisoner is the person.




GUILTY
.* Aged 17.—


Transported for Seven Years.
